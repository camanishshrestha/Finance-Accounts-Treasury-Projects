## 🎯 Project Objective

Reinsurance and reserve management are the **financial backbone of any insurance company** — reinsurance protects the insurer from catastrophic losses by transferring excess risk to reinsurers, while reserves ensure that the company has **sufficient funds to honor future policyholder obligations**. Inadequate reinsurance or understated reserves can lead to **insolvency, regulatory action, and failure to pay claims**. As Team Leader/Team Member, I conducted focused internal audits of **reinsurance arrangements and reserve adequacy** at two life insurance companies — verifying the accuracy of risk cession, reinsurance accounting, and actuarial reserve provisions.

## 🛠️ Step-by-Step: What I Did

---

### ✅ Step 1: Understanding Reinsurance Framework

- Studied the company's **reinsurance framework** before commencing audit:

#### 📋 Reinsurance Basics

| Concept | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Retention** | The maximum risk amount the insurer retains on a single life — any excess is ceded to reinsurer |
| **Cession** | The portion of risk transferred to the reinsurer |
| **Reinsurance Premium** | Premium paid by insurer to reinsurer for accepting the ceded risk |
| **Reinsurance Recovery** | Amount recoverable from reinsurer when a claim occurs on a ceded policy |
| **Treaty Reinsurance** | Automatic reinsurance — all policies meeting treaty criteria are automatically ceded |
| **Facultative Reinsurance** | Case-by-case reinsurance — individual high-value policies referred to reinsurer for acceptance |

#### 📋 Types of Reinsurance Treaties

| Treaty Type | Mechanism |
|-------------|-----------|
| **Quota Share** | Fixed percentage of every policy is ceded (e.g., 30% ceded, 70% retained) |
| **Surplus Treaty** | Amount exceeding retention limit is ceded — insurer retains up to retention, surplus goes to reinsurer |
| **Excess of Loss** | Reinsurer covers losses exceeding a specified threshold |

- Reviewed the company's **reinsurance treaties and agreements** with reinsurers
- Understood **retention limits** approved by the Board and Insurance Board

---

### ✅ Step 2: Audit Planning — Reinsurance & Reserves Focus

- Developed **risk-based audit plan** for reinsurance and reserves:

#### 📊 Key Risk Areas

| Area | Key Risks |
|------|-----------|
| **Reinsurance Cession** | Incorrect cession computation, policies not ceded when they should be, cession on lapsed policies |
| **Reinsurance Premium** | Incorrect reinsurance premium computation, untimely payment to reinsurer |
| **Reinsurance Recovery** | Under-recovery from reinsurer on claims, delayed recovery, unreconciled balances |
| **Reinsurance Accounting** | Incorrect accounting entries, misclassification, incomplete disclosure |
| **Reserve Adequacy** | Understated mathematical reserves, inadequate IBNR provision, incorrect actuarial assumptions |

- Selected **audit sample** covering:
  - High sum assured policies (above retention — must be ceded)
  - Policies near retention threshold (boundary cases)
  - Recent claims on reinsured policies
  - Random sample across product lines

---

### ✅ Step 3: Reinsurance Cession Accuracy Audit

- Verified the **accuracy of reinsurance cessions** for sampled policies:

#### 📋 Cession Verification Checklist

| Check | Verification Performed |
|-------|----------------------|
| **Retention Limit** | Verified Board-approved retention limit for individual lives |
| **Sum at Risk** | Computed sum at risk = Sum Assured – Mathematical Reserve (net amount the company would pay on death) |
| **Cession Trigger** | Verified that policies with sum at risk exceeding retention were ceded |
| **Cession Amount** | Verified cession amount = Sum at Risk – Retention |
| **Treaty Application** | Verified correct treaty applied — surplus treaty, quota share, or facultative |
| **Cession Timing** | Verified cession was done at policy inception (or within treaty-prescribed timeline) |
| **Cession on Renewals** | Verified cession continued on policy renewals and premium payments |
| **Non-Cession Identification** | Identified policies that **should have been ceded but were NOT** — potential retention limit breach |
| **Over-Cession Identification** | Identified policies where **more than necessary was ceded** — unnecessary reinsurance premium cost |
| **Lapsed Policy Cession** | Verified that cession was terminated when policies lapsed or surrendered |

- Reconciled **cession register** with:
  - Policy administration system records
  - Reinsurance treaty terms
  - Reinsurer's cession confirmation (bordereaux)

---

### ✅ Step 4: Reinsurance Premium Audit

- Verified **reinsurance premium computations and payments**:

#### 🔹 Premium Computation Verification
- For each sampled ceded policy:
  - Verified **reinsurance premium rate** — as per treaty schedule (based on age, sum at risk, policy term)
  - Verified **reinsurance premium amount**:
    ```
    Reinsurance Premium = Ceded Sum at Risk × Reinsurance Premium Rate
    ```
  - Checked for **experience rating adjustments** — profit commission, no-claim bonus from reinsurer

#### 🔹 Premium Payment Verification
- Verified **reinsurance premium payment**:
  - Reinsurance premium statements (bordereaux) submitted to reinsurer
  - Payment timelines — within treaty-prescribed due dates
  - Payment amounts — matching bordereaux totals
  - Foreign exchange treatment — reinsurance premiums often denominated in USD

#### 🔹 Payable Account Reconciliation
- Reconciled **reinsurance premium payable account**:
  Opening Balance + Premium Due – Premium Paid = Closing Balance

- Verified closing balance reasonableness

---

### ✅ Step 5: Reinsurance Recovery Audit (Claims)

- Verified **reinsurance recoveries on claims** for ceded policies:

#### 📋 Recovery Verification Checklist

| Check | Verification Performed |
|-------|----------------------|
| **Policy Ceded** | Confirmed the policy was indeed ceded to reinsurer at time of claim |
| **Cession Amount** | Verified ceded sum at risk at claim date |
| **Recovery Amount** | Recovery = Ceded proportion of claim amount — verified computation |
| **Recovery Claim Submitted** | Verified claim was intimated to reinsurer with required documentation |
| **Recovery Received** | Verified reinsurer paid the recovery — amount and timing |
| **Recovery Accounting** | Recovery correctly recorded — reducing gross claim cost |

#### 🔹 Outstanding Recovery Analysis
- Identified **outstanding reinsurance recoveries** — claims where recovery was due but not yet received
- Analyzed **ageing of outstanding recoveries** — identifying delayed recoveries
- Verified **reinsurance receivable balance** in financial statements

---

### ✅ Step 6: Reinsurance Accounting Verification

- Verified **reinsurance accounting entries** in the General Ledger:

#### 📋 Standard Reinsurance Journal Entries

| Transaction | Debit | Credit |
|-------------|-------|--------|
| **Reinsurance Premium Ceded** | Reinsurance Premium Expense | Reinsurance Premium Payable |
| **Reinsurance Premium Payment** | Reinsurance Premium Payable | Bank/Cash |
| **Reinsurance Recovery on Claim** | Reinsurance Receivable | Claims Expense (Reinsurer's Share) |
| **Receipt of Recovery** | Bank/Cash | Reinsurance Receivable |
| **Reinsurance Commission (Ceding Commission)** | Reinsurance Commission Receivable | Reinsurance Commission Income |

#### 🔹 Accounting Verification Checks
- Verified:
- Correct **classification** of reinsurance transactions in financial statements
- Proper **gross vs. net presentation** — gross claims and reinsurer's share presented separately (not netted)
- Adequate **disclosure** of reinsurance arrangements in notes to financial statements
- **Cut-off accuracy** — reinsurance transactions recorded in correct period

---

### ✅ Step 7: Reinsurer Reconciliation

- Performed **reconciliation between company records and reinsurer records**:

#### 🔹 Reconciliation Process
- Obtained **reinsurer's account statement** (periodic statement showing premiums due, claims recoverable, balances)
- Reconciled against company's internal records:

- Company Records:
Reinsurance Premium Payable : NPR XX,XXX
Reinsurance Recovery Receivable : NPR XX,XXX
Net Balance Payable/(Receivable) : NPR XX,XXX
Reinsurer's Statement:
Premium Due from Company : NPR XX,XXX
Claims Payable to Company : NPR XX,XXX
Net Balance Due from/(to) Company : NPR XX,XXX
─────────────────────────────────────
Difference : NPR XX,XXX


#### 🔹 Difference Investigation
- Investigated **reconciliation differences**:
  - Timing differences — transactions recorded in different periods
  - Premium computation differences
  - Foreign exchange rate differences
  - Disputed items
- Ensured reconciliation differences were **resolved or properly documented**

### ✅ Step 8: Actuarial Reserve Adequacy Review

- Reviewed the **adequacy of actuarial reserves** (mathematical reserves / life fund):

#### 📋 Types of Reserves Reviewed

| Reserve Type | Description |
|--------------|-------------|
| **Mathematical Reserve (Life Fund)** | Present value of future benefits – Present value of future premiums — computed policy-by-policy using actuarial methods |
| **IBNR Reserve** | Incurred But Not Reported — provision for claims that have occurred but not yet intimated |
| **Unearned Premium Reserve (UPR)** | Portion of premium relating to unexpired risk period |
| **Claims Outstanding Reserve** | Provision for intimated but not yet settled claims |
| **Unexpired Risk Reserve** | Additional reserve if UPR is inadequate to cover future claims |

#### 📋 Reserve Review Activities

| Activity | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Actuarial Report Review** | Reviewed the appointed actuary's valuation report — methodology, assumptions, results |
| **Assumptions Reasonableness** | Assessed reasonableness of key actuarial assumptions: mortality tables, discount rate, expense assumptions, lapse rates, bonus rates |
| **Data Integrity** | Verified that policy data fed into actuarial model was complete and accurate — reconciled policy count and sum assured with admin system |
| **Reserve Movement Analysis** | Analyzed reserve movement: Opening reserve + Premium received + Interest credited – Claims paid – Surrenders – Expenses = Closing reserve |
| **Adequacy Assessment** | Assessed whether reserves were adequate to meet all future policyholder obligations |
| **Solvency Ratio** | Verified solvency ratio computation: Available solvency margin vs. Required solvency margin — ensuring company met Insurance Board minimum solvency requirement |

---

### ✅ Step 9: Premium Accounting Audit

- Verified **premium accounting** as a supporting audit area:

#### 🔹 First Premium (New Business)
- Verified premium received before policy issuance
- Checked premium credited to correct product/fund account
- Verified premium amount matches proposal form and underwriting approval

#### 🔹 Renewal Premium
- Verified timely recording of renewal premiums
- Checked grace period compliance for late payments
- Verified policy lapse processing for non-payment beyond grace period

#### 🔹 Premium Register Reconciliation
- Reconciled premium register totals with:
  - Bank collections
  - Agent collection reports
  - General Ledger premium income account

#### 🔹 Agent Commission Audit
- Verified commission computation — correct rate applied based on product and policy year
- Verified commission payment authorization
- Checked for **churning indicators** — agents encouraging policyholders to surrender and buy new policies for fresh commission

---

### ✅ Step 10: Audit Report & Recommendations

- Prepared focused **internal audit report** on reinsurance and reserves:

#### 📋 Report Structure

| Section | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Executive Summary** | Overall assessment of reinsurance and reserve management |
| **Key Findings** | Classified by risk rating (Critical/High/Medium/Low) with supporting evidence |
| **Root Cause Analysis** | Underlying causes for each identified control weakness |
| **Recommendations** | Actionable, prioritized improvement suggestions |
| **Management Response** | Management's action plan and timeline for each finding |

#### 🔹 Sample Findings by Risk Rating

| Rating | Example Finding |
|--------|----------------|
| 🔴 **Critical** | Policies above retention not ceded; outstanding reinsurance recoveries aged beyond 180 days |
| 🟡 **High** | Reinsurer reconciliation differences unresolved; actuarial assumption documentation gaps |
| 🟢 **Medium** | Cession timing delays; reinsurance premium payment beyond due dates |
| ⚪ **Low** | Minor documentation improvements; MIS report enhancements |

#### 🔹 Key Recommendations
- Implementing automated cession trigger in policy admin system
- Monthly reinsurer reconciliation with escalation for aged differences
- Board review of retention limits annually
- Independent review of actuarial assumptions
- Strengthening IBNR estimation methodology
- Enhancing reinsurance MIS reporting

- Presented findings to **Senior Management and Audit Committee**
