## 🎯 Project Objective

While financial statements tell **what happened**, Management Information System (MIS) reports explain **why it happened and what should be done about it**. MIS is the **decision-making engine** of the bank — transforming raw financial data into **actionable intelligence** that enables Senior Management and the Board to make informed strategic, operational, and tactical decisions. As the person responsible for MIS reporting, I designed and delivered a comprehensive suite of **profitability analysis, cost control, and performance monitoring reports** that directly influenced management decisions on pricing, branch strategy, product focus, and cost optimization.

### ✅ Step 2: Branch-Wise Profitability Analysis

> This was one of the **most strategically important MIS reports** — determining which branches were contributing to the bank's profitability and which were not

#### 📊 Methodology: Branch Profitability Model
- Designed a **full-cost allocation branch profitability model**:

##### Revenue Attribution
- Attributed **revenue to each branch** based on:
  - **Interest income from loans** — loans originated and managed by the branch
  - **Interest income from deposits deployed** — branch deposits deployed as loans or investments (transfer pricing)
  - **Fee and commission income** — fees generated by the branch (LC, BG, remittance, processing fees)
  - **Other income** — locker fees, service charges, recovery from written-off loans

##### Cost Attribution
- Attributed **costs to each branch** using:
  - **Direct costs** — costs directly incurred by the branch:
    - Branch staff salaries and benefits
    - Branch rent and utilities
    - Branch-level depreciation (furniture, equipment, vehicles)
    - Branch travel and communication expenses
    - Branch cash handling and security costs
  - **Indirect costs (allocated)** — Head Office costs allocated to branches:
    - Head Office staff costs — allocated based on proportional usage or branch count
    - IT costs — allocated based on transaction volume or user count
    - Marketing costs — allocated based on branch revenue proportion or equal distribution
    - Compliance and audit costs — allocated based on branch risk rating or branch count
    - Central administration costs — allocated using a standard allocation key

##### Transfer Pricing
- Implemented **Funds Transfer Pricing (FTP)** mechanism:
  - Branches that were **net depositors** (collected more deposits than they disbursed as loans) earned a **transfer pricing credit** — recognizing that their deposits funded other branches' lending
  - Branches that were **net borrowers** (disbursed more loans than they collected in deposits) were charged a **transfer pricing debit** — recognizing they used funds from other branches
  - Transfer pricing rate was set based on **internal benchmark rate** (typically linked to weighted average cost of funds or interbank rate)
  - This ensured **fair profitability measurement** — preventing deposit-heavy branches from appearing unprofitable and loan-heavy branches from appearing disproportionately profitable

##### Branch Profitability Statement
- Prepared a **Branch Profitability Statement** for each branch:


- Prepared **consolidated branch profitability ranking report** — ranking all branches from most profitable to least profitable
- Identified **loss-making branches** and analyzed root causes:
  - New branches in ramp-up phase (expected)
  - High operating cost branches
  - Low business volume branches
  - High NPL branches
  - Unfavorable location branches
- Presented branch profitability analysis to **CEO, ALCO, and Board** with recommendations:
  - Branch expansion/consolidation decisions
  - Staffing optimization (overstaffed vs. understaffed branches)
  - Business target reallocation
  - Cost reduction initiatives for high-cost branches

### ✅ Step 3: Product-Wise Profitability Analysis

- Analyzed profitability at the **product level** — determining which banking products were most and least profitable:

#### 📊 Loan Product Profitability

| Product | Key Metrics Analyzed |
|---------|---------------------|
| Term Loans | Yield, volume, NPL ratio, provisioning cost, net margin |
| Overdraft / Working Capital | Utilization rate, yield, NPL ratio, revolving cost |
| Housing / Home Loans | Yield, volume, average ticket size, NPL ratio, collateral quality |
| Vehicle Loans | Yield, volume, NPL ratio, insurance adequacy |
| SME / Business Loans | Yield, volume, NPL ratio, sector concentration |
| Agriculture Loans | Yield, volume, NPL ratio, subsidy impact, deprived sector compliance |
| Personal / Consumer Loans | Yield, volume, NPL ratio, unsecured risk premium |
| Staff Loans | Concessional rate impact, IFRS 9 day-1 loss, employee benefit cost |

- For each loan product:
  - Computed **net yield after provisions** = Gross yield – (Provision expense / Average portfolio)
  - Computed **risk-adjusted return** — accounting for credit risk cost
  - Identified **high-yield but high-risk products** vs. **low-yield but stable products**
  - Recommended **portfolio rebalancing** strategies to optimize risk-return profile

#### 📊 Deposit Product Profitability

| Product | Key Metrics Analyzed |
|---------|---------------------|
| Savings Accounts | Volume, cost, stability, customer count, CASA contribution |
| Current Accounts | Volume, cost (zero/minimal), transaction volume, float income |
| Fixed Deposits (by tenure) | Volume, cost, maturity profile, renewal rate, concentration |
| Call Deposits | Volume, cost, volatility, institutional vs. retail |
| Margin Deposits | Volume, linked to LC/BG, cost, stability |

- Analyzed **CASA (Current Account Savings Account) ratio** — proportion of low-cost deposits
- Recommended strategies to **increase CASA ratio** — reducing overall cost of funds
- Identified **high-cost fixed deposit segments** and recommended rate rationalization

#### 📊 Fee-Based Product Profitability
- Analyzed profitability of fee-based services:
  - Remittance services — fee income vs. operational cost
  - Trade finance (LC/BG) — commission income vs. processing cost and risk
  - Card services (Debit/Credit/ATM) — interchange income vs. card issuance and maintenance cost
  - Digital banking — mobile/internet banking fee income vs. technology cost
- Identified **high-margin fee products** for growth focus
- Recommended **fee repricing** where fees were below cost recovery levels

### ✅ Step 4: Cost of Funds & Yield on Assets — Detailed Drill-Down

- Prepared detailed **cost of funds and yield analysis** going beyond the ALCO-level summary:

#### 📊 Cost of Funds Decomposition
- Decomposed cost of funds at multiple levels:
  - **By deposit product** — which products have the highest/lowest cost
  - **By branch** — which branches mobilize the cheapest/most expensive deposits
  - **By customer segment** — retail vs. corporate vs. institutional vs. government
  - **By maturity** — short-term vs. medium-term vs. long-term deposit cost
  - **By vintage** — older deposits (at historical rates) vs. new deposits (at current rates)
- Tracked cost of funds **trend over time** — monthly, quarterly, yearly
- Identified **drivers of cost increase** and recommended corrective actions

#### 📊 Yield on Assets Decomposition
- Decomposed yield on earning assets:
  - **By loan product** — yield on term loans, overdraft, housing, SME, agriculture, personal
  - **By branch** — branch-wise lending yield comparison
  - **By sector** — sector-wise lending yield
  - **By risk rating** — yield by borrower risk grade (higher risk should earn higher yield)
  - **By investment type** — T-Bill yield, Bond yield, interbank rate, equity dividend yield
- Identified **yield compression trends** and root causes — competitive pressure, NRB base rate constraints, portfolio mix shift

### ✅ Step 5: Key Performance Indicator (KPI) Dashboard

- Designed and maintained a comprehensive **KPI Dashboard** for Senior Management and Board:

#### 📊 Financial KPIs

| KPI | Formula | Target Benchmark |
|-----|---------|-----------------|
| Return on Assets (ROA) | Net Profit / Average Total Assets | Industry average |
| Return on Equity (ROE) | Net Profit / Average Shareholders' Equity | Industry average |
| Net Interest Margin (NIM) | Net Interest Income / Average Earning Assets | Peer comparison |
| Cost-to-Income Ratio | Operating Expenses / Operating Income | Below 50% (ideal) |
| EPS (Earnings Per Share) | Net Profit / Outstanding Shares | Growth trend |
| Book Value Per Share | Net Worth / Outstanding Shares | Growth trend |
| Dividend Payout Ratio | Dividend / Net Profit | Board policy |

#### 📊 Balance Sheet KPIs

| KPI | Formula | Target Benchmark |
|-----|---------|-----------------|
| Credit-to-Deposit (CD) Ratio | Total Loans / Total Deposits | NRB limit (≤80%) |
| CASA Ratio | (Current + Savings) / Total Deposits | Higher is better |
| Capital Adequacy Ratio (CAR) | Capital Fund / Risk-Weighted Assets | Above NRB minimum |
| NPL Ratio | Non-Performing Loans / Total Loans | Below 5% |
| Provision Coverage Ratio | Total Provisions / Total NPLs | Above 100% |

#### 📊 Productivity KPIs

| KPI | Formula | Purpose |
|-----|---------|---------|
| Revenue per Employee | Operating Income / Total Staff | Staff productivity |
| Revenue per Branch | Operating Income / Total Branches | Branch productivity |
| Loans per Credit Officer | Total Loans / Credit Officers | Credit team efficiency |
| Deposits per Branch | Total Deposits / Total Branches | Branch mobilization |
| Profit per Employee | Net Profit / Total Staff | Per capita value |

#### 📊 Liquidity KPIs

| KPI | Formula | Target |
|-----|---------|--------|
| CRR | NRB Balance / Eligible Deposits | Above NRB minimum |
| SLR | Liquid Assets / Deposit & Borrowings | Above NRB minimum |
| LCR | HQLA / Net Cash Outflows | Above NRB minimum |
| NSFR | Available Stable Funding / Required Stable Funding | Above NRB minimum |

- Designed KPI dashboard with **traffic light indicators**:
  - 🟢 Green — On target or above
  - 🟡 Yellow — Approaching threshold / needs attention
  - 🔴 Red — Below target / breach / immediate action required
- Updated KPI dashboard **monthly** and presented at Board and management meetings
- Tracked **KPI trends** over time — 12-month rolling trend charts for each KPI

### ✅ Step 8: Cost Control Monitoring & Savings Tracking

- Established a structured **cost control monitoring system**:

#### 📊 Cost Control Process
1. **Identification** — Identified cost reduction opportunities through:
   - ZBB decision package reviews (from Project 8)
   - Department expense analysis
   - Vendor contract reviews
   - Process efficiency assessments
   - Peer benchmarking (identifying areas where peer costs were lower)
2. **Quantification** — Estimated potential savings for each opportunity
3. **Recommendation** — Presented cost-saving proposals to management with:
   - Current cost vs. proposed cost
   - Implementation steps required
   - Risks and dependencies
   - Expected timeline for savings realization
4. **Approval** — Obtained management/Board approval for cost initiatives
5. **Implementation Tracking** — Monitored actual implementation progress
6. **Savings Measurement** — Measured **actual savings achieved** vs. estimated savings
7. **Reporting** — Reported cost savings dashboard to management quarterly

#### 📊 Cost Savings Areas Identified & Tracked

| Area | Initiative Examples |
|------|-------------------|
| **Vendor Renegotiation** | CBS maintenance contract, insurance premiums, security service contracts |
| **Process Automation** | Reducing manual reconciliation effort, automating report generation |
| **Print & Stationery** | Shift to digital communication, reduce physical printing |
| **Energy Costs** | LED lighting, efficient HVAC, energy audit recommendations |
| **Travel Costs** | Virtual meetings replacing physical travel where possible |
| **Staff Optimization** | Right-sizing based on branch profitability and workload analysis |
| **Procurement Optimization** | Bulk purchasing, competitive bidding for supplies |
