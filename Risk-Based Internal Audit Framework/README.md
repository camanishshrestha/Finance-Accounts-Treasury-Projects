## 🎯 Project Objective

This project focused on shifting internal audit from a **checklist-driven approach** to a **risk-based internal audit (RBIA) framework**.

The objective was to ensure audit efforts were directed toward:
- Highest risk areas  
- Critical controls  
- Meaningful business exposure  
- Regulatory-sensitive processes  

> ⚠️ Confidentiality Note: Institution-specific risk data and findings are excluded.

---

## 🛠️ Step-by-Step Work Performed

---

### ✅ Step 1: Reviewed Existing Audit Approach

- Analyzed traditional audit methodology
- Identified key gaps:
  - Equal audit coverage regardless of risk  
  - Checklist-based execution  
  - Weak risk linkage in planning  
  - Limited strategic prioritization  

Defined need for a **risk-based transformation**

---

### ✅ Step 2: Supported Audit Universe Mapping

- Identified full audit universe:
  - branches  
  - credit operations  
  - treasury  
  - finance  
  - HR & payroll  
  - procurement  
  - compliance  
  - IT systems  

- Structured auditable areas for risk classification

---

### ✅ Step 3: Assisted in Risk Identification

- Mapped risks to processes:
  - operational risk  
  - credit risk  
  - compliance risk  
  - fraud risk  
  - IT/control risk  
  - financial reporting risk  

- Ensured risks reflected real operational exposure

---

### ✅ Step 4: Supported Risk Scoring Model Development

Helped define risk prioritization factors:

- Financial impact  
- Regulatory exposure  
- Transaction volume  
- Control weakness history  
- Management concern  
- Time since last audit  
- Process complexity  

- Classified areas into:
  - High risk  
  - Medium risk  
  - Low risk  

---

### ✅ Step 5: Assisted in Risk & Control Matrices (RCM)

- Helped develop RCM structure:
  - process  
  - risk  
  - control  
  - audit objective  
  - testing approach  

- Strengthened audit planning foundation

---

### ✅ Step 6: Supported Risk-Based Audit Planning

- Helped align audit plan with risk ranking
- Ensured:
  - high-risk areas received priority coverage  
  - low-risk areas followed rotational approach  
  - audit timing reflected risk exposure  

---

### ✅ Step 7: Shifted Audit Programs to Risk Focus

- Reduced checklist-heavy procedures
- Increased focus on:
  - key controls  
  - exception patterns  
  - high-risk transactions  
  - control effectiveness  

- Improved audit efficiency and relevance

---

### ✅ Step 8: Assisted Documentation Standardization

- Structured RBIA documentation templates:
  - risk assessment sheets  
  - planning formats  
  - RCM-based working structure  

- Improved consistency and repeatability

---

### ✅ Step 9: Supported Team Adoption of RBIA Thinking

- Helped audit teams transition mindset:
  - from “what to check” → “what is the risk”  
  - from routine work → risk prioritization  
- Reinforced analytical audit approach

---

### ✅ Step 10: Supported First-Time RBIA Implementation

- Contributed to initial rollout of RBIA framework
- Helped establish:
  - structured audit prioritization  
  - risk-driven planning  
  - improved resource allocation  
  - more meaningful audit reporting
