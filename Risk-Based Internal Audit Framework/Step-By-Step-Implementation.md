## 🎯 Project Objective

Traditional audit approaches often focus on **routine checking** rather than directing audit effort toward the areas of **highest actual risk**. In this engagement, I contributed to the **first-time implementation of a Risk-Based Internal Audit (RBIA) framework** for a commercial bank. The goal was to help shift internal audit from a checklist-driven approach to a **risk-prioritized assurance model**.

> ⚠️ *Note: To maintain confidentiality, institution-specific risk scores, audit plans, internal control weaknesses, and strategic decisions are not disclosed here. This project describes the framework design and my role in implementation.*

## 🛠️ Step-by-Step: What I Did

---

### ✅ Step 1: Understood the Existing Audit Approach

- Reviewed how internal audit was being performed before RBIA implementation
- Identified gaps such as:

#### 📋 Pre-RBIA Audit Approach Gaps

| Gap | Impact |
|-----|--------|
| **Uniform audit frequency** | High-risk and low-risk areas audited with same intensity — inefficient resource use |
| **Limited risk prioritization** | Audit effort not aligned with actual business risk exposure |
| **Checklist-heavy approach** | Focus on procedural compliance rather than substantive risk assessment |
| **Weak enterprise risk linkage** | Audit plan not connected to organization's strategic or operational risk profile |

- Helped define why a **risk-based approach** was needed:
  - Better alignment with business objectives
  - More efficient use of limited audit resources
  - Enhanced value to management through risk-focused insights
  - Compliance with IIA Standards requiring risk-based audit planning

---

### ✅ Step 2: Supported Audit Universe Mapping

- Helped identify the full **audit universe** of the institution:

#### 📋 Audit Universe Structure

| Category | Auditable Units |
|----------|----------------|
| **Branches** | All branch locations — categorized by size, location risk, transaction volume |
| **Credit Operations** | Loan origination, appraisal, disbursement, monitoring, recovery, NPL management |
| **Treasury** | Investment portfolio, liquidity management, FX operations, money market transactions |
| **Finance & Reporting** | Financial statement preparation, regulatory reporting, tax compliance, GL management |
| **HR & Payroll** | Recruitment, compensation, benefits, performance management, separation |
| **Procurement** | Vendor selection, purchase approval, contract management, payment processing |
| **Compliance** | Regulatory adherence, policy implementation, AML/CFT, internal policy compliance |
| **IT & Systems** | Access controls, change management, data security, CBS/ERP controls, backup/DR |

- Grouped auditable units into a structure that could later be risk-ranked
- Ensured audit universe was **comprehensive yet manageable** — covering all material activities

### ✅ Step 3: Assisted in Risk Identification

- Helped identify major risk categories relevant to each auditable area:

#### 📋 Risk Categories by Audit Area

| Audit Area | Key Risk Categories |
|------------|-------------------|
| **Branches** | Cash handling risk, KYC/AML compliance, operational fraud, documentation gaps |
| **Credit Operations** | Credit appraisal weakness, collateral shortfall, classification/provisioning errors, evergreening |
| **Treasury** | Market risk, liquidity risk, counterparty risk, unauthorized trading, valuation errors |
| **Finance & Reporting** | Financial misstatement, regulatory non-compliance, tax exposure, disclosure gaps |
| **HR & Payroll** | Ghost employees, payroll fraud, benefit miscalculation, labor law non-compliance |
| **Procurement** | Vendor favoritism, split procurement, inadequate competition, contract leakage |
| **Compliance** | Regulatory penalty, reputational damage, policy violation, licensing issues |
| **IT & Systems** | Unauthorized access, data breach, system failure, change control weakness, cyber risk |

- Linked processes to the risks they were exposed to
- Ensured that risk identification reflected **actual business reality**, not just policy wording
- Conducted interviews with process owners to validate risk assessments

### ✅ Step 4: Contributed to Risk Scoring Methodology

- Supported development of a **risk scoring model** for audit prioritization:

#### 📋 Risk Scoring Factors & Weights

| Risk Factor | Weight | Scoring Scale (1-5) |
|-------------|--------|-------------------|
| **Financial Impact** | 25% | 1 (Low) to 5 (Very High) — potential loss magnitude |
| **Regulatory Exposure** | 20% | 1 (Low) to 5 (Very High) — penalty, license risk |
| **Transaction Volume** | 15% | 1 (Low) to 5 (Very High) — frequency/complexity |
| **Control Weakness History** | 15% | 1 (Strong) to 5 (Weak) — prior audit findings |
| **Management Concern** | 10% | 1 (Low) to 5 (High) — leadership priority areas |
| **Time Since Last Audit** | 10% | 1 (Recent) to 5 (Never) — audit coverage gap |
| **Operational Complexity** | 5% | 1 (Simple) to 5 (Very Complex) — process difficulty |

- Composite Risk Score = Σ (Factor Score × Weight)


#### 🔹 Risk Ranking Categories

| Score Range | Risk Rating | Audit Frequency |
|-------------|-------------|----------------|
| 4.0 – 5.0 | 🔴 High | Annual audit |
| 2.5 – 3.9 | 🟡 Medium | Audit every 2 years |
| 1.0 – 2.4 | 🟢 Low | Audit every 3 years or rotational |

- Helped organize auditable units into ranked categories such as high, medium, and low risk
- Validated scoring methodology with management and audit committee

### ✅ Step 5: Helped Build Risk and Control Matrices (RCM)

- Contributed to preparing **Risk and Control Matrices** for key audit areas:

#### 📋 RCM Structure Template

| Process Step | Key Risk | Expected Control | Control Owner | Audit Objective | Testing Approach |
|--------------|----------|-----------------|--------------|----------------|-----------------|
| [e.g., Loan Disbursement] | [e.g., Funds disbursed without meeting conditions] | [e.g., Pre-disbursement checklist signed by authorized officer] | [e.g., Credit Manager] | [Verify conditions precedent met before disbursement] | [Inspect disbursement file for checklist completion and approval] |

#### 🔹 RCM Development Process
- For selected areas, mapped:
  - **Process** — key steps in the business activity
  - **Key Risk** — what could go wrong at each step
  - **Expected Control** — what control should prevent/detect the risk
  - **Audit Objective** — what the audit seeks to verify
  - **Testing Direction** — how the audit will test the control

- This made audit planning more structured and aligned with key control expectations
- Enabled auditors to focus testing on controls that matter most

---

### ✅ Step 6: Supported Risk-Based Audit Planning

- Helped translate risk assessment results into a more focused audit plan:

#### 📋 Risk-Based Planning Principles Applied

| Principle | Implementation |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Risk Prioritization** | Higher-risk areas received more audit attention (frequency, depth, resources) |
| **Resource Alignment** | Audit team skills and time allocated based on risk ranking |
| **Flexible Timing** | Audit timing reflected business risk cycles, not only calendar routine |
| **Dynamic Adjustment** | Plan allowed for ad-hoc audits if emerging risks identified |

#### 🔹 Planning Outcomes
- Ensured that:
  - Higher-risk areas received more audit attention
  - Lower-risk areas followed a lighter or rotational coverage
  - Audit timing reflected business risk, not only calendar routine
- Contributed to aligning audit resources with priority risk areas
- Prepared draft annual audit plan for management and audit committee review

---

### ✅ Step 7: Helped Shift Audit Programs from Checklist to Risk Focus

- Reviewed audit programs and helped make them more risk-driven:

#### 📋 Audit Program Transformation

| Before (Checklist-Driven) | After (Risk-Based) |
|---------------------------|------------------|
| "Verify 10 loan files for documentation" | "Test controls over loan appraisal for high-value exposures" |
| "Check if reconciliations are signed" | "Assess whether reconciliation process effectively detects and resolves exceptions" |
| "Confirm KYC documents are present" | "Evaluate KYC process effectiveness in identifying and mitigating customer risk" |
| "Review 5 cash counts" | "Test cash control environment for branches with high transaction volume or prior findings" |

#### 🔹 Enhanced Audit Focus Areas
- Reduced unnecessary routine procedures where they added little value
- Increased focus on:
  - **Key control points** — controls that prevent or detect material errors/fraud
  - **High-risk transactions** — large, unusual, or complex transactions
  - **Exception patterns** — recurring overrides, manual adjustments, system bypasses
  - **Process weaknesses** — design or operating effectiveness gaps in critical controls
- Supported a more analytical and purpose-driven audit style

#### 🔹 Key Mindset Shifts Supported
