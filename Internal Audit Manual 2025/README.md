## 🎯 Project Objective

Upon joining the organization as **Head of Internal Audit & Compliance**, the group of **9 companies** lacked a **formal Internal Audit Manual**.

This resulted in:
- Inconsistent audit execution  
- No standardized methodology  
- Lack of documentation and guidance  

To address this, I **designed and authored the Internal Audit Manual 2025 from scratch**, establishing a **central governing framework** for all internal audit activities across the group.

> ⚠️ **Confidentiality Note:**  
> All company-specific procedures, risk data, and proprietary details are excluded. Only framework and methodology are presented.

## 🛠️ What I Did (Step-by-Step)

---

### ✅ Step 1: Need Assessment & Gap Analysis

**Key Findings:**
- No internal audit manual
- No standardized audit methodology
- No templates or documentation structure
- No QAIP (Quality Assurance Program)
- No risk-based planning framework
- Inconsistent reporting formats
- No follow-up mechanism

**Conclusion:**  
A **group-wide standardized manual** was critical.

---

---

### ✅ Step 3: Internal Audit Charter

- Defined authority, independence, and scope
- Functional reporting to Audit Committee
- Full access to records, personnel, and assets
- Alignment with IIA (IPPF)

**Why “Charter”?**  
It formally authorizes the audit function—like a constitution.

---

### ✅ Step 4: Risk Assessment & Audit Universe

**Audit Universe Includes:**
- Financial  
- Operational  
- Compliance  
- IT  
- Governance  
- HR  

**Risk Scoring Model:**
- Financial Impact (25%)
- Regulatory Risk (20%)
- Control Environment (20%)
- Complexity (15%)
- Time Since Audit (10%)
- Management Concern (10%)

**Composite Score = Risk × Weight**

**Why “Audit Universe”?**  
Represents all auditable areas—complete coverage of risks.

---

### ✅ Step 5: Annual Audit Planning

**Process:**
1. Update audit universe  
2. Perform risk assessment  
3. Assess resources  
4. Rank risks  
5. Allocate audits  

**Frequency:**
- 🔴 High → Annual  
- 🟡 Medium → Every 2 years  
- 🟢 Low → Every 3 years  

---

### ✅ Step 6: Engagement Planning

- Risk & Control Matrix (RCM)
- Defined objectives and scope
- Audit program and timeline
- Resource allocation
- Entrance meeting

**Why “RCM”?**  
Links risks → controls → testing.

---

### ✅ Step 7: Fieldwork & Execution

**Methods:**
- Inquiry  
- Observation  
- Inspection  
- Reperformance  
- Analytical Review  
- Confirmation  
- Data Analytics  

**Evidence must be:**
- Sufficient  
- Reliable  
- Relevant  
- Useful  

---

### ✅ Step 8: Audit Reporting

**Structure:**
- Executive Summary  
- Scope & Methodology  
- Findings  
- Dashboard  

**Finding Format:**
```
Criteria → Condition → Cause → Consequence → Recommendation
```

**Why this format?**  
Ensures root cause analysis, not just surface issues.

---

### ✅ Step 9: Follow-Up & Monitoring

- Central Finding Tracker
- Status tracking:
  - ✅ Closed  
  - 🔄 In Progress  
  - ❌ Overdue  

**Escalation:**
- 30 days → Department Head  
- 60 days → CEO  
- 90 days → Audit Committee  

---

### ✅ Step 10: QAIP (Quality Assurance)

- Ongoing reviews
- Annual self-assessment
- External review every 5 years

**Why QAIP?**  
Ensures continuous improvement of audit quality.

---

### ✅ Step 11: Special Audits

- Fraud investigations  
- Ad-hoc audits  
- Regulatory audits  
- IT audits  
- Compliance audits  

---

### ✅ Step 12: Templates & Checklists

Created 12+ templates:
- Audit Program  
- RCM  
- Working Papers  
- Audit Report  
- Finding Tracker  
- QA Checklist
