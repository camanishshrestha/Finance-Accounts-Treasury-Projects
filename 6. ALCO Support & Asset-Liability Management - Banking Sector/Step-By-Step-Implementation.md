## 🎯 Project Objective

The **Asset-Liability Committee (ALCO)** is the most critical **risk governance body** in any bank — it is where the **strategic decisions about the balance sheet are made**. ALCO determines how the bank manages the delicate balance between **earning sufficient returns (assets) while managing funding costs and risks (liabilities)** — all while maintaining regulatory compliance. As the **ALCO support function**, I was responsible for preparing the entire **ALCO information pack, drafting ALCO minutes, conducting risk analysis**, and ensuring that ALCO decisions were **documented, communicated, and implemented** across the bank.

> ⚠️ *Note: All institution-specific ALCO decisions, strategies, risk positions, and internal deliberations remain strictly confidential. Only general methodologies, frameworks, and task descriptions are described here.*

### ✅ Step 1: Understanding ALCO Structure & Governance Framework

- Studied and understood the **ALCO governance framework** within the bank:

#### 🔹 ALCO Composition:

- Members included heads of **Finance, Treasury, Credit, Risk Management, Operations**, and other senior management

#### 🔹 ALCO Mandate:

- Setting **deposit and lending interest rates**
- Approving **investment strategies and limits**
- Reviewing **regulatory compliance** — CRR, SLR, LCR, NSFR, CAR

#### 🔹 ALCO Meeting Frequency:
- Monthly (regular) and ad-hoc meetings during market stress or regulatory changes

### ✅ Step 4: Interest Rate Risk Analysis

- Prepared comprehensive **interest rate risk analysis** for ALCO decision-making:

#### 📊 Repricing Gap Analysis
- Classified all **Rate-Sensitive Assets (RSA)** and **Rate-Sensitive Liabilities (RSL)** by repricing maturity buckets:
  - Up to 1 month
  - 1–3 months
  - 3–6 months
  - 6–12 months
  - 1–2 years
  - 2–5 years
  - Over 5 years
  - Non-rate-sensitive
- Computed **Gap = RSA – RSL** for each maturity bucket
- Computed **Cumulative Gap** across all buckets
- Identified **positive gap** (asset-sensitive — benefits from rising rates) and **negative gap** (liability-sensitive — benefits from falling rates) positions
- Presented gap analysis with **visual charts** for easy ALCO interpretation

#### 📊 Net Interest Income (NII) Sensitivity Analysis
- Computed the impact on **Net Interest Income (NII)** under various interest rate shock scenarios:
  - Parallel shift: +100 bps, +200 bps, -100 bps, -200 bps
  - Asymmetric shifts: lending rates change more/less than deposit rates
 
####   ΔEVE = -(Duration of Assets × Market Value of Assets × Δr) + (Duration of Liabilities × Market Value of Liabilities × Δr)
- Presented EVE-at-Risk under stress scenarios for ALCO review
- EVE analysis provided a **long-term perspective** complementing the short-term NII analysis

---

### ✅ Step 5: Liquidity Risk Analysis

- Prepared comprehensive **liquidity risk analysis** for ALCO:

#### 📊 Liquidity Gap Analysis (Structural Liquidity)
- Classified all assets and liabilities by **residual maturity** (contractual maturity) into time buckets:
  - On demand / Up to 1 month
  - 1–3 months
  - 3–6 months
  - 6–12 months
  - 1–3 years
  - 3–5 years
  - Over 5 years
- Computed **liquidity gap = maturing assets – maturing liabilities** for each bucket
- Computed **cumulative liquidity gap** — identifying structural funding mismatches
- Identified **negative cumulative gaps** in near-term buckets as potential liquidity risk indicators
- Prepared **behavioral maturity adjustments** for:
  - Demand deposits — applying behavioral stability factors (core vs. volatile deposits)
  - Overdraft facilities — applying drawdown assumptions
  - Non-maturity deposits — applying assumed behavioral tenure

#### 📊 Regulatory Liquidity Ratios
- Computed and presented:
  - **CRR Position** — current vs. required, surplus/shortfall
  - **SLR Position** — current vs. required, surplus/shortfall
  - **LCR (Liquidity Coverage Ratio):**
    - HQLA (High-Quality Liquid Assets) — Level 1 (cash, NRB balance, government securities) and Level 2 assets
    - Net Cash Outflows — 30-day stress scenario
    - LCR = HQLA / Net Cash Outflows ≥ NRB minimum
  - **NSFR (Net Stable Funding Ratio):**
    - Available Stable Funding (ASF)
    - Required Stable Funding (RSF)
    - NSFR = ASF / RSF ≥ NRB minimum
- Tracked **trend analysis** of liquidity ratios over time — identifying improving or deteriorating trends

#### 📊 Deposit Concentration Analysis
- Analyzed **top depositor concentration** — percentage of total deposits held by:
  - Top 10 depositors
  - Top 20 depositors
  - Top 50 depositors
- Identified **concentration risk** — over-reliance on a few large depositors
- Analyzed **deposit type mix** — proportion of low-cost deposits (CASA) vs. high-cost fixed deposits
- Recommended **deposit diversification strategies** to ALCO

---

### ✅ Step 6: Cost of Funds & Yield Analysis

- Prepared detailed **cost of funds and yield analysis** for ALCO rate-setting decisions:

#### 📊 Weighted Average Cost of Deposits (WACD)
- Computed WACD by:
  - Product type — savings, current, fixed deposit (by tenure), call deposit, margin deposit
  - Customer segment — retail, corporate, institutional, government
  - Branch — branch-wise cost of funds comparison
- Tracked **WACD trend** — month-on-month and quarter-on-quarter movements
- Identified **high-cost deposit buckets** and recommended rate reduction strategies

#### 📊 Weighted Average Cost of Funds (WACF)
- Computed total cost of funds including:
  - Deposit costs
  - Interbank borrowing costs
  - NRB facility costs (SLF/OLF)
  - Debenture/subordinated debt costs
 
#### WACF = Total Interest Expense / Average Interest-Bearing Liabilities

#### 📊 Weighted Average Yield on Assets (WAYA)
- Computed yield on:
  - Loan portfolio — by product, sector, tenure
  - Investment portfolio — government securities, equity, interbank

#### WAYA = Total Interest Income / Average Interest-Earning Assets

#### 📊 Net Interest Margin (NIM) & Spread Analysis
- Computed:

#### NIM = (Interest Income – Interest Expense) / Average Interest-Earning Assets
#### Interest Spread = WAYA – WACF

- Tracked NIM and spread trends
- Presented **peer comparison** — benchmarking NIM against industry and peer bank performance
- Recommended **rate adjustments** to ALCO to optimize NIM while remaining competitive

---

### ✅ Step 7: Funding Strategy & Capital Adequacy Review

#### 🔹 Funding Strategy Analysis:
- Assessed current **funding structure** — deposits vs. borrowings vs. equity
- Analyzed **funding stability** — proportion of stable funding (core deposits, long-term borrowings) vs. volatile funding (large time deposits, interbank, NRB facilities)
- Recommended **optimal funding mix** to balance cost, stability, and regulatory compliance
- Assessed **upcoming funding needs** — loan growth pipeline, investment commitments, maturing liabilities requiring refinancing

#### 🔹 Capital Adequacy Review:
- Current CAR position — Tier 1 and Total CAR
- Projected CAR based on balance sheet growth assumptions
- Capital buffer above NRB minimum
- **Capital planning recommendations** — retained earnings, dividend policy, Tier 2 issuance (debenture)
- Stress test results on capital adequacy under adverse scenarios

### ✅ Step 8: ALCO Minute Preparation

- Drafted the **official ALCO Minutes** after every ALCO meeting — one of the most sensitive and important documents in the bank:

#### 📋 ALCO Minutes Structure:
- **Meeting Details** — Date, time, venue, attendees, absentees with reasons
- **Review of Previous Minutes** — Status of action items from previous ALCO meeting
- **Agenda-wise Deliberation:**
  - For each agenda item:
    - **Issue / Analysis Presented** — summary of data and analysis discussed
    - **Discussion Points** — key views, opinions, and concerns raised by members
    - **Decision / Resolution** — specific ALCO decision with clear action items
    - **Responsibility** — person/department responsible for implementation
    - **Timeline** — deadline for implementation or next review
- **Key Decisions Summary** — consolidated list of all decisions taken
- **Next Meeting Date** — scheduled date for next ALCO meeting

- Ensured minutes were:
  - **Accurate** — faithfully reflecting discussions and decisions
  - **Complete** — covering all agenda items and decisions
  - **Clear** — written in precise, unambiguous language
  - **Timely** — drafted and circulated within prescribed timeframe post-meeting
  - **Approved** — signed by ALCO Chairperson and filed for record

### ✅ Step 9: ALCO Decision Implementation & Follow-Up

- After ALCO meeting and minute finalization, ensured **ALCO decisions were implemented**:

#### 🔹 Interest Rate Changes:
- If ALCO decided to change deposit or lending rates:
  - Prepared **rate change circulars** for all branches
  - Coordinated with **IT/CBS team** to update rates in Core Banking System
  - Communicated rate changes to **marketing and customer-facing teams**
  - Monitored impact of rate changes on deposit mobilization and loan disbursement

#### 🔹 Investment Decisions:
- If ALCO approved new investments or portfolio changes:
  - Ensured Treasury executed within approved limits and timelines
  - Tracked investment performance against ALCO expectations

#### 🔹 Liquidity Management Actions:
- If ALCO directed specific liquidity actions (e.g., reduce interbank borrowing, increase CASA mobilization):
  - Coordinated with relevant departments for implementation
  - Tracked progress and reported back to next ALCO meeting

#### 🔹 Risk Limit Changes:
- If ALCO revised any risk limits (counterparty, investment, FX):
  - Updated **limit registers** and communicated to affected functions
  - Ensured monitoring systems reflected updated limits

- Maintained an **ALCO Action Tracker** — a live document tracking:
  - Decision / Action Item
  - Responsible Person / Department
  - Deadline
  - Current Status (Pending / In Progress / Completed / Overdue)
  - Remarks
- Presented **ALCO action tracker status** at the beginning of every subsequent ALCO meeting
