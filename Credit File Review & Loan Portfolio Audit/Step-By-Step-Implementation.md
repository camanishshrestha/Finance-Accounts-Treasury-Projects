## 🎯 Project Objective

Credit is the **highest-risk asset class** in most banks and financial institutions. My role in this project was to review loan files and the broader credit portfolio to assess whether lending decisions were supported by proper appraisal, documentation, collateral coverage, classification, and provisioning. The objective was to identify control gaps early and help management strengthen credit governance.

> ⚠️ *Note: Client-specific borrower data, file contents, exposure details, and audit findings remain confidential. This project describes the work approach and responsibilities only.*

---

## 🛠️ Step-by-Step: What I Did

---

### ✅ Step 1: Selected Loan Files for Review

- Obtained the loan portfolio listing from the institution
- Selected files based on risk indicators such as:

#### 📋 Risk-Based Sampling Criteria

| Risk Indicator | Rationale for Selection |
|---------------|------------------------|
| **Large Exposures** | Higher financial impact if control failures exist |
| **Overdue Accounts** | Potential credit quality deterioration or monitoring gaps |
| **Recently Restructured Loans** | Higher risk of evergreening or inadequate restructuring analysis |
| **Renewed Facilities** | Risk of automatic renewals without proper review |
| **Sector Concentration** | Systemic risk if sector faces downturn |
| **Related/Connected Exposures** | Risk of related party lending or concentration |

- Ensured sample coverage across different loan products and branches for representative assessment

---

### ✅ Step 2: Reviewed Credit Appraisal Quality

- Examined whether each selected loan had proper **credit appraisal** before approval:

#### 📋 Credit Appraisal Assessment Checklist

| Appraisal Component | Verification Performed |
|--------------------|----------------------|
| **Borrower Background** | KYC, business history, management competence, reputation check |
| **Business/Income Assessment** | Revenue streams, profitability trends, market position, competitive landscape |
| **Financial Statement Review** | Audited/unaudited financials analyzed for ratios, trends, anomalies |
| **Repayment Capacity** | DSCR, cash flow adequacy, debt service coverage assessment |
| **Cash Flow Assessment** | Operating cash flow vs. debt obligations, seasonality considerations |
| **Purpose of Loan** | Clear end-use defined; aligned with borrower's business needs |
| **Credit Bureau / CIB Check** | Borrower's credit history, existing exposures, default record verified |

- Evaluated whether sanction decisions were based on **evidence rather than formality**
- Identified cases where appraisal was superficial, outdated, or lacked critical analysis

---

### ✅ Step 3: Verified Approval Hierarchy Compliance

- Checked whether each loan was approved by the **correct authority level**:

#### 🔹 Approval Documentation Review

| Document | Verification Performed |
|----------|----------------------|
| **Sanction Note** | Prepared by credit officer; includes key risk factors and mitigation |
| **Approval Memo** | Signed by authorized person per delegation of authority matrix |
| **Committee Approval** | Minutes of Credit Committee/Board with quorum and decision recorded |
| **Limit Compliance** | Exposure within single obligor limit, sector limit, and product limit |

- Identified any cases where:
  - Approval flow was incomplete or bypassed required levels
  - Approvals were given outside delegated limits without escalation
  - Committee decisions lacked proper documentation or rationale

---

### ✅ Step 4: Tested Documentation Completeness

- Reviewed loan files for key documents:

#### 📋 Loan File Documentation Checklist

| Document Category | Required Documents |
|------------------|-------------------|
| **Application & KYC** | Loan application form, citizenship/passport, PAN, photographs, address proof |
| **Legal Agreements** | Loan agreement, promissory note, guarantee deed, hypothecation deed |
| **Collateral Documents** | Land ownership certificate, valuation report, insurance policy, charge registration |
| **Financial Documents** | Financial statements, tax returns, bank statements, cash flow projections |
| **Approval Records** | Credit appraisal note, sanction letter, approval memo, committee minutes |
| **Post-Disbursement** | Disbursement confirmation, end-use verification, stock statements, monitoring reports |

- Checked whether file documentation was:
  - **Complete** — all required documents present
  - **Signed** — proper authorization on agreements and forms
  - **Valid** — documents not expired (insurance, valuation, licenses)
  - **Properly maintained** — organized, indexed, easily retrievable

---

### ✅ Step 5: Reviewed Collateral Adequacy and Perfection

- Verified whether collateral was properly managed:

#### 🔹 Collateral Verification Framework

| Check | Verification Performed |
|-------|----------------------|
| **Proper Valuation** | Valuation report from approved valuer; methodology appropriate; value reasonable |
| **Legal Documentation** | Title deed verified; ownership confirmed; encumbrances disclosed |
| **Registration** | Mortgage/charge registered with relevant authority (Land Revenue Office, Company Registrar) |
| **Insurance Coverage** | Insurance policy in bank's favor; sum insured adequate; premium paid; policy current |
| **Collateral Coverage Ratio** | Collateral value / Exposure ratio meets policy minimum (e.g., 1.5x or 2x) |

#### 🔹 Common Collateral Issues Identified
- **Outdated valuation** — valuation older than policy threshold (e.g., >2 years)
- **Expired insurance** — policy lapsed without renewal notice to bank
- **Incomplete charge creation** — mortgage not registered; hypothecation not filed
- **Missing ownership support** — title deed not verified; ownership disputes undisclosed
- **Overvaluation risk** — collateral value inflated relative to market reality

- Assessed whether collateral records aligned with approved sanction terms

---

### ✅ Step 6: Checked Disbursement Controls

- Verified whether disbursement happened only after completion of required pre-disbursement conditions:

#### 📋 Disbursement Control Checklist

| Control | Verification Performed |
|---------|----------------------|
| **Condition Precedent Compliance** | All pre-disbursement conditions (insurance, registration, guarantees) fulfilled before release |
| **Disbursement Authority** | Disbursement approved by authorized person per delegation matrix |
| **End-Use Verification** | For term loans — evidence that funds used for stated purpose (invoices, contracts) |
| **Payment Method** | Direct payment to vendor/supplier vs. credit to borrower account — per policy |
| **Documentation at Disbursement** | Disbursement voucher, authorization, supporting documents properly filed |

- Identified any weaknesses where funds were disbursed despite incomplete compliance
- Flagged cases of "disburse first, document later" practices that increase risk

---

### ✅ Step 7: Reviewed Loan Classification and Provisioning

- Checked whether accounts were classified correctly based on overdue status and credit quality:

#### 📋 Classification & Provisioning Review

| Review Area | Verification Performed |
|-------------|----------------------|
| **Overdue Status** | Days past due calculated accurately; aging aligned with system records |
| **Classification Category** | Pass / Watchlist / Substandard / Doubtful / Loss assigned per regulatory criteria |
| **Provisioning Applied** | Provision rate (1%/5%/25%/50%/100%) applied correctly to classified amount |
| **Policy Consistency** | Classification and provisioning aligned with internal policy and NRB directives |
| **File Condition vs. System** | Actual file condition (repayment behavior, collateral status) matched system classification |

- Highlighted cases of:
  - **Delayed downgrade** — account deteriorating but classification not updated timely
  - **Weak monitoring** — overdue accounts not flagged or followed up systematically
  - **Possible under-provisioning** — provision amount insufficient relative to risk

---

### ✅ Step 8: Verified Renewal and Monitoring Discipline

- For revolving or renewable credit facilities, checked renewal and post-disbursement monitoring:

#### 🔹 Renewal Verification Checklist

| Check | Verification Performed |
|-------|----------------------|
| **Timely Renewal** | Renewal processed before expiry of existing limit; no gap in facility coverage |
| **Updated Financials** | Recent financial statements obtained and reviewed before renewal decision |
| **Stock/Business Review** | For working capital — stock statements, business performance reviewed |
| **Expired Limit Tracking** | System or manual tracking to flag limits approaching expiry for proactive renewal |

#### 🔹 Post-Disbursement Monitoring Assessment
- Assessed whether post-disbursement monitoring was **active or merely procedural**:
  - Regular site visits or borrower contact documented
  - Financial performance monitored against projections
  - Early warning signals (delayed payments, covenant breaches) acted upon
  - Monitoring reports prepared and reviewed by credit management

---

### ✅ Step 9: Analyzed Portfolio-Level Risk Themes

- Looked beyond individual files and reviewed broader portfolio trends:

#### 📋 Portfolio-Level Risk Analysis

| Risk Theme | Assessment Approach |
|------------|-------------------|
| **Sector Concentration** | Exposure by sector analyzed; concentration vs. policy limits assessed |
| **Overdue Trends** | Aging analysis of overdue portfolio; trend of NPL ratio over time |
| **Documentation Deficiency Patterns** | Common missing documents across files; systemic documentation gaps |
| **Collateral Shortfall Trends** | Frequency of collateral coverage below policy minimum; valuation gaps |
| **Renewal Backlog** | Number of facilities renewed late; reasons for delays; impact on risk |

- Identified whether issues were **isolated or systemic** within the credit function
- Recommended group-level interventions for systemic weaknesses

---

### ✅ Step 10: Reported Findings and Recommendations

- Documented observations in structured audit format:

#### 📋 Audit Finding Template

Finding #: [Sequential Number]
Area: Credit File Review — [Specific Area, e.g., Documentation, Collateral]
- OBSERVATION: [Clear, factual description of the control gap or deficiency]
- RISK: [Impact — credit loss, regulatory penalty, reputational damage]
- CAUSE: [Root cause — process gap, training need, resource constraint]
- RECOMMENDATION: [Specific, actionable improvement suggestion]
- RISK RATING: 🔴 Critical | 🟠 High | 🟡 Medium | 🟢 Low


#### 🔹 Common Findings & Recommendations

| Finding Category | Example Observation | Recommended Action |
|-----------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Incomplete Documentation** | Missing updated financials or insurance in loan files | Standardized file checklist; periodic file quality reviews |
| **Weak Appraisal Support** | Sanction based on outdated or superficial analysis | Mandatory appraisal template; credit officer training |
| **Delayed Renewals** | Facilities renewed after expiry creating coverage gaps | Automated renewal alerts; renewal timeline policy |
| **Collateral Gaps** | Expired insurance or unregistered charges | Collateral tracking system; periodic collateral audit |
| **Classification Inconsistencies** | Overdue accounts not downgraded per policy | Automated classification triggers; monthly classification review |

- Provided recommendations to strengthen:
  - **Credit appraisal discipline** — standardized templates, training, quality review
  - **Documentation control** — checklists, file audits, digital document management
  - **Review and renewal process** — timelines, alerts, escalation for delays
  - **Post-disbursement monitoring** — active monitoring, early warning systems
  - **Credit risk reporting** — portfolio dashboards, exception reports, trend analysis
