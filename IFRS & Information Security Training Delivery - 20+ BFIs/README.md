## 🎯 Project Objective

This project focused on building practical understanding of **IFRS/NFRS implementation** and **information security awareness** across financial institutions.

The goal was to convert complex standards and technical security concepts into **simple, usable, real-world knowledge** for banking professionals.

> ⚠️ Confidentiality Note: Institution-specific materials and participant data are excluded.

# PART A: IFRS / NFRS TRAINING

---

## 🛠️ Step-by-Step Work Performed

---

### ✅ Step 1: Identified Training Need

- IFRS transition created major knowledge gaps in BFIs
- Teams struggled with:
  - IFRS concepts vs local GAAP
  - ECL and financial instruments
  - journal-level application
- Identified need for **banking-specific practical IFRS training**

---

### ✅ Step 2: Designed IFRS Training Modules

Built structured modules:

- IFRS overview & transition (IFRS 1)
- IFRS 9: Financial Instruments
- ECL model (3-stage approach)
- IAS 19: Employee benefits
- IAS 12: Income tax & deferred tax
- IFRS 16: Leases
- IFRS 13: Fair value measurement
- IFRS 7: Risk disclosures
- IAS 1: Financial statement presentation
- NRB reconciliation & regulatory adjustments

Each module included:
- banking examples
- journal entries
- case-based learning
- common mistakes

---

### ✅ Step 3: Delivered Training Across 20+ BFIs

- Conducted sessions for:
  - Finance teams  
  - Branch accountants  
  - Treasury staff  
  - Management  

Formats used:
- classroom workshops  
- hands-on IFRS exercises  
- Q&A-based learning sessions  

Adjusted depth based on audience level.

---

### ✅ Step 4: Provided Practical Transition Support

- Helped teams apply IFRS on real data
- Assisted in:
  - journal entry conversion  
  - ECL computation walkthrough  
  - chart of accounts mapping  
  - financial statement transition  

---

### ✅ Step 5: Developed Reference Materials

Created reusable learning tools:
- IFRS slide decks  
- worked examples  
- journal entry guides  
- IFRS checklists  
- FAQ documents

- insecure communication habits

- password hygiene & MFA

- email security  
- clean desk policy  
- data classification  
- device security  
- incident reporting  
- regulatory expectations  
- daily security practices

### ✅ Step 8: Delivered Awareness Sessions
