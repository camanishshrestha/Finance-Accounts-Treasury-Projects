## 🎯 Project Objective

A **Strategic Plan** is the compass that guides every decision a bank makes — from where to open new branches, how to price products, which sectors to lend in, how much capital to raise, to how technology should evolve. It translates the **Board's vision into actionable targets and measurable outcomes** over a defined planning horizon. At Lumbini Bikas Bank Ltd, I played a key role in **implementing the 5-Year Strategic Plan (2080–2085 BS)** — translating strategic aspirations into financial projections, departmental targets, and performance monitoring frameworks. Additionally, across both banks, I was responsible for **drafting and formulating internal banking policies and procedures** that governed day-to-day operations and ensured regulatory compliance.

### ✅ Step 1: Strategic Plan — Situational Analysis & Environmental Scanning

- Before the strategic plan could be operationalized, conducted a comprehensive **situational analysis** of the bank's current position and operating environment:

#### 📊 Internal Analysis

##### Financial Performance Assessment
- Analyzed the bank's **5-year historical financial performance**:
  - Balance sheet growth — total assets, loans, deposits CAGR
  - Income statement trends — NII, non-interest income, operating expenses, net profit growth
  - Profitability metrics — ROA, ROE, NIM, EPS trend
  - Efficiency metrics — cost-to-income ratio, revenue per employee, revenue per branch
  - Asset quality — NPL ratio, provision coverage ratio trend
  - Capital adequacy — CAR trajectory, Tier 1/Tier 2 composition
  - Liquidity — CD ratio, LCR, NSFR trend
- Identified **strengths** — areas where the bank outperformed peers
- Identified **weaknesses** — areas requiring improvement

##### Operational Assessment
- Assessed operational capabilities:
  - **Branch network** — geographic coverage, branch profitability, expansion opportunities
  - **Product portfolio** — product mix, product competitiveness, gaps vs. peer banks
  - **Technology infrastructure** — CBS capability, digital banking maturity, IT investment level
  - **Human resources** — staff strength, skill gaps, training needs, attrition rate
  - **Customer base** — customer segmentation, concentration, acquisition trend, retention rate
  - **Brand and market position** — market share, brand awareness, customer satisfaction

##### SWOT Analysis
- Prepared a comprehensive **SWOT Analysis**:

| | **Favorable** | **Unfavorable** |
|---|---|---|
| **Internal** | **Strengths:** Strong capital base, experienced management, loyal customer base, competitive deposit rates | **Weaknesses:** Limited branch network, technology gaps, high cost-to-income ratio, concentrated loan portfolio |
| **External** | **Opportunities:** Financial inclusion push, digital banking growth, merger/acquisition possibilities, underserved geographic markets | **Threats:** Increasing competition, regulatory tightening, interest rate compression, cybersecurity risks, economic slowdown |

#### 📊 External Analysis

##### PESTEL Analysis
- Conducted **PESTEL Analysis** of the external operating environment:

| Factor | Key Observations |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Political** | Government stability, federal structure impact on banking, political influence on lending |
| **Economic** | GDP growth, inflation, remittance inflow, trade deficit, private sector credit demand |
| **Social** | Financial inclusion, urbanization, youth demographics, digital adoption, financial literacy |
| **Technological** | Mobile banking growth, fintech disruption, cybersecurity threats, AI/ML in banking |
| **Environmental** | Climate risk on agriculture lending, green finance opportunities, ESG awareness |
| **Legal** | NRB directive changes, Companies Act amendments, merger regulations, data protection |

##### Competitive Landscape Analysis
- Analyzed the **competitive positioning** of the bank:
  - Market share comparison — deposits, loans, total assets vs. peer development banks
  - Product and pricing comparison — interest rates, fee structures, digital offerings
  - Branch network comparison — geographic reach and expansion plans of competitors
  - Recent strategic moves by competitors — mergers, digital launches, capital raises

### ✅ Step 5: Strategic Plan — Performance Monitoring Framework

- Designed the **Strategic Plan performance monitoring framework** to track execution:

#### 📊 Balanced Scorecard (BSC) Approach
- Adopted the **Balanced Scorecard** methodology to monitor strategic plan execution across 4 perspectives:

| Perspective | Focus | Example KPIs |
|-------------|-------|--------------|
| **Financial** | Profitability, growth, efficiency | ROA, ROE, NIM, NPAT growth, cost-to-income |
| **Customer** | Market share, satisfaction, acquisition | Customer growth rate, CASA ratio, NPS score, complaint resolution TAT |
| **Internal Process** | Operational efficiency, risk management | Loan TAT, digital transaction %, NPL ratio, audit findings closure rate |
| **Learning & Growth** | People capability, innovation, technology | Training hours/employee, staff attrition rate, digital channel adoption, IT uptime |

#### 📊 Monitoring Cadence

| Review Level | Frequency | Participants | Focus |
|--------------|-----------|--------------|-------|
| **Board Review** | Semi-Annual | Board of Directors | Strategic plan progress, major deviations, strategic course corrections |
| **Management Review** | Quarterly | CEO + Department Heads | KPI tracking, departmental action plan status, resource reallocation |
| **Departmental Review** | Monthly | Department Head + Team | Activity-level tracking, issue resolution, operational adjustments |

- Prepared **strategic plan progress reports** for each review cycle:
  - KPI dashboard — actual vs. target with traffic light indicators (🟢🟡🔴)
  - Action plan status — Completed / On Track / Delayed / At Risk
  - Key achievements during the period
  - Key challenges and bottlenecks
  - Recommended course corrections
  - Updated resource requirements

#### 📊 Policy Document Standard Structure
- Each policy followed a **standardized structure**:

- PURPOSE
Why does this policy exist?

- SCOPE
Who and what does this policy cover?

- DEFINITIONS
Key terms and their definitions

- POLICY STATEMENTS
Core policy rules and principles

- ROLES & RESPONSIBILITIES
Who is responsible for what?

- PROCEDURES
Step-by-step operational procedures

- LIMITS & THRESHOLDS
Quantitative limits and approval thresholds

- EXCEPTIONS

- REPORTING

- REVIEW & UPDATE

Approving authority (Management/Board)

### ✅ Step 11: Strategic Plan — Course Correction & Annual Review

- Conducted **annual strategic plan review** to assess progress and make course corrections:

#### 📊 Annual Review Process

1. **Data Compilation** — Gathered actual performance data for the completed year
2. **Target vs. Actual Analysis** — Compared actual results against strategic plan targets for each KPI
3. **Gap Analysis** — Identified areas where performance fell short of targets
4. **Root Cause Investigation** — Analyzed why gaps occurred:
   - External factors (market downturn, regulatory changes, competitive pressure)
   - Internal factors (execution gaps, resource constraints, organizational issues)
5. **Course Correction Recommendations** — Proposed adjustments:
   - Revised targets (if original targets were unrealistic given changed circumstances)
   - New strategic initiatives to address emerging opportunities or threats
   - Resource reallocation — shifting budget/people from underperforming to high-potential areas
   - Process improvements — addressing execution bottlenecks
6. **Updated Projections** — Revised remaining-year projections based on:
   - Actual performance trend
   - Updated macroeconomic and market assumptions
   - Approved course corrections
7. **Board Presentation** — Presented annual strategic review to Board for approval of course corrections
8. **Communication** — Cascaded approved changes to all departments through memos and meetings
