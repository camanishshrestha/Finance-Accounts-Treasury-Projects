## 🎯 Project Objective

Budgeting in a bank is not just a **financial planning exercise** — it is a **strategic declaration of how the institution intends to allocate resources, grow its balance sheet, manage costs, and deliver shareholder value** over the coming period. Across two development banks, I was responsible for preparing **annual and quarterly budgets**, conducting **variance analysis**, and — most significantly — implementing **Zero-Based Budgeting (ZBB) for the first time** at Lumbini Bikas Bank Ltd, which fundamentally transformed how the bank approached cost management and resource allocation.

## 🛠️ Step-by-Step: What I Did

### ✅ Step 1: Understanding the Budgeting Framework & Regulatory Context

- Studied the bank's existing **budgeting framework and practices**:
  - **Traditional Incremental Budgeting** — the method previously used, where prior year actuals were adjusted by a growth percentage to derive the new year's budget
  - **NRB Guidelines on Budgeting** — regulatory expectations for BFIs to prepare and submit annual budgets
  - **Board-approved Budget Policy** — internal policy governing the budget process, timelines, approval authority, and variance thresholds
- Identified **weaknesses of incremental budgeting** in the existing system:
  - Perpetuated **historical inefficiencies** — if a cost was incurred last year, it was automatically assumed for next year
  - No mechanism to **challenge the base** — every expense was taken for granted
  - Departments inflated budget requests knowing they would be cut — the **budget game**
  - Limited linkage between **budget allocations and strategic priorities**
  - Weak accountability — difficult to trace whether budgeted expenditures delivered value
- This understanding formed the foundation for recommending and implementing **Zero-Based Budgeting (ZBB)**

---

### ✅ Step 2: Annual Budget Preparation — Income Statement Budget

- Prepared the **Income Statement (Profit & Loss) Budget** covering all revenue and expense lines:

#### 📊 Interest Income Budget
- Projected **interest income from loans** based on:
  - Opening loan portfolio balance
  - Projected loan growth — aligned with strategic plan and credit pipeline
  - Weighted average lending rate — based on current portfolio rate and expected rate changes
  - Loan mix by product — term loans, overdraft, housing, SME, agriculture, personal (each with different yield)
  - Expected repayments and prepayments — reducing the earning base
  - Non-performing loan adjustments — NPL portion not generating interest income
- Projected **interest income from investments** based on:
  - T-Bill portfolio — projected holdings and weighted average yield
  - Development Bonds — coupon income and amortization schedule
  - Interbank placements — projected average placements and rates
  - NRB SDF placements — projected surplus and SDF rate
- Projected **interest income from other sources** — staff loan interest, penal interest, overdue interest recovery

#### 📊 Interest Expense Budget
- Projected **interest expense on deposits** based on:
  - Opening deposit base
  - Projected deposit growth — aligned with business targets and branch expansion
  - Deposit mix — savings (low cost), fixed deposits (by tenure — higher cost), current accounts (zero/low cost), call deposits
  - Weighted average deposit rate — current rates and expected adjustments per ALCO decisions
  - Seasonal deposit patterns — considering festival season inflows, government fund parking patterns
- Projected **interest expense on borrowings**:
  - Interbank borrowing — projected frequency, amounts, and rates

#### NII = Interest Income – Interest Expense

#### 📊 Non-Interest Income Budget
- Projected fee and commission income:
  - Loan processing fees, LC commission, BG commission
  - Remittance fees, ATM/card fees, locker fees
  - Foreign exchange gains
  - Dividend income from equity investments
  - Other operating income

#### 📊 Operating Expense Budget
- Projected all operating expenses:
  - **Staff costs** — salaries, allowances, bonus, PF/SSF contributions, gratuity, leave encashment, staff training, staff insurance
  - **Administrative expenses** — rent, electricity, communication, travel, printing, stationery, legal fees, audit fees
  - **Depreciation and amortization** — based on existing fixed asset register and planned capital expenditures
  - **IT expenses** — CBS maintenance, software licenses, cybersecurity, hardware maintenance
  - **Marketing expenses** — advertising, promotions, CSR activities
  - **Other operating expenses** — insurance, AGM expenses, Board meeting expenses

#### 📊 Provision & Tax Budget
- Projected **loan loss provisions** — based on projected NPL levels and NRB provisioning/IFRS 9 ECL requirements
- Projected **staff bonus** — as per regulatory requirements (percentage of net profit before bonus)
- Projected **income tax expense** — applying corporate tax rate to projected taxable profit with deferred tax adjustments
- Computed **projected Net Profit After Tax (NPAT)**

### ✅ Step 3: Annual Budget Preparation — Balance Sheet Budget

- Prepared the **Balance Sheet Budget** projecting all asset, liability, and equity positions:

#### 📊 Asset Side Budget

| Asset Category | Projection Methodology |
|---------------|----------------------|
| **Cash & NRB Balance** | Based on projected deposit base × CRR requirement + operational cash needs |
| **Loan Portfolio** | Opening balance + projected disbursements – projected repayments – projected write-offs |
| **Investment Portfolio** | T-Bills, Bonds, equity — based on SLR requirement, surplus deployment strategy, ALCO decisions |
| **Interbank Placements** | Based on projected surplus liquidity and counterparty limits |
| **Fixed Assets** | Opening balance + planned CAPEX – projected disposals – depreciation |
| **Other Assets** | Interest receivable, prepaid expenses, deferred tax assets, other receivables |

#### 📊 Liability Side Budget

| Liability Category | Projection Methodology |
|-------------------|----------------------|
| **Deposits** | Opening balance + projected new mobilization – projected withdrawals/maturities, by product type |
| **Borrowings** | Interbank, NRB facilities, debentures — based on funding gap and liquidity strategy |
| **Other Liabilities** | Interest payable, provisions, accrued expenses, deferred tax liabilities |

#### 📊 Equity Budget
- Opening equity + projected retained earnings (NPAT – projected dividend) + any new capital issuance
- Projected **CAR (Capital Adequacy Ratio)** based on balance sheet budget — ensuring CAR remained above NRB minimum

### ✅ Step 4: Annual Budget Preparation — Capital Expenditure (CAPEX) Budget

- Prepared the **CAPEX Budget** for planned capital investments:
  - **Branch expansion** — new branch setup costs, leasehold improvements
  - **IT infrastructure** — server upgrades, network equipment, CBS enhancements, cybersecurity tools
  - **Vehicles** — new vehicle purchases or replacements
  - **Furniture and fixtures** — office furniture, branch furnishing
  - **ATM/POS machines** — new installations and replacements
  - **Software investments** — new application licenses, system integrations
- Each CAPEX item was evaluated for:
  - **Business justification** — why is this investment needed?
  - **Estimated cost** — vendor quotes, market benchmarks
  - **Expected benefits** — revenue enhancement, cost reduction, efficiency gains, regulatory compliance
  - **Payback period / ROI** — financial viability assessment
- CAPEX budget was presented to **management and Board** for approval within overall budget

### ✅ Step 5: Zero-Based Budgeting (ZBB) Implementation — Lumbini Bikas Bank Ltd

> This was the **landmark achievement** — implementing **Zero-Based Budgeting (ZBB) for the first time** at Lumbini Bikas Bank Ltd

ZBB fundamentally changed the bank's budgeting approach from **"What did we spend last year?"** to **"Why should we spend this at all?"**

#### 🏗️ ZBB Implementation — Step-by-Step Process

##### Phase 1: ZBB Concept Introduction & Management Buy-In
- Prepared a **ZBB proposal presentation** for Senior Management and Board explaining:
  - What is Zero-Based Budgeting and how it differs from incremental budgeting
  - Benefits of ZBB — cost optimization, elimination of wasteful spending, better resource allocation
  - Challenges of ZBB — more time-intensive, requires departmental engagement, cultural change
  - Implementation roadmap and timeline
- Secured **management and Board approval** to implement ZBB for the upcoming budget cycle

##### Phase 2: Decision Unit Identification
- Identified all **decision units** (budget units/cost centers) within the bank:
  - Head Office departments — Finance, Credit, Operations, HR, IT, Legal, Compliance, Risk, Marketing
  - Branch network — each branch as a separate decision unit
  - Support functions — Administration, Premises, Transport, Security
- Each decision unit was designated a **decision unit manager** (typically the department head or branch manager) responsible for preparing their ZBB submission

##### Phase 3: Decision Package Development
- Required each decision unit manager to prepare **decision packages** for every activity and expense:
  - **Purpose** — Why does this activity/expense exist? What business objective does it serve?
  - **Description** — What exactly is the activity? What resources does it require?
  - **Cost** — How much will it cost? (detailed breakdown)
  - **Benefits** — What value does it deliver? Revenue generation? Risk reduction? Compliance requirement?
  - **Consequences of elimination** — What happens if this activity/expense is NOT approved?
  - **Alternatives** — Are there cheaper or more efficient ways to achieve the same outcome?
  - **Priority ranking** — How critical is this activity compared to other activities in the same decision unit?
- Conducted **training sessions** for department heads and branch managers on how to prepare ZBB decision packages
- Provided **templates and guidelines** to standardize decision package submissions across all units

##### Phase 4: Ranking & Prioritization
- Collected decision packages from all decision units
- Facilitated **ranking sessions** with Senior Management:
  - Each decision package was ranked by priority — **Critical / High / Medium / Low / Discretionary**
  - Mandatory/regulatory expenses were categorized as **non-discretionary** (must-fund)
  - Remaining expenses were ranked based on **value contribution** and **strategic alignment**
- Prepared a **consolidated ranking matrix** showing all decision packages ranked from highest to lowest priority
- Drew a **funding line** — activities above the line were funded; activities below the line were:
  - Deferred to next budget cycle
  - Eliminated
  - Reduced in scope/cost

##### Phase 5: ZBB Budget Compilation & Approval
- Compiled the approved decision packages into the **final ZBB-based budget**
- Reconciled ZBB budget with:
  - Income statement budget
  - Balance sheet budget
  - CAPEX budget
  - Cash flow budget
- Presented the **final ZBB budget** to the Board for approval
- Budget was approved with clear documentation of:
  - Every expense justified from zero
  - Priority ranking of all activities
  - Eliminated/deferred items with reasons

### ✅ Step 6: Quarterly Budget Preparation & Rolling Forecasts

- In addition to the annual budget, prepared **quarterly budgets** that:
  - Broke down the annual budget into quarterly targets
  - Adjusted for **seasonal patterns** — e.g., higher deposit mobilization during Dashain/Tihar festival, government fiscal year-end fund parking patterns
  - Incorporated **updated assumptions** — NRB monetary policy changes, interest rate movements, market conditions
- Prepared **rolling forecasts** — updating full-year projections each quarter based on actual YTD performance:

###  Actual (Q1) + Forecast (Q2 + Q3 + Q4) = Updated Full-Year Forecast

  - Highlighted **forecast vs. original budget deviations** with explanations
  - Recommended **corrective actions** for underperforming areas

### ✅ Step 7: Budget Variance Analysis

- Conducted **monthly and quarterly budget variance analysis** — comparing actual results against budget:

#### 📊 Variance Analysis Framework

| Component | Analysis Performed |
|-----------|------------------|
| **Interest Income** | Actual vs. Budget — decomposed into volume variance (loan/investment growth) and rate variance (yield changes) |
| **Interest Expense** | Actual vs. Budget — decomposed into volume variance (deposit/borrowing growth) and rate variance (cost changes) |
| **Net Interest Income** | Actual NII vs. Budgeted NII — spread and margin analysis |
| **Non-Interest Income** | Actual fees, commissions, FX gains vs. budget |
| **Operating Expenses** | Line-by-line actual vs. budget comparison with percentage variance |
| **Provisions** | Actual ECL/NRB provisions vs. budgeted provisions |
| **Net Profit** | Bottom-line actual vs. budget with bridge analysis |
| **Balance Sheet** | Actual loan growth, deposit growth, investment, capital vs. budget |
| **Ratios** | Actual CAR, NIM, CD ratio, cost-to-income ratio vs. budgeted ratios |

#### 📊 Variance Classification
- Classified variances as:
  - **Favorable** — actual better than budget (higher income / lower expense)
  - **Adverse** — actual worse than budget (lower income / higher expense)
- Investigated all **material variances** (exceeding threshold — typically ±5% or ±10%):
  - **Root cause analysis** — why did the variance occur?
  - **Controllable vs. uncontrollable** — was it within management's control or driven by external factors?
  - **One-time vs. recurring** — is this a one-off or will it persist?
  - **Corrective action** — what should be done to address adverse variances?

### ✅ Step 8: Cost Control & Efficiency Monitoring

- Prepared **cost control reports** for management with focus on operational efficiency:

#### 📊 Key Efficiency Metrics Monitored

| Metric | Formula | Purpose |
|--------|---------|---------|
| **Cost-to-Income Ratio** | Operating Expenses / Operating Income | Overall operational efficiency |
| **Staff Cost Ratio** | Staff Costs / Operating Income | Human resource cost efficiency |
| **Cost per Branch** | Total Branch Costs / Number of Branches | Branch-level cost efficiency |
| **Cost per Employee** | Total Operating Costs / Number of Employees | Per capita cost efficiency |
| **Revenue per Employee** | Total Operating Income / Number of Employees | Per capita productivity |
| **Revenue per Branch** | Total Operating Income / Number of Branches | Branch-level productivity |
| **IT Cost Ratio** | IT Expenses / Operating Expenses | Technology cost proportion |

- Identified **cost reduction opportunities**:
  - Consolidation of underperforming branches
  - Renegotiation of vendor contracts
  - Process automation to reduce manual effort
  - Energy cost optimization
  - Print and stationery reduction through digitization
- Presented cost control recommendations to **management and ALCO**
- Tracked **implementation of cost-saving initiatives** and measured actual savings achieved

### ✅ Step 9: Budget Communication & Department Coordination

- After Board approval of the annual budget, managed the **budget communication and rollout process**:
  - **Budget circular** — issued to all departments and branches communicating approved budgets and targets
  - **Department-level budget packs** — distributed individual budgets to each decision unit/department/branch
  - **Target-setting meetings** — conducted meetings with department heads and branch managers to discuss:
    - Their specific budget targets (revenue and cost)
    - KPIs linked to budget performance
    - Reporting requirements and timelines
    - Escalation process for budget overruns
  - **Branch-level targets** — set specific targets for each branch:
    - Deposit mobilization targets
    - Loan disbursement targets
    - Revenue targets
    - Expense limits
    - Profitability targets
- Ensured **alignment between budgets and individual/team performance objectives**
- Established **monthly budget review meetings** with department heads

---

### ✅ Step 10: Budget Documentation & Regulatory Submission

- Prepared complete **budget documentation** for internal records and regulatory compliance:
  - **Board-approved Budget Document** — comprehensive document with all budget components, assumptions, and Board resolution
  - **Budget Assumptions Document** — detailed documentation of all assumptions used:
    - Macroeconomic assumptions (GDP growth, inflation, interest rates)
    - NRB monetary policy assumptions
    - Business growth assumptions
    - Rate assumptions (deposit rates, lending rates, investment yields)
    - Cost escalation assumptions (salary increment, rent increase, inflation adjustment)
  - **Budget Summary Presentation** — executive summary with key highlights for Board and management
  - **NRB Budget Submission** — submitted approved budget to NRB as per regulatory requirements
- Maintained **budget version control** — tracking original budget, revised budget (if any), and actual performance
- Archived all budget-related documents for **audit trail and future reference**

### ✅ Step 11: Garima Bikas Bank — Annual Budget Finalization (FY 2024/25)

- At Garima Bikas Bank Ltd, led the **finalization of the Annual Budget for FY 2024/25**:
  - Reviewed draft budget prepared by the team
  - Validated all assumptions against current market conditions and NRB monetary policy
  - Conducted **sensitivity analysis** — testing budget outcomes under different scenarios:
    - Base case — most likely assumptions
    - Optimistic case — favorable market conditions
    - Pessimistic case — adverse conditions (higher NPLs, lower growth, tighter liquidity)
  - Refined expense budgets based on prior year actual trends and ZBB principles
  - Coordinated with all departments for final budget inputs
  - Presented finalized budget to **Senior Management and Board** for approval
  - Obtained Board approval and initiated budget rollout process
