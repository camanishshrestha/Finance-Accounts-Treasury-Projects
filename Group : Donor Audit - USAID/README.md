## 🎯 Project Objective

USAID audits are among the most **stringent donor audits**, governed by US federal regulations under **2 CFR 200 (Uniform Guidance)**.

The core objective is to verify whether **US taxpayer funds were used in accordance with applicable regulations and award terms**, with a strong focus on:

- Allowability of costs  
- Allocability of costs  
- Reasonableness of costs  

As Team Leader/Team Member, I performed audits of USAID-funded projects ensuring full compliance with US federal requirements and award conditions.

> ⚠️ *Note: All project financial data and findings remain confidential. Only audit methodology is shared.*

## 🛠️ Step-by-Step: What I Did

---

### ✅ Step 1: Understanding USAID Compliance Framework

Reviewed core regulations:

- 2 CFR 200 (Uniform Guidance)
- ADS 303 (Grants & Cooperative Agreements)
- ADS 591 (Audit Requirements)
- FAR (for contracts)
- USAID Standard Provisions

#### Cost Principles (3 Pillars)

- **Allowability** — Is the cost permitted?
- **Allocability** — Does it benefit the project?
- **Reasonableness** — Is the cost justified?

---

### ✅ Step 2: Audit Planning

- Reviewed award agreement and budget
- Identified restrictions and compliance requirements
- Defined risk areas:
  - Unallowable costs
  - Procurement violations
  - Sub-recipient risks
  - Weak internal controls

---

### ✅ Step 3: Cost Allowability Testing

Evaluated transactions against 2 CFR 200:

Checked:
- Necessity for project
- Compliance with award terms
- Unallowable cost categories:
  - Alcohol
  - Entertainment
  - Lobbying
  - Fines & penalties
  - Fundraising
  - Bad debts

Classified findings as:
- Allowable
- Questioned
- Unallowable

---

### ✅ Step 4: Transaction Vouching

Verified supporting documents:

- Invoice / receipt
- Purchase order / contract
- Goods received note
- Payment proof
- Approval authorization
- Budget alignment

Identified:
- Unsupported costs
- Duplicate payments
- Misclassified expenses

---

### ✅ Step 5: Indirect Cost (NICRA) Review

Verified:
- Valid NICRA agreement
- Correct indirect cost rate
- Correct MTDC base calculation
- Exclusion of non-allowable base items

Detected:
- Overcharged indirect costs
- Missing or invalid NICRA application

---

### ✅ Step 6: Procurement Audit

Verified compliance with 2 CFR 200 thresholds:

- Micro purchase
- Small purchase
- Competitive bidding
- Sealed bids
- Sole source justification

Checked:
- Competition adequacy
- Vendor selection fairness
- Conflict of interest
- SAM.gov exclusion screening

Findings included:
- Split procurement
- Sole source misuse
- Insufficient quotations

---

### ✅ Step 7: Sub-Recipient Monitoring

Reviewed:

- Risk assessment of partners
- Sub-award agreements
- Financial reporting
- Site visits and monitoring
- Audit compliance (if applicable)

Ensured:
- Proper oversight by prime recipient
- Correct flow-down of USAID rules

---

### ✅ Step 8: Cost Share Verification (If Applicable)

Verified:
- Approved cost-share commitments
- Allowability of contributions
- Proper valuation of in-kind contributions
- Supporting documentation

---

### ✅ Step 9: Property Management Review

Checked:
- Asset register accuracy
- Physical verification
- Asset condition and usage
- USAID marking compliance
- Disposal procedures

---

### ✅ Step 10: Internal Control Assessment

Evaluated:
- Control environment
- Risk assessment process
- Segregation of duties
- Financial reporting systems
- Monitoring mechanisms

Identified control weaknesses impacting compliance and reporting accuracy.

---

### ✅ Step 11: Audit Reporting

Prepared USAID audit report including:

- Financial statement opinion
- Internal control findings
- Compliance findings
- Schedule of expenditure
- Questioned costs
- Corrective action plan

Reported:
- Ineligible costs
- Unsupported costs
- Compliance deviations

Submitted to USAID and relevant oversight bodies.
