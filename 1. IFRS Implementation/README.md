# Project 1: IFRS Convergence & First-Time Adoption — Nepalese BFIs

## 📌 Overview
This project covers large-scale IFRS/NFRS implementation across 20+ Banks and Financial Institutions (BFIs) in Nepal between 2019–2024. The objective was to transition from NRB/local GAAP to IFRS and enhance financial reporting quality, compliance, and risk measurement.

## 🎯 Objective
- Enable smooth transition to IFRS/NFRS
- Identify and resolve GAAP-to-IFRS gaps
- Improve transparency and consistency in financial reporting
- Implement forward-looking credit risk models

## 🛠️ Key Work Performed
- Conducted GAAP-to-IFRS gap analysis across financial instruments, taxation, and employee benefits  
- Redesigned Chart of Accounts and restructured financial statements (including OCI)  
- Implemented IFRS 9 classification (SPPI & business model assessment)  
- Built Excel-based Expected Credit Loss (ECL) models using PD, LGD, EAD with scenario analysis  
- Accounted for IAS 19 employee benefits and IAS 12 deferred taxes  
- Prepared IFRS 1 transition balance sheets and reconciliations  
- Aligned reporting with NRB, SEBON, and NEPSE requirements  
- Delivered IFRS training and developed accounting manuals  

## 📊 Key Outcomes
- IFRS implemented across 20+ BFIs  
- Multiple ECL models developed with forward-looking assumptions  
- Large-scale IFRS training delivered to finance teams  
- Improved regulatory compliance and financial transparency
