## 🎯 Project Objective

Life insurance companies handle some of the most **sensitive financial transactions** — processing death claims, maturity claims, and survival benefit claims where accuracy, timeliness, and documentation integrity directly impact **policyholders' families and beneficiaries**. Any lapse in claims processing controls can result in **fraudulent claim payments, delayed settlements causing customer distress, or wrongful claim rejections damaging the company's reputation**. As Team Leader/Team Member, I conducted focused internal audits of **claims processing and underwriting operations** at two life insurance companies — evaluating controls, identifying gaps, and recommending improvements.

## 🛠️ Step-by-Step: What I Did

---

### ✅ Step 1: Understanding Life Insurance Operations

- Before commencing audit fieldwork, studied the **life insurance business model and operations**:

#### 📋 Key Life Insurance Operations

| Function | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Underwriting** | Risk assessment and acceptance of new policy proposals — determining insurability and premium |
| **Policy Issuance** | Issuing policy documents after underwriting approval and premium payment |
| **Premium Collection** | Collecting regular premiums (monthly/quarterly/annual) from policyholders |
| **Claims Processing** | Processing and settling claims — death claims, maturity claims, survival benefits, surrender |
| **Reinsurance** | Ceding excess risk to reinsurers through treaty and facultative arrangements |
| **Investment** | Investing policyholder funds in approved asset classes to generate returns |

- Studied relevant **regulatory framework**:
  - Insurance Act, 2049 (Nepal)
  - Insurance Board directives and guidelines
  - IAIS (International Association of Insurance Supervisors) core principles
  - Anti-Money Laundering (AML) requirements for insurance

---

### ✅ Step 2: Audit Planning — Claims & Underwriting Focus

- Developed **risk-based audit plan** specifically targeting claims and underwriting:

#### 📊 Key Risk Areas Identified

| Area | Key Risks |
|------|-----------|
| **Claims Processing** | Fraudulent claims, delayed settlements, inadequate documentation, unauthorized payments, incorrect claim amount computation |
| **Underwriting** | Adverse selection (insuring high-risk without adequate assessment), inadequate medical examination, incorrect premium computation, policy document errors |

- Defined **audit objectives**:
  - Verify that claims are **processed accurately, completely, and timely**
  - Verify that claim payments are **properly authorized and documented**
  - Verify that underwriting decisions are **based on adequate risk assessment**
  - Assess compliance with **Insurance Board guidelines and internal policies**
  - Identify **control weaknesses** and recommend improvements

- Selected **audit sample** using:
  - **Risk-based sampling** — focusing on large claims, early claims (within 2-3 years of policy), suspicious patterns
  - **Random sampling** — representative sample across claim types and branches
  - **Judgmental sampling** — specific cases flagged by management or prior audits

---

### ✅ Step 3: Death Claim File Review

- Conducted detailed review of **death claim files** — the most sensitive and high-risk claim category:

#### 📋 Death Claim Documentation Checklist

| Document | Verification Performed |
|----------|----------------------|
| **Claim Intimation Form** | Timely intimation by nominee/beneficiary; completeness of information |
| **Death Certificate** | Authenticity verification; cause of death; issuing authority |
| **Policy Document** | Policy in-force at date of death; premium paid up to date; policy terms |
| **Identity Proof of Deceased** | Citizenship certificate, passport — identity confirmation |
| **Identity Proof of Nominee** | Nominee/beneficiary identity verification |
| **FIR / Post-Mortem Report** | Required for unnatural deaths — accident, suicide, suspicious circumstances |
| **Hospital Records / Medical Certificate** | Cause of death details; treatment history; attending doctor certificate |
| **Nominee Verification Report** | Nominee relationship verification; field investigation report (if conducted) |
| **Original Policy Bond** | Submitted by claimant; verified against company records |
| **Premium Payment Records** | All premiums paid; no lapsed period; reinstatement history (if any) |
| **Assignment Records** | Any policy assignment — absolute or conditional |
| **Previous Claim History** | Any previous partial claims or riders claimed |

#### 🔹 Key Verification Checks:
- **Early Claims (within 2-3 years):** Enhanced scrutiny — higher risk of non-disclosure or fraud
- **Cause of Death vs. Medical History:** Cross-checked cause of death against proposal form medical disclosures — identifying potential non-disclosure
- **Suicide Clause:** Verified compliance with suicide exclusion clause (typically excluded within first year)
- **Nominee Legitimacy:** Verified nominee was correctly designated and legally entitled

### ✅ Step 4: Maturity & Survival Benefit Claim Review

- Reviewed **maturity claim and survival benefit claim files**:

#### 📋 Maturity Claim Verification

| Check | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Policy Maturity Date** | Verified maturity date calculation — from policy commencement + policy term |
| **Sum Assured** | Verified sum assured as per policy document |
| **Bonus Computation** | Verified accumulated bonuses (reversionary bonus, terminal bonus) — computation accuracy |
| **Maturity Amount** | Sum Assured + Accumulated Bonuses = Total Maturity Amount — recalculated |
| **Premium Status** | All premiums paid in full — no outstanding premiums |
| **Policyholder Identity** | Identity verification of person collecting maturity proceeds |
| **Payment Authorization** | Approved at appropriate authority level per claims policy |
| **Tax Deduction** | TDS deducted on maturity proceeds (if applicable under tax law) |

#### 📋 Survival Benefit Verification

| Check | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Benefit Schedule** | Verified survival benefit dates as per policy terms |
| **Benefit Amount** | Verified benefit amount computation — percentage of sum assured |
| **Payment Records** | Verified past survival benefit payments — correct amounts on correct dates |
| **Policyholder Alive** | Confirmation that policyholder is alive at benefit date (survival certificate or equivalent) |

---

### ✅ Step 5: Surrender & Partial Withdrawal Claim Review

- Reviewed **policy surrender and partial withdrawal files**:

#### 🔹 Surrender Value Verification:
- Verified **surrender value computation**:
  - **Guaranteed Surrender Value (GSV)** — minimum surrender value as per IRDA/Insurance Board guidelines
  - **Special Surrender Value (SSV)** — company-computed surrender value based on policy benefits and premiums paid
  - **Surrender value paid** = Higher of GSV and SSV
- Verified **minimum tenure requirement** — policies typically must be in force for minimum period (e.g., 3 years) before eligible for surrender
- Verified **surrender request documentation** — policyholder's written surrender request, identity proof, policy bond submission
- Checked for **loan against policy** — any outstanding policy loan deducted from surrender value before payment
- Verified **payment authorization** at appropriate level

---

### ✅ Step 6: Claim Payment Authorization & Settlement Audit

- Audited the **claim payment authorization process**:

#### 🔹 Approval Hierarchy Verification:

| Claim Amount | Approval Authority |
|--------------|-------------------|
| Up to NPR X | Claims Officer |
| NPR X to NPR Y | Claims Manager |
| NPR Y to NPR Z | Head of Claims |
| Above NPR Z | Claims Committee / CEO |

#### 🔹 Key Control Checks:
- Checked for instances where:
  - Claims were approved **without proper documentation**
  - Claims were approved by **unauthorized persons**
  - Claims were approved **above the approver's limit**
  - Payment was made to **incorrect beneficiary**
- Verified **payment mode**:
  - Bank transfer to nominee's verified bank account (preferred)
  - Cheque payment — proper endorsement and delivery
  - No cash payments for large claims (control requirement)
- Verified **payment recording** — accurately recorded in claims system and General Ledger

---

### ✅ Step 7: Turnaround Time (TAT) Analysis

- Analyzed **claims settlement turnaround time** — a key performance and customer service metric:

#### 🔹 TAT Computation & Analysis:
TAT = Date of Claim Settlement — Date of Claim Intimation


- Compared TAT against:
  - **Company's internal TAT targets** (e.g., death claims within 30 days, maturity within 15 days)
  - **Insurance Board prescribed timelines**
  - **Industry benchmarks**

#### 🔹 Root Cause Investigation for Delayed Claims:
- Incomplete documentation from claimant
- Internal processing bottlenecks
- Investigation delays (for suspicious claims)
- Reinsurance approval delays (for large claims)
- System/process inefficiencies

#### 🔹 TAT Analysis Report Outputs:
- Average TAT by claim type
- TAT distribution — percentage settled within target vs. beyond target
- Trend analysis — improving or deteriorating TAT
- Recommended process improvements to **reduce TAT and improve customer experience**

### ✅ Step 8: Underwriting File Review

- Conducted detailed review of **underwriting/new business files**:

#### 📋 Underwriting Verification Checklist

| Area | Verification Performed |
|------|----------------------|
| **Proposal Form** | Completeness of proposal form — all sections filled, signed by proposer |
| **Personal Details** | Name, age, occupation, income — verified against identity documents |
| **Medical Disclosure** | Health declaration — existing diseases, hospitalization history, family health history |
| **Medical Examination** | Medical report from company-approved doctor — completed for policies above threshold sum assured |
| **Age Proof** | Age verification document — citizenship, birth certificate, passport |
| **Income Proof** | Income documents — salary slip, tax return, business documents — for income-proportionate sum assured |
| **Sum Assured Reasonableness** | Sum assured proportionate to income (Human Life Value approach) — not excessively high |
| **Premium Computation** | Premium correctly computed based on age, sum assured, policy term, rider selections, and risk loading (if any) |
| **Risk Classification** | Correct risk classification — standard, substandard, rated-up, declined |
| **Underwriting Decision** | Decision properly documented with reasons — accepted, accepted with loading, postponed, declined |
| **Approval Authority** | Underwriting decision approved by authorized underwriter per authority matrix |
| **Rider Selection** | Optional riders (accidental death, critical illness, waiver of premium) properly documented and priced |
| **Nomination** | Nominee details recorded correctly |
| **Policy Issuance** | Policy document issued accurately — reflecting all terms, conditions, and exclusions |

#### 🔹 Key Underwriting Risk Checks:
- **Adverse Selection Detection:** Identified patterns where:
  - Very high sum assured relative to income
  - Multiple policies taken in short period
  - Proposal submitted shortly before claim (early claim indicator)
- **Non-Disclosure Detection:** Cross-referenced medical disclosures with hospital records (where available)
- **Agent Influence:** Checked for patterns of specific agents generating disproportionate claims — potential moral hazard

---

### ✅ Step 9: Claims Register & MIS Verification

- Verified the **claims register and MIS reports** maintained by the company:

#### 🔹 Claims Register Verification:
- **Completeness** — all intimated claims recorded in register
- **Accuracy** — claim details (amount, date, type, status) correctly recorded
- **Status tracking** — open, under investigation, approved, settled, rejected, repudiated

#### 🔹 Outstanding Claims Report:
- Verified outstanding claims at reporting date
- Assessed adequacy of **outstanding claims provision** (IBNR — Incurred But Not Reported, and case reserves)

#### 🔹 Claims MIS Metrics:
- **Claim settlement ratio** — percentage of claims settled vs. total claims received
- **Repudiation ratio** — percentage of claims rejected
- **Average claim size** — by product type
- **Claims by cause** — natural death, accidental death, maturity, surrender
