## 🎯 Project Objective

As Team Leader, I conducted branch operational audits across **60+ branches nationwide** as part of a commercial bank internal audit engagement. The objective was to assess whether branch-level operations were being carried out with proper **control, accuracy, compliance, and documentation discipline**, while also identifying operational weaknesses and recommending corrective improvements.

> ⚠️ *Note: Specific branch-level findings, customer information, and internal control details remain confidential. This project describes only the audit approach, responsibilities, and nature of work performed.*

---

## 🛠️ Step-by-Step: What I Did

---

### ✅ Step 1: Planned Branch Audit Visits

- Prepared branch-wise audit schedules and visit plans
- Prioritized branches based on:
  - **Size of operations** — transaction volume, deposit/loan portfolio
  - **Past audit findings** — branches with recurring issues or high-risk ratings
  - **Location risk** — remote areas, high-crime zones, new branches
  - **Management concern areas** — branches flagged for review by senior management
- Reviewed prior branch audit reports before each visit to understand historical issues
- Prepared branch-specific checklists for operational audit coverage

---

### ✅ Step 2: Reviewed Branch Operations End-to-End

- Assessed key branch processes including:

#### 📋 Branch Operational Areas Reviewed

| Process Area | Key Checks Performed |
|--------------|---------------------|
| **Account Opening** | KYC completeness, documentation, authorization, system entry accuracy |
| **Cash Receipt & Payment** | Teller procedures, cash handling limits, dual custody, receipt issuance |
| **Teller Operations** | Cash balancing, exception handling, override controls, end-of-day reconciliation |
| **Clearing Activities** | Cheque handling, clearing register maintenance, return item processing |
| **Locker Operations** | Access control, dual key system, register maintenance, rent collection |
| **Internal Approvals** | Delegation of authority compliance, maker-checker discipline, exception approvals |

- Checked whether branch operations were being performed as per bank policy and operating guidelines
- Verified whether daily operational controls were actually functioning in practice, not just documented

---

### ✅ Step 3: Verified Cash Management Controls

- Conducted physical cash verification during branch visits:

#### 🔹 Cash Verification Procedures

| Check | Verification Performed |
|-------|----------------------|
| **Teller Cash Position** | Counted cash in each teller drawer; compared with system balance |
| **Vault Cash Balance** | Verified vault cash against branch cash ledger and CBS balance |
| **Joint Custody Controls** | Confirmed dual key access; verified both custodians present during cash handling |
| **Excess Cash Handling** | Checked whether excess cash was deposited to NRB/head office as per policy |
| **Surprise Cash Count** | Conducted unannounced cash counts to test control effectiveness |

- Compared physical cash with branch records and system balance
- Reported any shortages, excesses, or control weaknesses with root cause analysis

---

### ✅ Step 4: Tested KYC / AML Compliance

- Reviewed customer files to assess KYC completeness:

#### 📋 KYC Documentation Checklist

| Document | Verification Performed |
|----------|----------------------|
| **Customer Identification** | Citizenship/Passport copy verified; photo matched with customer |
| **PAN Registration** | PAN card copy attached; PAN verified against IRD database where possible |
| **Account Opening Form** | Form completely filled; signatures verified; risk category assigned |
| **Photographs** | Recent photographs attached; digital photo captured in CBS |
| **Address Proof** | Utility bill, bank statement, or other acceptable address verification |
| **Risk-Based Information** | Source of funds, occupation, expected transaction pattern documented |

- Checked whether high-risk accounts and suspicious patterns were being handled appropriately:
  - Enhanced due diligence for PEPs, high-net-worth individuals, cash-intensive businesses
  - Transaction monitoring for unusual patterns or thresholds
  - Suspicious Transaction Report (STR) filing process awareness at branch level
- Identified documentation gaps and compliance deviations at branch level

---

### ✅ Step 5: Reviewed Branch Reconciliations and Pending Items

- Examined branch-level reconciliations:

#### 🔹 Reconciliation Areas Reviewed

| Reconciliation Type | Key Checks |
|--------------------|------------|
| **Inter-Branch Balances** | Verified matching entries between branches; investigated differences |
| **Suspense Accounts** | Reviewed nature of suspense items; checked aging and clearance timeline |
| **Sundry Balances** | Verified legitimacy of sundry debits/credits; checked authorization |
| **Un-cleared Entries** | Reviewed old uncleared cheques, pending transfers, system glitches |

#### 🔹 Reconciliation Quality Assessment
- Checked whether reconciliations were:
  - **Prepared regularly** — daily/weekly as per policy
  - **Reviewed by responsible officials** — supervisor/branch manager sign-off
  - **Cleared on time** — items resolved within prescribed timelines
- Flagged long-outstanding items requiring management attention with recommended actions

---

### ✅ Step 6: Checked Documentation and Record Maintenance

- Reviewed branch filing discipline and document retention practices:

#### 📋 Documentation Assessment Criteria

| Document Type | Assessment Focus |
|--------------|-----------------|
| **Vouchers** | Proper numbering, authorization, supporting documents attached |
| **Registers** | Cash register, clearing register, locker register — complete and up-to-date |
| **Logs** | Vault access log, key handover log, system access log — maintained consistently |
| **Control Sheets** | Daily balancing sheets, exception reports, approval logs — properly filed |

- Assessed whether important operational documents were:
  - **Properly filed** — organized by type, date, or reference number
  - **Easily traceable** — quick retrieval for audit or regulatory review
  - **Securely maintained** — restricted access, fire/water protection
  - **Authorized where necessary** — signatures, stamps, approvals present
- Highlighted weak documentation practices and record maintenance issues with practical recommendations

---

### ✅ Step 7: Verified Fixed Assets and Physical Controls

- Conducted basic physical verification of branch-level fixed assets:

#### 🔹 Asset Verification Process

| Step | Activity |
|------|----------|
| **1** | Obtained branch fixed asset register from head office records |
| **2** | Selected sample of assets for physical verification |
| **3** | Physically inspected assets: computers, printers, safes, furniture, equipment |
| **4** | Verified asset existence — is the asset actually present at branch? |
| **5** | Verified asset condition — functional, maintained, being used for branch operations |
| **6** | Verified asset tagging — bank asset tag affixed with unique identification |
| **7** | Verified asset location — matches register location; not moved without authorization |
| **8** | Documented findings with photographs and signed verification records |

#### 🔹 Physical Control Environment Observation
- Also observed physical control environment such as:
  - **Restricted area access** — vault, server room, cash counting area — controlled entry
  - **Key control** — master keys, vault keys — dual custody, log maintained
  - **Record cabinet security** — locked cabinets for sensitive documents
  - **Basic safety arrangements** — fire extinguisher, emergency exits, CCTV coverage

---

### ✅ Step 8: Assessed Operational Control Discipline

- Evaluated branch-level adherence to control practices:

#### 📋 Control Discipline Assessment

| Control Practice | Assessment Approach |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Maker-Checker Discipline** | Verified that critical transactions had independent review and approval |
| **Approval Hierarchy** | Checked that exceptions, overrides, and high-value transactions followed delegation matrix |
| **Exception Handling** | Reviewed how branches handled system errors, reversals, and manual adjustments |
| **Daily Balancing** | Verified that tellers and branch prepared daily cash and transaction balancing |
| **Supervisory Review** | Checked that branch manager reviewed key reports, exceptions, and reconciliations |

- Looked for signs of process shortcuts or weak oversight:
  - Same person performing maker and checker functions
  - Approvals given retrospectively or in bulk
  - Exceptions becoming routine without proper justification
- Identified where branch practice differed from approved process and recommended corrective actions

---

### ✅ Step 9: Documented Findings and Discussed with Branch Management

- Prepared branch-specific audit observations in structured format:

#### 📋 Finding Documentation Template

Finding #: [Sequential Number]
Branch: [Branch Name & Code]
Area: [Operational Area — e.g., Cash Management, KYC, Reconciliations]
- OBSERVATION: [Clear, factual description of what was found]
- RISK: [Impact — financial loss, compliance breach, reputational damage]
- CAUSE: [Root cause — process gap, training need, resource constraint]
- RECOMMENDATION: [Specific, actionable improvement suggestion]
- RISK RATING: 🔴 Critical | 🟠 High | 🟡 Medium | 🟢 Low


- Discussed key issues with branch management before closing fieldwork:
  - Validated facts and understanding with branch staff
  - Clarified root causes and practical constraints
  - Agreed on realistic corrective actions and timelines
- Ensured facts were validated before final reporting to avoid disputes
- Categorized findings based on significance and risk impact for prioritized remediation

---

### ✅ Step 10: Consolidated Reporting and Follow-Up Support

- Consolidated branch audit results into formal audit reporting:

#### 🔹 Consolidated Reporting Approach
- Aggregated findings across all 60+ branches to identify:
  - **Branch-specific issues** — unique to individual locations
  - **Recurring network-wide themes** — systemic weaknesses requiring group-level intervention

#### 📋 Common Recurring Themes Identified

| Theme | Typical Manifestation | Recommended Action |
|-------|---------------------|-------------------|
| **Documentation Gaps** | Missing KYC documents, unsigned vouchers, incomplete registers | Standardized checklists, branch training, periodic quality reviews |
| **Unresolved Reconciliations** | Old suspense items, uncleared inter-branch balances | Centralized tracking, escalation protocol, monthly review mandate |
| **KYC Deficiencies** | Incomplete customer files, missing PAN, outdated photographs | KYC cleanup drive, system validation controls, staff refresher training |
| **Weak Cash Controls** | Excess cash retention, single custody, delayed NRB deposits | Cash limit enforcement, surprise count program, vault access logs |
| **Incomplete Records** | Missing logs, unfiled vouchers, poor filing discipline | Document retention policy, branch filing standards, periodic audits |

- Supported follow-up on previously reported branch issues:
  - Tracked status of corrective actions from prior audits
  - Verified whether implemented actions were effective in practice
  - Escalated persistent or repeated issues to senior management
- Helped management identify whether issues were isolated or systemic across the branch network
