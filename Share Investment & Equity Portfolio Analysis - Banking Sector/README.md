## 🎯 Project Objective

Banks invest in equity shares to earn dividends and capital gains while staying within regulatory limits.  
My role was to analyze, monitor, and manage the equity investment portfolio, including valuation under IFRS 9, investment proposals, and compliance reporting to ALCO and management.

> ⚠️ *Note: All portfolio data and internal strategies are confidential. This describes only the methodology.*

## 🛠️ Step-by-Step: What I Did

---

### ✅ Step 1: Investment Policy & Regulatory Review
- Reviewed Board-approved investment policy
- Understood NRB limits (single company, sector, aggregate)
- Reviewed NEPSE trading and listing rules

---

### ✅ Step 2: Fundamental Analysis of Companies

**Financial Analysis**
- Profitability (ROE, ROA, margins)
- EPS trend and quality
- Capital strength (CAR, net worth)
- Liquidity position
- Dividend history

**Valuation**
- EPS, BVPS
- P/E, P/B ratios
- Dividend yield

**Qualitative**
- Management quality
- Sector outlook
- Governance standards

---

### ✅ Step 3: Investment Proposal Preparation

Prepared structured investment proposals:

- Financial summary (EPS, P/E, ROE, etc.)
- Investment rationale
- Risk factors
- Compliance check (NRB limits)
- Buy / Hold / Sell recommendation

---

### ✅ Step 4: Daily Market Monitoring

Maintained daily portfolio sheet:
- Quantity, cost, market price
- Unrealized gain/loss
- Market movements from NEPSE
- Corporate actions tracking

---

### ✅ Step 5: IFRS 9 Valuation

**Classification**
- FVTPL → P&L impact
- FVOCI → OCI impact

**Fair Value Measurement**
- Listed: NEPSE market price
- Unlisted: NAV / multiples / DCF

**Reporting**
- IFRS 7 disclosures
- Fair value hierarchy (Level 1–3)

---

### ✅ Step 6: Dividend Management
- Dividend income recognition
- TDS tracking
- Bonus and right share accounting
- Journal entries for income and receipts

---

### ✅ Step 7: Compliance Monitoring
Tracked NRB limits:
- Single company exposure
- Sector exposure
- Total portfolio exposure

Prepared monthly compliance reports and flagged breaches early.

---

### ✅ Step 8: Portfolio Risk Analysis
- Market risk (NEPSE movement sensitivity)
- Concentration risk
- Liquidity risk
- Valuation risk
- Regulatory risk

---

### ✅ Step 9: Buy / Hold / Sell Decisions
- Based on valuation + fundamentals + market conditions
- Used peer comparison and trend analysis
- Recommended portfolio rebalancing when needed

---

### ✅ Step 10: Reporting to Management
Prepared structured reports including:
- Portfolio summary
- Sector allocation
- Gain/loss analysis
- Compliance status
- Risk dashboard
- Investment recommendations
