## 🎯 Project Objective

Banks invest in **equity shares** of other institutions as part of their overall investment strategy — generating returns through dividends and capital appreciation while maintaining regulatory compliance. As the officer responsible for investment analysis, I managed the bank's **share investment portfolio** — conducting fundamental analysis, preparing investment proposals, performing portfolio valuation under IFRS 9, monitoring investment limits, and reporting to ALCO and management.

## 🛠️ Step-by-Step: What I Did

---

### ✅ Step 1: Understood the Investment Policy & Regulatory Framework

- Reviewed the bank's **Board-approved Investment Policy** governing equity investments:
  - Permitted investment types (listed equity, unlisted equity, subsidiaries, associates)
  - Maximum investment limits (single company, sector, aggregate)
  - Approval authority hierarchy
  - Holding period considerations
  - Exit/disposal criteria
- Understood **NRB investment regulations** for development banks:
  - Maximum equity investment as percentage of core capital
  - Single company exposure limits
  - Sector concentration limits
  - Reporting requirements to NRB
- Understood **NEPSE listing and trading rules** relevant to the bank's listed holdings

---

### ✅ Step 2: Conducted Fundamental Analysis of Investee Companies

- Performed **fundamental analysis** before any investment decision:

#### 📊 Financial Statement Analysis

| Parameter | Analysis |
|-----------|----------|
| **Profitability** | Net profit trend, ROA, ROE, NIM (for BFIs), operating profit margin |
| **Earnings Quality** | EPS trend, EPS sustainability, one-off vs. recurring income |
| **Asset Quality** | NPL ratio, provision coverage (for BFIs), asset growth quality |
| **Capital Strength** | CAR, net worth, capital buffer (for BFIs); debt-to-equity (for corporates) |
| **Liquidity** | Current ratio, cash position, LCR/NSFR (for BFIs) |
| **Dividend History** | Dividend per share, payout ratio, consistency of dividend payments |
| **Growth** | Revenue growth, asset growth, deposit/loan growth (for BFIs) |

#### 📊 Valuation Analysis

| Metric | Formula | Purpose |
|--------|---------|---------|
| **Book Value Per Share** | Net Worth / Outstanding Shares | Intrinsic value benchmark |
| **Price-to-Book (P/B)** | Market Price / Book Value | Value vs. market perception |
| **Earnings Per Share (EPS)** | Net Profit / Outstanding Shares | Earning power |
| **Price-to-Earnings (P/E)** | Market Price / EPS | Market valuation multiple |
| **Dividend Yield** | Dividend Per Share / Market Price | Income return |
| **Price-to-Earnings Growth (PEG)** | P/E / EPS Growth Rate | Growth-adjusted valuation |

#### 📊 Qualitative Assessment

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|------------|
| **Management Quality** | Board composition, management track record, governance standards |
| **Market Position** | Market share, competitive positioning, brand strength |
| **Sector Outlook** | Industry growth prospects, regulatory environment, disruption risk |
| **Corporate Governance** | Related party practices, transparency, audit quality |

### ✅ Step 5: Portfolio Valuation Under IFRS 9

- Performed **quarterly and annual portfolio valuation** under IFRS 9:

#### 📊 IFRS 9 Classification of Equity Investments

| Classification | Measurement | Gains/Losses |
|---------------|-------------|--------------|
| **FVTPL (Default)** | Fair value at each reporting date | Gains/losses recognized in Profit & Loss |
| **FVOCI (Irrevocable Election)** | Fair value at each reporting date | Gains/losses recognized in OCI (never recycled to P&L) |

- For **listed investments**:
  - Fair value = NEPSE closing market price at reporting date × quantity held
  - Level 1 fair value hierarchy — quoted price in active market
- For **unlisted investments**:
  - Fair value estimated using valuation techniques:
    - Net Asset Value (NAV) approach — book value per share
    - Comparable company multiples — P/E, P/B of similar listed companies
    - Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) — where sufficient information available
  - Level 2 or Level 3 fair value hierarchy
- Recorded **fair value adjustments**:
  - FVTPL: Debit/Credit Investment Account ↔ Fair Value Gain/Loss (P&L)
  - FVOCI: Debit/Credit Investment Account ↔ OCI Reserve (Equity)
- Prepared **IFRS 7 disclosures** for investment portfolio:
  - Classification summary
  - Fair value hierarchy analysis
  - Sensitivity analysis
  - Concentration analysis

---

### ✅ Step 6: Dividend Income Recognition & Recording

- Managed **dividend income** from equity investments:
  - Monitored **NEPSE corporate action announcements** for dividend declarations
  - Recorded dividend income when:
    - Right to receive dividend was established (ex-dividend date)
    - Amount was reliably measurable
  - Journal entry:
    ```
    Dr. Dividend Receivable
        Cr. Dividend Income (P&L)
    ```
  - Upon receipt:
    ```
    Dr. Bank
        Cr. Dividend Receivable
    ```
  - Managed **TDS on dividend** — TDS deducted by paying company on dividend distributed
  - Tracked **bonus shares** received — recorded at zero cost; adjusted cost per share for existing holding
  - Tracked **right share entitlements** — assessed whether to subscribe or let lapse based on investment merit

---

### ✅ Step 7: Investment Limit Monitoring & Compliance

- Monitored compliance with **NRB investment limits** on an ongoing basis:

#### 📋 Investment Limit Compliance Framework

| Limit Type | Computation | Compliance Check |
|------------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Single Company Limit** | Investment in one company / Core Capital | Within NRB prescribed % |
| **Sector Limit** | Total investment in one sector / Core Capital | Within NRB prescribed % |
| **Aggregate Investment Limit** | Total equity investments / Core Capital | Within NRB prescribed % |
| **Internal Policy Limits** | Board-approved limits (may be stricter than NRB) | Within Board-approved % |

- Prepared **monthly investment limit compliance report**
- Flagged any **near-breach or breach situations** to management immediately
- Ensured new investment proposals were checked against limits **before approval**

---

### ✅ Step 8: Portfolio Risk Assessment

- Assessed **investment portfolio risks** and reported to ALCO:

#### 📊 Portfolio Risk Assessment Framework

| Risk Type | Assessment Approach |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **Market Risk** | Sensitivity of portfolio value to NEPSE market movements — beta analysis |
| **Concentration Risk** | Over-exposure to single company, sector, or instrument type |
| **Liquidity Risk** | Ability to liquidate holdings — daily trading volume vs. holding size |
| **Credit Risk (for BFI investments)** | Investee company's financial health — risk of dividend cut or capital erosion |
| **Regulatory Risk** | Risk of NRB changing investment limits or rules |
| **Valuation Risk** | Risk of significant fair value decline — especially for unlisted investments |

- Prepared **portfolio risk dashboard** for ALCO review

---

### ✅ Step 9: Buy / Hold / Sell Recommendations

- Periodically reviewed the portfolio and prepared **buy / hold / sell recommendations**:

#### 📋 Investment Decision Criteria

| Action | Criteria |
|--------|----------|
| **Buy** | Undervalued stock, strong fundamentals, within investment limits, strategic fit |
| **Hold** | Fair value near intrinsic value, stable dividend, no immediate catalyst for exit |
| **Sell** | Overvalued, deteriorating fundamentals, limit breach risk, better opportunities elsewhere |

- Recommendations were based on:
  - updated fundamental analysis
  - valuation comparison (current P/E vs. historical, peer comparison)
  - dividend outlook
  - market conditions
  - portfolio rebalancing needs
  - regulatory limit considerations
- Presented recommendations to ALCO/management with supporting rationale

---

#### 📋 Portfolio Report Structure
