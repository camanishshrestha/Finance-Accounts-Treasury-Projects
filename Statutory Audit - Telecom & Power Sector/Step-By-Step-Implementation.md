## 🎯 Project Objective

Auditing non-banking entities like **telecom and power companies** presents a completely different set of challenges compared to banking audits — requiring understanding of **industry-specific revenue recognition, large-scale fixed asset management, complex contractual arrangements, and sector-specific regulatory compliance**. As a team member, I participated in the statutory audit of **NCELL (Nepal's leading private telecom operator)** and **Himal Power Ltd (a major hydropower company)** — gaining diverse audit experience beyond the banking sector.

## 🛠️ Step-by-Step: What I Did

---

### ✅ Step 1: Understanding the Business & Industry

- Studied each company's **business model, revenue streams, and operating environment**:

#### 📡 NCELL — Telecom Sector

**Key Revenue Streams:**
- Voice revenue (prepaid & postpaid)
- Data revenue (mobile internet)
- SMS revenue
- Value-added services (VAS)
- Interconnect revenue
- SIM card activation fees

**Key Cost Areas:**
- Network infrastructure and tower maintenance
- Spectrum license fees and royalties
- Interconnect charges payable to other operators
- Employee costs (large workforce)
- Marketing and distribution costs

**Regulatory Environment:**
- Nepal Telecommunications Authority (NTA) regulations
- Spectrum licensing and renewal
- Quality of Service (QoS) compliance
- Revenue-sharing obligations with government

#### ⚡ Himal Power Ltd — Hydropower Sector

**Key Revenue Streams:**
- Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) based revenue — electricity sold to Nepal Electricity Authority (NEA)
- Energy generation — seasonal patterns (monsoon vs. dry season)

**Key Cost Areas:**
- Plant operation and maintenance
- Depreciation of hydropower plant (massive fixed assets)
- Royalties to government
- Financing costs (long-term project loans)

**Regulatory Environment:**
- Nepal Electricity Regulatory Commission (NERC) guidelines
- PPA terms and tariff structure
- Licensing and concession period

### ✅ Step 3: Revenue Audit — NCELL

- Assisted in auditing NCELL's **revenue recognition** — one of the most complex areas due to automated billing systems:

#### 🔹 Prepaid Revenue
- Verified revenue recognition for prepaid recharges — revenue recognized **as airtime/data is consumed**, not when recharged
- Verified **deferred revenue balance** — unearned portion of prepaid recharges at reporting date
- Reconciled billing system reports with General Ledger revenue accounts

#### 🔹 Postpaid Revenue
- Verified monthly billing accuracy — sampled customer bills and reconciled to CBS
- Verified revenue cut-off — bills raised in correct period
- Verified accounts receivable — outstanding postpaid balances

#### 🔹 Interconnect Revenue
- Verified interconnect revenue against agreements with other operators
- Reconciled interconnect receivables and payables

#### 🔹 Analytical Procedures
- **ARPU (Average Revenue Per User) analysis** — reasonableness check
- Subscriber growth vs. revenue growth correlation
- Revenue per segment trend analysis

---

### ✅ Step 4: Revenue Audit — Himal Power Ltd

- Assisted in auditing Himal Power's **revenue recognition**:

#### 🔹 PPA-Based Revenue Verification
- Revenue was primarily derived from **Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) with NEA**
- Verified:
  - **Energy generation data** — units generated (kWh) per plant meter readings
  - **PPA tariff rate** — applicable rate as per agreement (dry season vs. wet season rates)
  - **Revenue computation** — Units generated × Applicable tariff rate
  - **NEA confirmation** — reconciled revenue with NEA purchase records
  - **Accounts receivable from NEA** — verified outstanding balance and ageing
  - **Revenue cut-off** — energy generated in correct period recorded correctly

#### 🔹 Seasonal Pattern Analysis
- Analyzed **seasonal revenue pattern** — monsoon months generating significantly higher revenue due to higher water flow

---

### ✅ Step 5: Fixed Asset Audit — Hydropower Plant

- Assisted in auditing Himal Power's **massive fixed asset base** — the hydropower plant itself:

#### 🔹 Verification Activities

| Activity | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| **Fixed Asset Register Review** | Verified completeness, accuracy, and classification of all plant components |
| **Original Cost Verification** | Verified against historical construction records and capitalization documentation |
| **Componentization Review** | Major components identified separately for depreciation (dam, penstock, turbines, generators, transmission lines, civil structures) |
| **Depreciation Computation** | Verified method (straight-line), useful life (aligned with concession period), residual value assumptions |
| **CAPEX vs. OPEX Classification** | Ensured only qualifying costs were capitalized |
| **Impairment Assessment (IAS 36)** | Assessed indicators suggesting plant value may be impaired |

- Reviewed **Capital Work-in-Progress (CWIP)** — any ongoing construction or upgrade projects

---

### ✅ Step 6: Fixed Asset Audit — NCELL (Telecom Infrastructure)

- Assisted in auditing NCELL's **telecom infrastructure assets**:

#### 🔹 Tower and Network Equipment
- Verified tower asset register — tower locations, cost, depreciation
- Reviewed tower lease arrangements — operating lease vs. finance lease treatment
- Verified network equipment — BTS, switches, routers, fiber optic cables

#### 🔹 Spectrum License (Intangible Asset)
- Verified spectrum license as **intangible asset** per IAS 38
- Checked amortization over license period
- Verified license renewal terms and contingent payments

#### 🔹 Right-of-Use Assets (IFRS 16)
- Reviewed tower site lease agreements
- Verified IFRS 16 lease liability and ROU asset computation
- Checked discount rate and lease term assumptions

---

### ✅ Step 7: Expense Verification

- Performed **substantive testing on operating expenses** for both companies:

#### 🔹 Vouching Procedure
- Selected sample expense transactions and vouched to:
  - Vendor invoices
  - Purchase orders / contracts
  - Delivery receipts / service completion certificates
  - Payment authorization records
  - Bank payment evidence

#### 🔹 Cut-off & Accrual Testing
- Verified expense **cut-off** — expenses recorded in correct accounting period
- Verified **accrued expenses** — completeness of accruals at reporting date

#### 🔹 Related Party & Government Fee Testing
- Tested **related party transactions** — identifying transactions with related parties and verifying arm's length pricing
- Checked **royalty and government fee computations**:

| Company | Government Fees Verified |
|---------|-------------------------|
| **NCELL** | Royalty to government, rural telecom development fund, spectrum fees |
| **Himal Power** | Royalty to government, water usage fees, local government contributions |

---

### ✅ Step 8: Tax Audit Procedures

- Assisted in **tax-related audit procedures**:

#### 🔹 Corporate Income Tax
- Reviewed **corporate income tax computation** — accounting profit to taxable profit reconciliation

#### 🔹 Deferred Tax (IAS 12)
- Verified **deferred tax computation**:
  - Identified temporary differences (depreciation, provisions, lease adjustments)
  - Verified DTA/DTL calculation using applicable tax rate

#### 🔹 Tax Disputes & Contingencies
- Reviewed **tax disputes and contingencies**:
  - NCELL had significant **tax disputes** with Inland Revenue Department — reviewed contingent liability assessment
  - Verified **disclosure adequacy** for tax contingencies per IAS 37

#### 🔹 TDS Compliance
- Verified **TDS compliance** — TDS deducted and remitted on vendor payments, rent, professional fees
