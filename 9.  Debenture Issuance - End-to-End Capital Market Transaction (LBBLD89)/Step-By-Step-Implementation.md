## 🎯 Project Objective

A **debenture issuance** is one of the most complex, high-stakes, and multi-dimensional transactions a bank can undertake — it requires simultaneous expertise in **financial modeling, legal compliance, regulatory filing, capital markets mechanics, and stakeholder coordination**. As the primary execution officer at Lumbini Bikas Bank Ltd, I managed the **complete end-to-end debenture issuance process** for **LBBLD89** — from preparing 10-year financial projections to getting the instrument listed on the Nepal Stock Exchange (NEPSE). This was not a routine finance task — it was a **landmark capital market transaction** that demanded precision, coordination, and deep technical expertise across multiple disciplines.

### ✅ Step 1: Understanding Debenture Issuance — Framework & Regulatory Requirements

- Conducted comprehensive study of the **regulatory framework** governing debenture issuance by BFIs in Nepal:

#### 📋 Key Regulatory Framework

| Regulation | Governing Body | Key Requirements |
|------------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Companies Act, 2063** | Government of Nepal | Debenture issuance powers, Board authorization, debenture trustee requirements |
| **Securities Act, 2063** | SEBON | Prospectus requirements, public offering rules, disclosure standards |
| **Securities Registration & Issuance Regulations** | SEBON | Registration process, prospectus format, credit rating requirement |
| **NEPSE Listing Rules** | NEPSE | Listing criteria, listing agreement, ongoing disclosure obligations |
| **NRB Unified Directives** | NRB | Capital adequacy treatment of debentures (Tier 2 capital), borrowing limits |
| **Income Tax Act, 2058** | IRD | TDS on debenture interest, tax treatment for issuer and holders |

- Mapped the **complete issuance process timeline** — identifying every step, responsible party, regulatory deadline, and dependency:
  - Phase 1: Internal Authorization & Preparation
  - Phase 2: Credit Rating
  - Phase 3: Prospectus Preparation & SEBON Filing
  - Phase 4: SEBON Approval & Public Offering
  - Phase 5: Subscription & Allotment
  - Phase 6: NEPSE Listing
  - Phase 7: Post-Listing Compliance
- This timeline mapping became the **project management backbone** for the entire issuance

### ✅ Step 2: Internal Authorization & Board Approval

- Prepared comprehensive **Board presentation and resolution** for debenture issuance approval:
  - **Purpose of issuance** — why the bank needs to issue debentures (capital augmentation, Tier 2 capital, long-term funding)
  - **Issuance structure** — amount, tenure, coupon rate (fixed/floating), redemption terms, security (secured/unsecured)
  - **Impact on capital adequacy** — how debentures strengthen Tier 2 capital and improve CAR
  - **Impact on cost of funds** — weighted average cost impact of debenture on overall funding cost
  - **Repayment capacity** — financial projections demonstrating ability to service debenture obligations
  - **Regulatory compliance** — NRB limits on Tier 2 capital inclusion (≤100% of Tier 1 within prescribed limits)
  - **Risk factors** — risks associated with the issuance and mitigation measures
- Drafted **Board resolution** authorizing:
  - Debenture issuance amount and key terms
  - Authorization of Senior Management to execute all related documents
  - Appointment of debenture trustee
  - Engagement of credit rating agency
  - Engagement of issue manager / RTS

- Prepared **NRB no-objection application**:
  - Required NRB approval / no-objection before proceeding with SEBON filing
  - Prepared application with:
    - Board resolution
    - Proposed debenture terms
    - Impact on CAR
    - Justification for issuance
  - Coordinated with NRB for approval and responded to NRB queries

### ✅ Step 3: Credit Rating Process Management

- Coordinated the **credit rating process** — a mandatory requirement for public debenture issuance in Nepal:
  - Selected **NRB-approved credit rating agency** through competitive selection
  - Executed **credit rating engagement agreement**
  - Prepared and submitted all required information to the rating agency:
    - Audited financial statements (3–5 years)
    - Detailed loan portfolio data — sector-wise, product-wise, NPL analysis
    - Capital adequacy data — CAR computation, Tier 1/Tier 2 breakdown
    - Liquidity data — CRR/SLR compliance, LCR/NSFR
    - Management structure and governance information
    - Business strategy and growth plans
    - Regulatory compliance history
  - Conducted **management presentation to rating committee** — presenting the bank's financial position, risk profile, and strategic outlook
  - Responded to **rating committee queries** with supporting data and analysis
  - Obtained **credit rating report and rating certificate**
  - Rating outcome influenced:
    - Investor confidence in the debenture
    - Pricing (coupon rate) of the debenture
    - SEBON approval ease
  - Disclosed rating in the **prospectus** as required by SEBON regulations

### ✅ Step 4: 10-Year Financial Projections Preparation

> This was one of the **most technically demanding tasks** — preparing detailed **10-year financial projections** for inclusion in the debenture prospectus

Investors and regulators use these projections to assess the bank's **ability to service debenture obligations** over the entire tenure.

#### 📊 Projection Methodology

##### Macroeconomic Assumptions
- Researched and documented macroeconomic assumptions underlying projections:
  - Nepal GDP growth rate (historical and projected)
  - Inflation rate projections
  - Interest rate outlook — NRB monetary policy direction
  - Banking sector growth trends
  - Regulatory environment assumptions (NRB directive changes, capital requirement evolution)

##### Balance Sheet Projections (10 Years)
- Projected **loan portfolio growth**:
  - Base year: current loan portfolio
  - Growth rate: assessed based on historical CAGR, strategic plan targets, market growth
  - Product mix evolution — shift toward higher-yield, lower-risk products
  - NPL ratio trajectory — improving trend with strengthened credit underwriting
- Projected **deposit mobilization**:
  - Deposit growth aligned with loan growth (maintaining CD ratio within NRB limits)
  - CASA ratio improvement trend
  - Fixed deposit tenure mix — managing maturity profile
- Projected **investment portfolio**:
  - Government securities aligned with SLR requirements
  - Equity investments within NRB-prescribed limits
- Projected **capital adequacy**:
  - Tier 1 capital — retained earnings + planned capital issuances
  - Tier 2 capital — including proposed debenture
  - Risk-weighted assets growth aligned with balance sheet growth
  - CAR trajectory — ensuring CAR remains above NRB minimum throughout 10-year period

##### Income Statement Projections (10 Years)
- Projected **interest income**:
  - Loan portfolio × projected weighted average yield
  - Yield assumptions — reflecting interest rate outlook and portfolio mix
- Projected **interest expense**:
  - Deposit base × projected weighted average deposit rate
  - Debenture interest expense — fixed coupon on outstanding debenture balance
  - Other borrowing costs
- Projected **NIM** — trend analysis showing NIM sustainability over 10 years
- Projected **non-interest income** — fee, commission, FX income growth
- Projected **operating expenses** — with cost-to-income ratio targets
- Projected **provisions** — based on NPL trajectory and ECL/NRB provisioning
- Projected **net profit after tax** — demonstrating sustained profitability

##### Cash Flow Projections (10 Years)
- Prepared **10-year cash flow projections** demonstrating:
  - **Operating cash flows** — cash generated from banking operations
  - **Investing cash flows** — investment portfolio changes, CAPEX
  - **Financing cash flows** — deposit changes, borrowings, dividend payments, debenture issuance and repayment
- Key focus: demonstrating **sufficient free cash flow** to:
  - Service debenture interest payments every period
  - Repay debenture principal at maturity
  - Continue business operations and growth
  - Maintain regulatory liquidity ratios

##### Debt Service Coverage Analysis
- Computed **Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR)** for each projection year:

##### DSCR = (Net Operating Income + Non-Cash Charges) / (Interest Expense + Principal Repayment)


- Ensured DSCR remained **above 1.0x** (preferably 1.5x+) throughout the debenture tenure
- Presented DSCR trend as key evidence of **debenture repayment capacity**

##### Sensitivity Analysis
- Prepared **multi-scenario sensitivity analysis**:

| Scenario | Assumptions | DSCR Impact |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Base Case** | Historical growth rates, stable interest rates, moderate NPL improvement | Primary projection |
| **Optimistic Case** | Higher growth, favorable rates, NPL improvement | Better DSCR |
| **Pessimistic Case** | Lower growth, rising rates, higher NPLs, NRB regulatory tightening | Worst-case DSCR |
| **Stress Case** | Severe economic downturn, significant NPL spike | Extreme stress test |

- Demonstrated that even under the **pessimistic scenario**, the bank maintained sufficient capacity to service the debenture

- The prospectus was prepared in compliance with **SEBON Securities Registration & Issuance Regulations**

#### 📋 Prospectus Structure

| Section | Content |

| **10-Year Financial Projections** | Balance sheet, income statement, cash flow projections with assumptions |
| **Debt Service Coverage** | DSCR analysis and sensitivity scenarios |

| **Legal Disclosures** | Litigation status, regulatory compliance, material contracts |

  - Debenture Prospectus (draft)

### ✅ Step 7: Debenture Trustee Arrangement

- Managed the **Debenture Trustee** appointment and agreement:
  - A debenture trustee acts as the representative of debenture holders — protecting their interests throughout the debenture tenure
  - Selected **eligible debenture trustee** (typically a commercial bank or financial institution approved by SEBON)
  - Negotiated **trustee fee** and terms of engagement
  - Prepared and executed the **Debenture Trust Deed** — a legal document defining:
    - Trustee rights and obligations
    - Bank's covenants and obligations to debenture holders
    - Events of default and trustee powers upon default
    - Security arrangements (if secured debenture)
    - Reporting obligations from issuer to trustee
    - Communication process with debenture holders
  - Ensured Trustee Deed was signed, stamped, and registered as required
  - Provided copies to **SEBON** as part of regulatory filing

---

### ✅ Step 8: RTS (Registrar to Share) Dealing & Agreement

- Managed the **RTS (Registrar to Share/Securities) engagement** — the entity responsible for managing the subscription process, allotment, and investor records:
  - Selected **SEBON-approved RTS** through competitive process
  - Negotiated **RTS service agreement** covering:
    - Subscription collection and processing
    - Application verification and validation
    - Allotment computation and lottery (if oversubscribed)
    - Refund processing for unsuccessful applicants
    - Debenture certificate issuance (or DEMAT entry)
    - Ongoing investor record maintenance
    - Interest payment processing (coupon payments to debenture holders)
    - Principal repayment at maturity
  - Executed **RTS Agreement** — legally binding contract
  - Coordinated with RTS throughout the subscription period:
    - Provided prospectus and subscription forms to RTS
    - Monitored subscription progress during the offer period
    - Received daily subscription reports from RTS
    - Coordinated allotment process post-subscription closure

---

### ✅ Step 9: Public Subscription Management

- Managed the **public subscription period** for the debenture:
  - Published **public notice** in national newspapers announcing subscription opening
  - Coordinated with all **bank branches** to act as collection points for subscription applications
  - Coordinated with **RTS** for centralized collection through their network
  - Provided **investor communication support**:
    - Responded to investor queries about debenture terms
    - Clarified tax treatment of debenture interest
    - Explained subscription process and payment methods
  - Monitored **subscription progress** daily:
    - Total applications received
    - Total amount subscribed
    - Subscription-to-offer ratio (tracking oversubscription / undersubscription)
  - At subscription closure:
    - Compiled all subscription data from branches and RTS
    - Verified total subscription against offer amount
  - **Allotment Process:**
    - If oversubscribed: coordinated lottery/pro-rata allotment through RTS
    - If fully subscribed / undersubscribed: straightforward allotment to all applicants
    - Prepared **allotment basis** approved by SEBON
    - Coordinated **refund of unsuccessful applications** through RTS and banking channels
  - Prepared **post-subscription report** for SEBON submission

### ✅ Step 10: NEPSE Listing

- Managed the **NEPSE listing process** — making the debenture tradeable on the Nepal Stock Exchange:

#### 📋 NEPSE Listing Application Package
- Prepared and submitted the NEPSE listing application with:
  - SEBON-approved prospectus
  - SEBON registration certificate
  - Allotment details — total debenture holders, debenture units allotted
  - DEMAT credit confirmation — confirmation that debentures were credited to allottees' DEMAT accounts
  - NRB no-objection
  - Board resolution
  - Listing fee payment
  - Signed NEPSE Listing Agreement

- **NEPSE Listing Agreement:**
  - Executed the **NEPSE Listing Agreement** — committing the bank to ongoing obligations:
    - Timely financial disclosures
    - Material event reporting
    - Interest payment notifications
    - Compliance with NEPSE rules and regulations
  - This agreement remained in force throughout the debenture tenure

- **DEMAT Coordination:**
  - Coordinated with **CDS and Clearing Limited (CDSC)** — Nepal's central depository
  - Ensured all allotted debentures were **credited to investors' DEMAT accounts** before listing
  - Verified DEMAT credit confirmation from CDSC

- **Trading Commencement:**
  - After NEPSE approval — debenture commenced trading on NEPSE
  - Coordinated public notice of listing and trading commencement
  - Monitored **initial trading activity** — price discovery, trading volume
