## 🎯 Project Objective

Treasury is the **nerve center of a bank** — it is where **liquidity meets strategy**. Every day, the Treasury function must balance regulatory compliance (CRR, SLR, LCR), optimize returns on surplus funds, manage funding costs, and ensure the bank never faces a **liquidity crisis**. Working as the officer **leading most treasury functions independently** at two development banks, I managed the full spectrum of treasury operations — from bidding in NRB Treasury Bill auctions to managing overnight standing facilities, interbank placements, deposit auctions, and the bank's investment portfolio.

## 🛠️ Step-by-Step: What I Did

---

### ✅ Step 1: Daily Liquidity Position Management

- Started every working day with the most critical treasury task: **assessing the bank's daily liquidity position**
- Prepared the **Daily Liquidity Position Statement** covering:
  - **Opening NRB balance** — balance maintained with Nepal Rastra Bank
  - **Expected inflows** — loan repayments, deposit collections, interbank receivables, NRB facility maturities, T-Bill/Bond maturities
  - **Expected outflows** — loan disbursements, deposit withdrawals, interbank payables, NRB facility utilizations, payment obligations
  - **Net liquidity position** — surplus or deficit
- Monitored **real-time CRR compliance**:

### CRR = NRB Balance / Total Eligible Deposit Liabilities

- Ensured CRR was maintained **above NRB prescribed minimum** at all times
- Any shortfall could trigger **NRB penalties** — so precision was non-negotiable
- Monitored **SLR compliance**:

### SLR = (Cash + NRB Balance + Government Securities + Other Approved Assets) / Total Deposit & Borrowing Liabilities

- Ensured adequate liquid asset buffer above regulatory minimum
- Prepared **rolling cash flow forecasts** (daily, weekly, monthly) to anticipate liquidity needs and deployment opportunities
- Communicated daily liquidity status to **HOD, CEO, and ALCO** for decision-making

---

### ✅ Step 2: Government Securities Management — Treasury Bills (T-Bills)

- Managed the bank's **Treasury Bill portfolio** end-to-end:

#### 🏛️ T-Bill Auction Process
- Monitored **NRB T-Bill auction calendar** — weekly auction schedule published by NRB
- Analyzed:
- **Auction size** — total amount offered by NRB
- **Market liquidity conditions** — system-wide liquidity surplus or deficit
- **Historical yield trends** — tracking cut-off rates from previous auctions
- **Bank's own liquidity position** — available surplus for T-Bill investment
- Prepared **bid recommendations** for ALCO/management approval:
- Recommended **bid amount** based on available surplus and liquidity requirements
- Recommended **bid rate (yield)** based on market analysis and return optimization
- Prepared competitive bid and non-competitive bid strategies
- Submitted bids through the **NRB auction platform** within prescribed timelines
- After auction results:
- Recorded **T-Bill allotments** — amount allotted, cut-off rate, settlement date, maturity date
- Ensured **timely settlement** — NRB debited the bank's NRB account on settlement date
- Posted **accounting entries** in CBS for T-Bill purchase

#### 📊 T-Bill Portfolio Monitoring
- Maintained a **T-Bill portfolio register** tracking:
- Purchase date, face value, discount, settlement amount
- Maturity date and days to maturity
- Yield to maturity (YTM)
- Carrying value (amortized cost using EIR method per IFRS 9)
- Performed **daily interest accrual** on T-Bill portfolio using the Effective Interest Rate (EIR) method
- Managed **T-Bill maturities** — ensuring proceeds were credited to NRB account on maturity date
- Made **reinvestment decisions** — rolling matured T-Bills into new auctions or deploying elsewhere based on liquidity needs

### ✅ Step 3: Government Securities Management — Development Bonds

- Managed the bank's **Development Bond (Government Bond) portfolio**:
- Participated in **NRB Development Bond issuances** — primary market purchases
- Evaluated bond characteristics:
  - Coupon rate
  - Tenure (typically 3–10 years)
  - Coupon payment frequency (semi-annual / annual)
  - Yield to maturity at purchase price
- Assessed **investment decision** based on:
  - Bank's SLR requirements — bonds qualify as SLR-eligible assets
  - Interest rate outlook — buying long-duration bonds in a declining rate environment for capital gains
  - Liquidity needs — bonds can be used as **collateral for NRB borrowing (SLF/Repo)**
  - Portfolio diversification — maturity profile balancing
- Managed **bond portfolio accounting** under IFRS 9:
- Classification — Amortized Cost or FVOCI depending on business model
- EIR-based interest recognition
- Fair value measurement for FVOCI bonds — unrealized gains/losses in OCI
- Coupon receipt recording and reconciliation
- Maintained **bond portfolio register** with complete details — ISIN, face value, coupon, maturity, carrying value, fair value

### ✅ Step 4: NRB Standing Facilities Management

- Managed the bank's utilization of **NRB Standing Facilities** — critical tools for overnight and short-term liquidity management:

#### 🏛️ Standing Liquidity Facility (SLF)
- **Purpose:** Short-term borrowing from NRB against eligible collateral (Government Securities) when the bank faced overnight liquidity shortage
- **Process:**
- Identified liquidity shortfall based on daily position
- Determined **SLF borrowing amount** required to meet CRR or payment obligations
- Submitted SLF request to NRB with details of **collateral pledged** (T-Bills / Bonds)
- SLF rate was set at NRB's **bank rate + prescribed premium**
- Ensured **timely repayment** on maturity (typically overnight / short-term)
- Posted **accounting entries** — SLF borrowing, interest expense, collateral pledge/release

#### 🏛️ Standing Deposit Facility (SDF)
- **Purpose:** Overnight deposit placement with NRB when the bank had excess liquidity with no better deployment opportunity
- **Process:**
- Identified **surplus liquidity** after meeting all obligations and CRR requirements
- Placed surplus funds with NRB under SDF at **NRB's deposit rate**
- SDF earned a lower return than T-Bills or interbank — used as a **last resort** for surplus parking
- Ensured funds were credited back to NRB account on SDF maturity (next business day)
- Posted accounting entries — SDF placement, interest income

#### 🏛️ Overdraft Liquidity Facility (OLF)
- **Purpose:** Emergency borrowing facility from NRB when the bank faced critical liquidity stress beyond SLF limits
- **Process:**
- OLF was utilized only in **extreme liquidity stress situations**
- Higher penalty rate than SLF — signaling NRB's expectation that this facility should be used sparingly
- Prepared OLF request with justification and collateral details
- Monitored OLF utilization and reported to ALCO/Board as a **liquidity risk indicator**
- Ensured repayment within the shortest possible timeframe

#### 📊 Standing Facility Tracking
- Maintained a **standing facility utilization register** tracking:
- Facility type (SLF / SDF / OLF)
- Utilization date and maturity date
- Amount utilized
- Interest rate applied
- Interest expense / income
- Collateral pledged (for SLF/OLF)
- Prepared **monthly standing facility utilization reports** for ALCO and management review
- Analyzed **frequency and pattern of facility utilization** — frequent SLF/OLF usage indicated structural liquidity issues requiring strategic intervention

### ✅ Step 5: NRB Deposit Auction Management

- Managed the bank's participation in **NRB Deposit Auctions** — a monetary policy instrument used by NRB to manage system-wide liquidity:
- **Deposit Collection Auction:** NRB collects deposits from banks to absorb excess liquidity from the system
- **Deposit Injection Auction:** NRB injects liquidity by placing deposits with banks during system-wide liquidity shortages
- **Process:**
- Monitored NRB deposit auction announcements and schedules
- Assessed the bank's liquidity position and determined optimal participation amount
- Prepared **auction bid** — bid amount and bid rate
- Submitted bid through NRB auction platform
- Recorded allotment results and posted accounting entries:
  - Deposit received from NRB (liability) / Deposit placed with NRB (asset)
  - Interest expense / interest income
- Managed **maturity of deposit auction placements** — ensuring timely settlement
- Analyzed deposit auction trends as indicators of **system-wide liquidity conditions** — informing treasury strategy

### ✅ Step 6: Interbank Borrowing & Lending Management

- Managed **interbank money market operations** for short-term liquidity management:

#### 💰 Interbank Lending (Surplus Deployment)
- When the bank had **short-term surplus liquidity** beyond CRR/SLR requirements and T-Bill deployment:
- Identified **counterparty banks** with borrowing needs
- Negotiated **interbank lending rate** — typically benchmarked to NRB policy rate and market conditions
- Assessed **counterparty credit risk** before placement
- Executed placement with agreed tenure (overnight / 1-week / 2-week / 1-month)
- Prepared **interbank placement confirmation letters** and maintained documentation
- Tracked maturity and ensured **timely receipt of principal and interest**

#### 💰 Interbank Borrowing (Liquidity Deficit)
- When the bank faced **short-term liquidity deficit**:
- Contacted counterparty banks for interbank borrowing
- Negotiated competitive borrowing rates
- Executed borrowing with agreed tenure
- Ensured **timely repayment** on maturity date
- Monitored **interbank borrowing exposure** against internal limits and NRB guidelines

#### 📊 Interbank Portfolio Management
- Maintained an **interbank placement/borrowing register**
- Monitored **counterparty exposure limits** — ensuring diversification across counterparty banks
- Prepared **interbank position reports** for daily liquidity monitoring and ALCO review
- Analyzed **interbank rate trends** to optimize timing of placements and borrowings

### ✅ Step 7: Share Investment & Equity Portfolio Analysis

- Managed and monitored the bank's **equity investment portfolio**:
- **Listed equity investments** — shares of other BFIs, corporate entities listed on NEPSE
- **Unlisted equity investments** — strategic investments in subsidiaries, associates, or unlisted entities
- Key activities:
- Conducted **fundamental analysis** of investee companies:
  - Financial statement analysis — profitability, solvency, liquidity ratios
  - EPS, P/E ratio, book value, dividend yield analysis
  - Sector outlook and market positioning
- Prepared **investment proposals** with buy/hold/sell recommendations for ALCO and Board
- Monitored **daily market value** of listed investments against NEPSE trading data
- Performed **portfolio valuation** under IFRS 9:
  - FVTPL investments — mark-to-market through profit or loss
  - FVOCI investments — fair value changes through OCI
  - Amortized cost investments — EIR-based measurement (for debt instruments)
- Computed **unrealized gains/losses** and reported in financial statements
- Monitored **NRB investment limits** — maximum equity investment as percentage of core capital
- Prepared **investment portfolio reports** for ALCO and Board with:
  - Portfolio composition by sector, instrument type, and risk rating
  - Return analysis — dividend income, capital gains/losses
  - Concentration risk assessment
  - Compliance with investment policy limits

### ✅ Step 8: Foreign Exchange (FX) Operations

- Managed **foreign exchange operations** within the scope of the bank's treasury:
- **FX Buying and Selling** — processing customer and interbank FX transactions
- **FX Rate Management** — setting daily buying/selling rates based on NRB reference rates and market conditions
- **FX Open Position Monitoring:**
  - Calculated the bank's **net open FX position** (long or short) across all currencies
  - Ensured open position remained within **NRB prescribed limits** to manage FX risk
  - Reported FX position to ALCO and NRB
- **Nostro Account Reconciliation:**
  - Reconciled **Nostro account balances** (bank's accounts held with foreign correspondent banks) with correspondent bank statements
  - Investigated and resolved reconciling items
- **Remittance Operations Oversight** — monitoring inward remittance inflows and their impact on liquidity
- Ensured compliance with **NRB Foreign Exchange Regulations** and **FEMA guidelines**

### ✅ Step 9: Treasury Accounting & IFRS Compliance

- Ensured all treasury transactions were accurately accounted for under **IFRS/NFRS standards**:

| Treasury Item | IFRS Treatment |
|--------------|---------------|
| **T-Bills** | Amortized Cost — EIR-based interest recognition, discount amortization |
| **Development Bonds** | Amortized Cost or FVOCI — EIR interest, fair value in OCI (if FVOCI) |
| **Interbank Placements** | Amortized Cost — interest accrual at contracted rate |
| **NRB Standing Facilities** | Financial liability — interest expense accrual |
| **Equity Investments** | FVTPL or FVOCI — mark-to-market or fair value through OCI |
| **FX Positions** | IAS 21 — functional currency translation, FX gains/losses in P&L |

- Performed **daily interest accruals** on all treasury instruments
- Prepared **monthly treasury P&L** — interest income from investments, interest expense on borrowings, FX gains/losses, investment gains/losses
- Reconciled **treasury portfolio balances** with CBS General Ledger on a daily/monthly basis
- Ensured proper **disclosure of treasury instruments** in financial statements per IFRS 7 and IFRS 9
