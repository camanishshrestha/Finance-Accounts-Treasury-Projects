# Project 2: Internal Audit — Banking, Financial Institutions & Insurance Sector

## 📌 Overview
This project covers internal audit engagements across banks, financial institutions, and insurance companies in Nepal (2019–2022). The focus was on evaluating risk management, internal controls, and regulatory compliance using Risk-Based Internal Auditing (RBIA), IIA Standards, COSO framework, and NRB/IAIS guidelines.

## 🎯 Objective
- Assess effectiveness of internal controls and risk management  
- Ensure compliance with regulatory and internal policies  
- Identify control gaps and recommend improvements  
- Strengthen governance and operational efficiency  

## 🛠️ Key Work Performed
- Performed risk-based audit planning and developed audit programs  
- Evaluated internal controls using COSO framework and walkthrough testing  
- Conducted detailed credit file reviews and loan portfolio audits  
- Verified trial balances, journal entries, and reconciliations  
- Executed branch audits (60+ branches) covering cash, KYC/AML, and operations  
- Audited treasury operations, investments, and ALCO governance  
- Reviewed insurance claims, underwriting, and reserve adequacy  
- Assessed IT controls, AML/CFT compliance, and fraud risk  
- Prepared internal audit reports with risk ratings and action plans  
- Implemented Risk-Based Internal Audit (RBIA) framework  

## 📊 Key Outcomes
- 60+ branches audited across Nepal  
- RBIA framework successfully implemented  
- Multiple audit reports delivered to management and audit committees  
- Improved control environment, compliance, and risk visibility
