## 🎯 Project Objective

Internal Audit in the banking and financial sector goes far beyond checking numbers — it is about independently evaluating whether risk is being managed, controls are working, and operations are compliant with regulatory and institutional standards. Across multiple engagements as Team Leader and Team Member, I conducted comprehensive internal audits covering credit risk, operational risk, market risk, treasury operations, branch operations, and insurance claims — adding measurable assurance value to each institution.

## 🛠️ Step-by-Step: What I Did

### ✅ Step 1: Audit Planning & Risk Assessment
- Studied the institution's business model, organizational structure, regulatory environment, and prior audit findings
- Conducted preliminary risk assessment to identify high-risk areas across:
  - Credit / Loan Portfolio
  - Treasury & Investment Operations
  - Branch Operations
  - Trade Finance & Off-Balance Sheet Items
  - Human Resources & Payroll
  - IT Systems & Access Controls
  - Insurance Claims Processing (for insurance clients)
- Prepared a structured Annual Audit Plan and Audit Universe mapping all auditable units
- Developed Risk-Based Audit Programs with tailored audit procedures for each function
- Defined audit scope, objectives, timelines, and resource allocation for each engagement
- Assigned audit team members to specific areas based on expertise and risk priority

### ✅ Step 2: Internal Control Evaluation
- Reviewed and evaluated the design and operating effectiveness of internal controls across all audited areas
- Used **COSO Internal Control Framework** as the reference for control assessment
- Performed walkthrough procedures — tracing transactions end-to-end from initiation to recording — to understand the actual control environment vs. documented policies
- Identified control deficiencies, gaps, and weaknesses and classified them by severity:
  - Critical / High Risk
  - Moderate Risk
  - Low Risk / Observation
- Tested key controls for completeness, accuracy, authorization, occurrence, and existence
- Reviewed Segregation of Duties (SoD) across critical functions such as loan disbursement, payment processing, treasury dealing, and claims settlement

### ✅ Step 3: Credit File Review & Loan Portfolio Audit
- Reviewed credit files end-to-end — from loan application and appraisal to disbursement and recovery
- Verified compliance with:
  - NRB Unified Directives on credit risk management
  - Internal credit policies and lending guidelines
  - Collateral valuation and documentation requirements
  - Loan classification and provisioning norms
- Checked for:
  - Proper credit appraisal documentation (financial analysis, CIB reports, site visits)
  - Authorized approval at appropriate levels per credit policy
  - Adequacy and perfection of collateral (mortgage registration, insurance, valuation)
  - Timely renewal of loan facilities
  - Accuracy of loan classification (Pass, Watchlist, Substandard, Doubtful, Loss)
  - Correctness of provisioning against NRB Directive requirements
- Identified instances of documentation deficiencies, irregular renewals, collateral shortfalls, and concentration risk in loan portfolios
- Prepared detailed credit audit findings with recommendations for corrective action

### ✅ Step 4: Trial Balance & Journal Entry Verification
- Obtained and analyzed the institution's Trial Balance at audit date
- Performed journal entry testing to verify:
  - **Completeness** — All transactions are recorded
  - **Accuracy** — Amounts and accounts are correctly captured
  - **Occurrence** — Transactions actually took place
  - **Existence** — Recorded assets and liabilities actually exist
  - **Authorization** — Entries are approved by authorized personnel
- Identified and investigated unusual, round-figure, or end-of-period journal entries as potential indicators of manipulation or error
- Verified inter-branch reconciliations and suspense account clearances for timeliness and completeness
- Cross-checked account balances with supporting documentation, bank reconciliations, and subsidiary ledgers

### ✅ Step 5: Branch Operational Audit (Field Visits)
- Conducted on-site branch audits across multiple locations — visiting **60+ branches across Nepal** during the Citizens Bank engagement alone
- During branch visits, examined:
  - **Cash Management** — Physical cash count, vault limits, dual custody compliance
  - **Teller Operations** — Transaction limits, authorization, overage/shortage records
  - **KYC / AML Compliance** — Customer due diligence, document completeness, transaction monitoring
  - **Locker Operations** — Access controls, dual key compliance, locker register maintenance
  - **Branch Reconciliations** — Daily closing, inter-branch accounts, suspense accounts
  - **Fixed Assets** — Physical verification, tagging, condition assessment
  - **Staff Attendance & Leave Records** — Compliance with HR policies
  - **Housekeeping of Records** — Proper filing, record retention, document security
- Documented findings branch-by-branch and consolidated into branch audit reports
- Followed up on previously reported findings to assess management action and compliance

### ✅ Step 6: Treasury & Investment Audit
- Reviewed treasury dealing operations for compliance with internal policies and NRB guidelines
- Verified:
  - **Dealing Room Controls** — Segregation of front office (dealing) and back office (settlement)
  - **Investment Authorization** — Board/ALCO approval for investment limits and instruments
  - **Investment Portfolio** — Government Securities (T-Bills, Development Bonds), interbank placements, equity investments
  - **Mark-to-Market Valuation** — Accuracy of fair value computation for investment portfolio
  - **SLF/SDF/OLF Transactions** — Compliance with NRB standing facilities guidelines
  - **FX Transactions** — Compliance with NRB forex guidelines and open position limits
- Checked dealer limits, counterparty limits, and stop-loss limits for adherence
- Reviewed ALCO minutes for evidence of proper governance over market risk

### ✅ Step 7: Insurance Claims & Risk Audit (Insurance Sector)
For Gurans Life Insurance and Reliance Life Insurance engagements, conducted specialized audit of:
- **Claims Processing** — End-to-end review of life insurance claim files from intimation to settlement
- **Claims Documentation** — Verification of completeness and authenticity of claim documents (death certificates, medical records, policy documents)
- **Claims Authorization** — Proper approval hierarchy for claim settlement amounts
- **Turnaround Time (TAT)** — Compliance with IAIS/internal standards for claim settlement timelines
- **Underwriting Files** — Review of policy issuance, premium collection, and risk classification
- **Reinsurance Arrangements** — Verification of reinsurance treaties and cession accuracy
- **Reserve Adequacy** — Review of actuarial reserves and provisioning for outstanding claims
- Identified instances of documentation gaps, delayed settlements, and process bottlenecks in claims handling

### ✅ Step 8: Operational Risk & Compliance Audit
- Reviewed key operational risk areas including:
  - **IT Access Controls** — User access management, password policies, system access logs
  - **Business Continuity & Disaster Recovery** — BCP/DRP documentation and testing evidence
  - **Fraud Risk** — Assessment of fraud indicators, whistle-blower policy, incident reporting
  - **AML/CFT Compliance** — Suspicious Transaction Reports (STRs), transaction monitoring, PEP screening
  - **Regulatory Compliance** — Adherence to NRB Directives, SEBON regulations, Insurance Board guidelines
- Verified compliance with institution's own internal policies, procedures, and Board-approved limits
- Assessed adequacy of management response to prior audit findings

### ✅ Step 9: Audit Report Preparation & Communication
- Consolidated all audit findings into structured Internal Audit Reports covering:
  - Executive Summary
  - Audit Scope and Methodology
  - Key Findings classified by Risk Rating (Critical / High / Medium / Low)
  - Root Cause Analysis for each finding
  - Management Recommendations
  - Management Response and Action Plan
- Presented audit findings to Senior Management and Audit Committees in a clear, concise manner
- Ensured audit reports maintained a constructive, improvement-oriented tone while clearly communicating risks
- Tracked management action plans and followed up on implementation status in subsequent audits

### ✅ Step 10: Risk-Based Internal Audit Implementation — Himalayan Bank Ltd
> A landmark engagement: Implemented the **First Risk-Based Internal Audit (RBIA) framework** for Himalayan Bank Limited

- Transitioned the bank's internal audit approach from a compliance/checklist-based audit to a Risk-Based Internal Audit (RBIA) methodology
- Key activities included:
  - Developing the **Audit Universe** — comprehensive mapping of all auditable entities and processes
  - Building a **Risk and Control Matrix (RCM)** for each auditable area
  - Designing risk scoring methodology to prioritize audit resources toward highest-risk areas
  - Establishing audit cycle frequency based on risk ratings
  - Aligning the RBIA framework with **IIA (Institute of Internal Auditors) Professional Standards**
  - Training the internal audit team on RBIA concepts and methodology
