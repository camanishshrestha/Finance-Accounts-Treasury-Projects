## 🎯 Project Objective

Hospital procurement is a high-stakes, high-value function — involving the purchase of medical equipment, pharmaceuticals, surgical supplies, infrastructure services, and IT systems where incorrect procurement decisions can directly impact **patient safety, service quality, and financial efficiency**. As Team Leader, I provided procurement assistance to OM Hospital — evaluating procurement bids, prequalification documents, and vendor quotations to ensure the hospital obtained **best value for money** with transparency, fairness, and compliance.

### ✅ Step 1: Understanding Hospital Procurement Framework

- Studied the procurement framework applicable to the hospital:

#### 📋 Procurement Regulatory Framework

| Regulation | Applicability |
|------------|--------------|
| **Public Procurement Act (PPA)** | Applicable if hospital receives government funding or operates under public sector rules |
| **Public Procurement Regulations (PPR)** | Detailed procedural rules under PPA |
| **Donor Procurement Guidelines** | If procurement funded by specific donors — donor rules may override or supplement |
| **Hospital Internal Procurement Policy** | Hospital's own Board-approved procurement procedures |

#### 📋 Procurement Categories in Healthcare

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|

### ✅ Step 2: Prequalification Bid Evaluation

- Evaluated prequalification bids — the first stage of procurement for high-value purchases:

#### 📋 Prequalification Assessment Criteria

| Criteria | Evaluation Performed |
|----------|---------------------|
| **Legal Standing** | Company registration certificate, PAN/VAT registration, tax clearance certificate — valid and current |
| **Financial Capacity** | Audited financial statements — reviewed for turnover, profitability, liquidity, net worth — ensuring vendor has financial strength to deliver |
| **Technical Capacity** | Technical qualifications, certifications, equipment, qualified personnel — assessed against procurement requirements |
| **Experience** | Past experience in similar procurement — number of similar contracts completed, contract values, client references |
| **Client References** | Contacted references to verify vendor's past performance — delivery quality, timeliness, after-sales support |
| **Manufacturing Authorization** | For medical equipment — authorized dealership/distributorship certificate from manufacturer |
| **ISO Certification** | ISO 9001, ISO 13485 (medical devices), ISO 14001 — quality management system certification |
| **Litigation History** | Declaration of no ongoing litigation or blacklisting from any government or institutional procurement |

#### 🔹 Prequalification Evaluation Report:
- Listed all applicants
- Scored each applicant against evaluation criteria
- Identified qualified vendors meeting minimum threshold
- Identified disqualified vendors with documented reasons
- Recommended shortlist of prequalified vendors for the next stage (bidding)

---

### ✅ Step 3: Quotation Collection & Examination

- For procurements requiring quotation-based evaluation (below formal bidding threshold):

#### 📋 Quotation Examination Process

| Step | Activity |
|------|----------|
| **1** | Verified minimum quotations received — typically 3+ written quotations from different vendors |
| **2** | Checked quotation completeness — vendor details, item descriptions, specifications, unit price, total price, delivery terms, payment terms, validity period |
| **3** | Verified quotation authenticity — signed, stamped, on vendor letterhead, dated |
| **4** | Checked for identical quotations — suspicion of collusion if quotations are suspiciously similar |
| **5** | Verified specifications compliance — each quotation meets technical specifications in the purchase request |
| **6** | Checked vendor eligibility — registered, tax-compliant, not blacklisted |
| **7** | Verified price reasonableness — compared against market prices, historical purchase prices, budget estimates |

### ✅ Step 5: Medical Equipment Procurement — Specialized Evaluation

- For medical equipment procurement, performed additional specialized evaluation:

#### 📋 Medical Equipment Evaluation Criteria

| Criteria | Evaluation Details |
|----------|-------------------|
| **FDA/CE Marking** | Equipment has FDA (US) or CE (EU) marking — international safety and quality certification |
| **Technical Specifications** | Detailed technical spec comparison against hospital's requirement — resolution, capacity, throughput, accuracy |
| **Brand & Model** | Reputable manufacturer; model suitability for hospital's patient volume and clinical requirements |
| **Installation Requirements** | Space, electrical, plumbing, environmental requirements — compatibility with hospital infrastructure |
| **Training Provision** | Vendor provides operator training for hospital staff — included or additional cost |
| **Warranty & AMC** | Warranty period, scope of coverage, Annual Maintenance Contract (AMC) terms and cost |
| **Spare Parts Availability** | Local availability of spare parts; lead time for parts procurement |
| **Service Support** | Local service engineer availability; response time commitment |
| **Consumables** | Ongoing consumable requirements and costs (for equipment requiring consumables) |
| **Technology Lifecycle** | Equipment technology currency — not outdated/end-of-life technology |
| **Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)** | Equipment cost + installation + training + AMC + consumables over expected useful life |

> **TCO analysis was critical** — the cheapest equipment upfront may be the most expensive over its lifetime due to high consumable costs, frequent breakdowns, or expensive AMC

### ✅ Step 6: Infrastructure & Service Procurement Evaluation

- For infrastructure and service contracts, evaluated:

#### 📋 Infrastructure Bid Evaluation

| Criteria | Evaluation Details |
|----------|-------------------|
| **Bill of Quantities (BOQ)** | Verified BOQ completeness — all items included, quantities reasonable, unit rates realistic |
| **Rate Analysis** | Checked unit rates against district rates / government-published rates |
| **Contractor Experience** | Past hospital/healthcare construction experience, similar project references |
| **Contractor Financial Capacity** | Ability to finance construction during payment delays |
| **Timeline** | Realistic construction timeline with milestone-based progress schedule |
| **Quality Standards** | Compliance with building codes, healthcare facility standards |
| **Performance Bond** | Contractor provides performance guarantee / bank guarantee |

#### 📋 Service Contract Evaluation

| Criteria | Evaluation Details |
|----------|-------------------|
| **Service Scope** | Clear definition of services — cleaning, security, catering, waste management |
| **Staffing Plan** | Number of staff deployed, qualifications, supervision structure |
| **Service Standards** | Service level agreements (SLAs) — response time, quality metrics |
| **Pricing Structure** | Monthly fixed fee vs. per-unit pricing; escalation clauses |
| **Contract Duration** | Contract period, renewal terms, termination clauses |
| **Compliance** | Labor law compliance, minimum wage, safety requirements |

---

### ✅ Step 7: Conflict of Interest & Integrity Verification

- Verified procurement integrity at every stage:

#### 📋 Integrity Verification Checklist

| Check | Verification |
|-------|-------------|
| **Conflict of Interest** | Evaluation committee members declared no personal/financial relationship with any bidder |
| **Bid Confidentiality** | Bids kept sealed until official opening; no information leakage |
| **Collusion Check** | Analyzed quotations for signs of collusion — identical formatting, sequential pricing, similar errors |
| **Vendor Blacklist** | Verified vendors not on any government or institutional blacklist |
| **Market Price Verification** | Cross-checked quoted prices against independent market price data |
| **Post-Evaluation Contact** | No unauthorized contact between evaluation committee and vendors during evaluation |

---

### ✅ Step 8: Evaluation Report & Recommendation

- Prepared the final procurement evaluation report for hospital management:

#### 📋 Evaluation Report Structure

| Section | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Background** | Procurement purpose, requirement description, budget allocation |
| **Process Summary** | Procurement method used, number of vendors invited/responded, evaluation committee composition |
| **Evaluation Criteria** | Criteria and weightages used for evaluation |
| **Bid Summary** | Summary of all bids received — vendors, prices, key terms |
| **Comparative Analysis** | Detailed comparison table with scoring |
| **Technical Evaluation** | Technical compliance assessment — pass/fail for each vendor |
| **Financial Evaluation** | Price comparison, TCO analysis, value for money assessment |
| **Recommendation** | Recommended vendor with clear justification |
| **Risk Assessment** | Risks associated with recommended vendor and mitigation measures |
| **Committee Signatures** | All evaluation committee members signed the report |

- Report was submitted to hospital management / procurement committee for final approval
- Ensured the entire process was **transparent, documented, and auditable**
