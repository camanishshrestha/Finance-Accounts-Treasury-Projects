## 🎯 Project Objective

FMCG companies rely heavily on trade discounts, dealer incentives, and promotional schemes to drive sales. These also create a high fraud and leakage risk if not properly controlled.

My role was to audit discount and trade claims to ensure they were **valid, properly supported, correctly calculated, and linked to actual sales and stock movement**.

> ⚠️ Note: All financial figures, distributor data, and internal findings remain confidential. Only audit methods are disclosed.

## 🛠️ Step-by-Step: What I Did

---

### ✅ Step 1: Understanding Discount Scheme Structure

- Reviewed promotional and discount schemes
- Understood claim types:
  - Trade discounts
  - Dealer incentives
  - Volume rebates
  - Promotional support schemes
- Studied approval rules and eligibility conditions

---

### ✅ Step 2: Claim File Review

- Checked claim documents for completeness:
  - Claim forms
  - Sales invoices
  - Delivery records
  - Scheme approvals
  - Distributor confirmations
- Verified claims were within approved scheme period

---

### ✅ Step 3: Sales Linkage Verification

- Matched claims with sales records
- Verified eligibility based on:
  - Product type
  - Customer eligibility
  - Time period
  - Volume thresholds
- Detected duplicate or unsupported claims

---

### ✅ Step 4: Stock Inspection

- Conducted physical stock verification at distributor/warehouse level
- Cross-checked:
  - Physical stock vs inventory records
  - Sales dispatch records vs claim volumes
- Identified mismatches between stock movement and claims

---

### ✅ Step 5: Authorization Testing

- Verified approval hierarchy (delegation of authority)
- Checked maker-checker controls
- Confirmed management approval for exceptions
- Identified weak or missing approval trails

---

### ✅ Step 6: Claim Recalculation

- Recomputed claim values based on scheme rules:
  - Discount rate
  - Quantity eligibility
  - Time validity
- Verified arithmetic accuracy of claims
- Ensured alignment with approved commercial terms

---

### ✅ Step 7: Risk & Red Flag Analysis

- Identified suspicious patterns:
  - Repeated distributor claims
  - Round figure claims
  - Missing documentation
  - End-period sales spikes
  - Claims without sales backing
- Flagged high-risk exceptions

---

### ✅ Step 8: Audit Observations & Reporting

- Documented findings using structured format:
  - Observation
  - Risk
  - Root cause
  - Recommendation
- Grouped issues by severity
- Suggested improvements in:
  - Scheme design
  - Approval controls
  - Documentation discipline
  - Reconciliation processes
