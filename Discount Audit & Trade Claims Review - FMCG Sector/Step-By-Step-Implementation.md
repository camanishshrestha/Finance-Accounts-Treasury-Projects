## 🎯 Project Objective

In the FMCG sector, sales growth is often driven by **trade discounts, promotional schemes, dealer incentives, and distribution claims**. These schemes can become a major control risk if claims are processed without proper validation. My role was to audit discount and trade claim files to verify that **claims were genuine, properly supported, authorized, and linked to actual sales/stock movement**.

> ⚠️ *Note: All claim values, distributor data, pricing structures, and internal findings remain confidential. Only the audit approach and my responsibilities are described here.*

## 🛠️ Step-by-Step: What I Did

---

### ✅ Step 1: Understood the Discount Scheme Structure

- Reviewed the company's **discount and promotional scheme framework**
- Understood different types of claims such as:

#### 📋 Types of Trade Claims Reviewed

| Claim Type | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| **Trade Discounts** | Volume-based or value-based discounts offered to distributors/dealers |
| **Dealer Incentives** | Performance-based incentives for achieving sales targets |
| **Volume-Based Rebates** | Retroactive rebates based on cumulative purchase volumes |
| **Promotional Support Claims** | Claims for marketing activities, displays, sampling, events |
| **Secondary Scheme Claims** | Claims linked to secondary sales (distributor to retailer) |

- Identified the **approval process**, eligibility criteria, and documents required for each claim type

---

### ✅ Step 2: Reviewed Claim Files

- Examined submitted **discount claim files**
- Verified whether each file contained the required supporting documents:

#### 📋 Claim File Documentation Checklist

| Document | Verification Performed |
|----------|----------------------|
| **Claim Form** | Properly filled, dated, signed by claimant |
| **Sales Invoices** | Invoices supporting claimed sales volume/value |
| **Delivery Records** | Proof of goods delivered to eligible customers |
| **Scheme Approval Document** | Reference to approved scheme with validity period |
| **Dealer/Distributor Support** | Acknowledgment or confirmation from downstream channel |
| **Authorization** | Approval from responsible company officials per delegation matrix |

- Checked whether the claim was raised under an **approved scheme period**

---

### ✅ Step 3: Verified Sales Linkage

- Matched claim amounts with **actual sales records**:

#### 🔹 Sales Linkage Verification Steps
- Verified whether the quantity sold met the scheme threshold
- Checked that discounts were applied only to:
  - **Eligible products** — as per scheme terms
  - **Eligible customers** — authorized distributors/dealers
  - **Eligible periods** — within scheme validity dates
- Identified any cases where claims appeared to be:
  - **Duplicated** — same sales supporting multiple claims
  - **Unsupported** — no corresponding sales evidence
  - **Outside approved scheme terms** — ineligible products/customers/periods

---

### ✅ Step 4: Performed Stock Inspection

- Conducted **site visits and stock inspection** to validate claim authenticity:

#### 🔹 Stock Inspection Activities

| Activity | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| **Physical Stock Count** | Verified actual stock position at distributor/dealer premises |
| **Inventory Record Review** | Compared physical stock with distributor's inventory records |
| **Dispatch Record Verification** | Checked goods receipt notes against company dispatch records |
| **Sales Record Reconciliation** | Compared distributor sales records with claimed volumes |

#### 🔹 Key Checks Performed
- Verified whether stock availability and movement were consistent with the claim
- Checked for abnormal differences between claim volume and actual stock movement
- Identified potential **stock parking** or **channel stuffing** indicators

---

### ✅ Step 5: Tested Authorization Controls

- Reviewed whether discount claims were approved according to the **delegation of authority**:

#### 🔹 Authorization Control Testing

| Control | Testing Approach |
|---------|-----------------|
| **Approval Hierarchy** | Verified claims approved at appropriate level per authority matrix |
| **Maker-Checker** | Confirmed segregation between claim preparer and approver |
| **Exception Handling** | Checked special discounts/exceptions had proper management approval |
| **Approval Trail** | Verified complete documentation of approval workflow |

- Noted cases where approval trail was weak or incomplete

---

### ✅ Step 6: Checked Claim Accuracy

- Recalculated the claim amount based on the approved scheme logic:

#### 🔹 Claim Accuracy Verification

| Check | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Rate of Discount** | Verified correct discount rate applied per scheme terms |
| **Quantity Eligibility** | Confirmed claimed quantity met minimum threshold requirements |
| **Period Eligibility** | Verified sales/claims fell within scheme validity period |
| **Mathematical Accuracy** | Recalculated claim amount: Quantity × Rate = Total Claim |
| **Commercial Terms Alignment** | Ensured final claim matched approved commercial agreements |

---

### ✅ Step 7: Looked for Red Flags

- Reviewed claims for common risk indicators:

#### 🚩 Red Flag Indicators Monitored

| Red Flag | Potential Risk |
|----------|---------------|
| **Repeated claims from same distributor** | Potential scheme abuse or collusion |
| **Round-figure claims** | Possible estimation rather than actual calculation |
| **Missing documents** | Weak documentation control or intentional omission |
| **Unusual volume spikes near period-end** | Possible channel stuffing to meet targets |
| **Claims without corresponding sales support** | Unsupported or fraudulent claims |
| **Claims exceeding scheme caps** | Non-compliance with scheme limits |

- Flagged exceptions for management attention and further investigation

---

### ✅ Step 8: Prepared Audit Observations

- Documented findings in a structured format:

#### 📋 Audit Observation Template
- Observation: [Clear description of the finding]
- Risk: [Impact on financials, compliance, or operations]
- Cause: [Root cause — process gap, control weakness, human error]
- Recommendation: [Specific, actionable improvement suggestion]


#### 🔹 Findings Categorization
- Categorized findings based on significance:
  - 🔴 **High** — Material financial impact or control failure
  - 🟡 **Medium** — Moderate risk requiring management attention
  - 🟢 **Low** — Minor observations for process improvement

#### 🔹 Recommendation Areas
- Shared practical recommendations to strengthen:
  - **Claim documentation** — standardized checklists, mandatory attachments
  - **Approval process** — clear delegation matrix, system-based workflows
  - **Scheme monitoring** — real-time tracking, exception reporting
  - **Reconciliation controls** — periodic claim-to-sales reconciliation
