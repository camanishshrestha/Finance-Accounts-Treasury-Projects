## 🎯 Project Objective

A debenture prospectus requires **10-year financial projections** that demonstrate the bank's ability to **service interest payments and repay principal** over the entire debenture tenure. These projections are reviewed by **regulators (SEBON), investors, credit rating agencies, and auditors** — so they must be technically rigorous, internally consistent, and supported by defensible assumptions. I was the **primary preparer** of the complete 10-year financial model for the LBBLD89 debenture issuance.

> ⚠️ *Note: All projection figures, assumptions, and institution-specific financial data remain confidential. Only the modeling methodology and approach are described here.*

## 🛠️ Step-by-Step: What I Did

---

### ✅ Step 1: Established the Projection Foundation

- Gathered the **base year audited financial statements** as the starting point
- Collected **5-year historical data** to analyze growth patterns and trends
- Reviewed:
  - historical balance sheet CAGR (assets, loans, deposits)
  - income statement trends (NII, non-interest income, expenses, provisions, net profit)
  - key ratio trajectories (ROA, ROE, NIM, cost-to-income, NPL, CAR)
- Established the **base year position** from which all projections would be built forward

---

### ✅ Step 2: Defined Macroeconomic Assumptions

- Researched and documented **macroeconomic assumptions** underlying the projections:

#### 📋 Macroeconomic Assumptions Framework

| Assumption | Source | Approach |
|------------|--------|----------|
| **GDP Growth** | NRB Economic Survey, World Bank | Historical trend + forward outlook |
| **Inflation Rate** | NRB data, IMF projections | Moving average with moderate forecast |
| **Credit Growth** | NRB monetary policy, banking sector data | Sector growth rate as reference |
| **Interest Rate Outlook** | NRB policy rate trend, monetary policy direction | Stable / rising / declining scenarios |
| **Deposit Growth** | Banking sector trends, demographic factors | Aligned with credit growth assumption |
| **Regulatory Changes** | NRB directive trajectory | Conservative assumption — no major relaxation |

- Documented every assumption with **source reference and rationale**
- These assumptions were subject to **regulatory and auditor scrutiny** — so defensibility was critical

---

### ✅ Step 3: Projected the Balance Sheet (10 Years)

- Built year-by-year **balance sheet projections** for 10 years:

#### 📊 Asset Side Projections

| Asset Component | Projection Method |
|----------------|------------------|
| **Loan Portfolio** | Base year + projected growth rate (aligned with strategic plan and market growth); product mix assumptions |
| **Government Securities** | Projected to maintain SLR compliance; aligned with surplus deployment strategy |
| **Interbank Placements** | Projected based on average surplus and deployment pattern |
| **Fixed Assets** | Existing assets + planned CAPEX – depreciation |
| **Cash & NRB Balance** | Projected to maintain CRR compliance based on deposit growth |
| **Other Assets** | Interest receivable, prepaid, DTA — projected based on balance sheet drivers |

#### 📊 Liability Side Projections

| Liability Component | Projection Method |
|---------------------|------------------|
| **Deposits** | Base year + projected deposit growth; product mix (CASA vs. FD); maturity profile |
| **Borrowings** | Interbank, NRB facilities — projected based on funding gap |
| **Debenture** | LBBLD89 amount added in issuance year; outstanding throughout tenure; repaid at maturity |
| **Other Liabilities** | Interest payable, provisions, accruals — projected based on balance sheet drivers |

#### 📊 Equity Projections

| Component | Projection Method |
|-----------|------------------|
| **Share Capital** | Existing + planned capital issuance (if any) |
| **Retained Earnings** | Opening + projected net profit – projected dividend |
| **Reserves** | Statutory reserve transfers per regulatory requirements |
| **OCI Reserves** | Projected actuarial and fair value movements |

- Ensured **balance sheet balanced** every year: Assets = Liabilities + Equity
- Verified **CAR compliance** in every projection year — critical for regulatory credibility

---

### ✅ Step 4: Projected the Income Statement (10 Years)

- Built year-by-year **income statement projections**:

#### 📊 Interest Income Projection

| Component | Method |
|-----------|--------|
| **Loan Interest** | Average loan portfolio × projected weighted average yield |
| **Investment Interest** | T-Bill/Bond portfolio × projected yield |
| **Interbank Interest** | Average placement × projected rate |
| **Other Interest** | Staff loan interest, penal interest — estimated based on trends |

- Yield assumptions reflected:
  - current portfolio rates
  - expected rate changes (NRB monetary policy outlook)
  - portfolio mix shift (higher/lower yield products)

#### 📊 Interest Expense Projection

| Component | Method |
|-----------|--------|
| **Deposit Interest** | Average deposit base × projected weighted average cost |
| **Debenture Interest** | LBBLD89 outstanding × fixed coupon rate |
| **Borrowing Interest** | Projected borrowings × estimated rate |

- Cost assumptions reflected:
  - current deposit rate structure
  - projected CASA ratio improvement (reducing cost)
  - fixed debenture coupon (known and certain)

#### 📊 Other Income & Expense Projection

| Component | Method |
|-----------|--------|
| **Fee/Commission Income** | Historical growth trend; aligned with business volume growth |
| **FX/Trading Income** | Conservative trend-based projection |
| **Staff Costs** | Headcount growth + salary increment assumption |
| **Administrative Expenses** | Inflation-adjusted growth on existing base |
| **Depreciation** | Based on existing + projected fixed asset base |
| **Provisions** | Based on projected NPL trajectory × provisioning rate |
| **Tax** | Projected taxable profit × corporate tax rate (30% for BFIs) |

- Computed **projected Net Profit After Tax (NPAT)** for each year

---

### ✅ Step 5: Projected Cash Flow Statement (10 Years)

- Built **10-year cash flow projections** using indirect method:

#### 📊 Cash Flow Components

| Section | Key Items |
|---------|-----------|
| **Operating Activities** | Net profit + depreciation + provision charges – working capital changes |
| **Investing Activities** | Investment portfolio changes, CAPEX, fixed asset purchases/disposals |
| **Financing Activities** | Deposit changes, borrowing changes, debenture issuance/repayment, dividend payments |
| **Net Cash Flow** | Operating + Investing + Financing = Net increase/decrease in cash |
| **Closing Cash** | Opening cash + Net cash flow = Closing cash position |

- Verified that closing cash position aligned with **projected CRR requirement**
- Ensured cash flow projections were **internally consistent** with balance sheet and income statement projections

---

### ✅ Step 6: Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR) Analysis

- Computed **DSCR for each of the 10 projection years** — the most critical metric for debenture investors and regulators:

- DSCR = (Net Profit After Tax + Depreciation + Interest on Debenture) / (Interest on Debenture + Principal Repayment*)
- Principal repayment applies only at maturity year (bullet repayment) or annually if amortizing structure

### ✅ Step 7: Capital Adequacy Projection

- Projected **Capital Adequacy Ratio (CAR)** for each year to ensure regulatory compliance:
CAR = Total Capital Fund / Total Risk-Weighted Assets
Where:
Total Capital Fund = Tier 1 (Core Capital) + Tier 2 (Supplementary Capital)
Tier 2 includes LBBLD89 debenture (within NRB limits)
RWA grows aligned with balance sheet growth


- Verified that CAR remained **above NRB minimum** throughout the 10-year period
- Showed the **positive impact of debenture issuance** on CAR (strengthening Tier 2 capital)

### ✅ Step 9: Sensitivity Analysis — Multi-Scenario Modeling

- Prepared **sensitivity analysis** testing projection resilience under different conditions:

#### 📊 Scenario Framework

| Scenario | Key Assumptions | Purpose |
|----------|----------------|---------|
| **Base Case** | Most likely assumptions — moderate growth, stable rates, gradual NPL improvement | Primary projection for prospectus |
| **Optimistic Case** | Higher growth, favorable rates, faster NPL improvement, higher NIM | Best-case capacity demonstration |
| **Pessimistic Case** | Lower growth, rising cost of funds, higher NPLs, compressed NIM | Stress resilience test |
| **Severe Stress** | Significant economic downturn, NPL spike, capital pressure, liquidity stress | Extreme downside test |
