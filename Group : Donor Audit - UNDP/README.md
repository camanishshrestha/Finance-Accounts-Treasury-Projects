## 🎯 Project Objective

UNDP donor audits focus on **accountability rather than profitability**. The key objective is to ensure donor funds are used **efficiently, transparently, and in compliance with grant agreements**.

As Team Leader/Team Member, I audited UNDP-funded projects to verify fund utilization, procurement compliance, and implementing partner controls.

> ⚠️ *Note: All project-specific financial data and findings remain confidential. Only audit methodology is disclosed.*

## 🛠️ Step-by-Step: What I Did

---

### ✅ Step 1: Understanding UNDP Audit Framework

Studied UNDP governance structure:

| Component | Description |
|---|---|
| Donor | Funding agency providing project resources |
| UNDP Country Office | Oversees implementation |
| Implementing Partner | Executes project activities |
| ProDoc | Defines objectives, budget, and scope |
| AWP | Annual activity and budget plan |
| FACE Form | Expenditure reporting & fund request document |

Key standards:
- UNDP POPP  
- HACT Framework  
- IPSAS  
- UNDP Procurement Guidelines  

---

### ✅ Step 2: Audit Planning & Risk Assessment

- Reviewed ProDoc and AWP
- Defined audit scope and period
- Identified risks:
  - Misaligned expenditure
  - Procurement non-compliance
  - Unsupported costs
  - Asset mismanagement
  - Weak internal controls

---

### ✅ Step 3: Budget vs Actual Verification

Compared planned vs actual expenditure:

- Staff costs
- Travel
- Training
- Equipment
- Consultancy
- Contractual services

Identified:
- Over/under spending
- Unauthorized reallocations
- Budget deviations without approval

---

### ✅ Step 4: Expenditure Vouching

Verified sample transactions:

Checked:
- Authorization
- Supporting documents (invoice, PO, GRN, certificates)
- Eligibility under UNDP rules
- Accuracy in accounting records
- FACE form consistency

Detected:
- Unsupported expenses
- Ineligible costs
- Duplicate payments
- Misclassification errors

---

### ✅ Step 5: Staff Cost Audit

Verified:
- Contracts and payroll
- Salary payments via bank
- Time allocation across projects
- Compliance with UNDP salary scales
- Ghost employee risk

---

### ✅ Step 6: Travel Verification

Checked:
- Travel approvals
- Purpose alignment
- Per diem compliance
- Boarding passes & hotel bills
- No duplicate claims

---

### ✅ Step 7: Procurement Audit

Verified procurement thresholds:

- Quotations (minimum 3 where required)
- Competitive bidding compliance
- Evaluation fairness
- Contract issuance before delivery
- Vendor selection integrity

Detected:
- Split procurement
- Single sourcing without justification
- Missing quotations
- Vendor bias risks

---

### ✅ Step 8: Asset Verification

Performed physical inspection:

- Asset existence
- Condition and usage
- Asset tagging compliance
- Location verification
- Custodian identification

Findings:
- Missing assets
- Poor maintenance
- Non-project usage
- Unregistered assets

---

### ✅ Step 9: FACE Form Verification

Reconciled:
- FACE submission vs accounting records
- Bank balances vs reported balances
- Supporting documentation

Ensured:
- Accuracy
- Completeness
- Timely submission

---

### ✅ Step 10: Audit Reporting

Prepared donor audit report including:
- Auditor’s opinion
- Statement of expenditure
- Findings (ineligible/unsupported costs)
- Procurement & control issues
- Recommendations
- Management responses
