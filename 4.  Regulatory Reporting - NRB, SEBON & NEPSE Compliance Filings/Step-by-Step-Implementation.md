## 🎯 Project Objective

Banks and Financial Institutions (BFIs) in Nepal operate under one of the most **heavily regulated environments** — with multiple regulatory bodies demanding frequent, accurate, and timely submissions of financial and supervisory data. As the **Checker** in the NRB SIS Portal and the second-in-command of the Finance Department, I was responsible for ensuring the bank's **complete and accurate regulatory compliance** across all reporting obligations — a function where even a single error or missed deadline can result in **regulatory penalties, reputational damage, and supervisory action**.

### ✅ Step 2: NRB SIS Portal — Checker Role

- Acted as the designated **Checker** in the **NRB Supervisory Information System (SIS) Portal** — the primary digital platform for regulatory submissions to Nepal Rastra Bank
- **Maker-Checker workflow:**
  - **Maker** (team member) prepared and entered data into the SIS Portal
  - **Checker** (my role) independently verified data accuracy, completeness, and consistency before final submission
- Key verification activities as Checker:
  - **Cross-verified SIS Portal data** against source documents — General Ledger, Trial Balance, CBS reports, subsidiary records
  - **Validated mathematical accuracy** — checking totals, sub-totals, and computed fields
  - **Ensured inter-report consistency** — data reported in one return must reconcile with data in related returns
  - **Identified and corrected discrepancies** before submission — preventing regulatory queries
  - **Approved and submitted** returns through the SIS Portal after satisfactory verification
- Also submitted returns through the **NRB Old Reporting Portal** for legacy reporting requirements that had not yet migrated to SIS

### ✅ Step 3: Capital Adequacy Reporting (Basel Framework)

- Prepared and submitted **Capital Adequacy Returns** to NRB under the Basel II/III framework:

#### 🔹 Capital Fund Computation:
- **Tier 1 Capital (Core Capital)** — Paid-up capital, share premium, retained earnings, general reserves
- **Tier 2 Capital (Supplementary Capital)** — Subordinated term debt, revaluation reserves, general loan loss provisions (within limits)
- **Regulatory adjustments and deductions** — goodwill, deferred tax assets, investments in subsidiaries

#### 🔹 Risk-Weighted Assets (RWA) Computation:
- **Credit Risk RWA** — Risk-weighted on-balance sheet exposures and off-balance sheet equivalents using standardized approach
- **Market Risk RWA** — Risk charge for trading book positions, FX open positions
- **Operational Risk RWA** — Basic Indicator Approach (BIA) computation

#### 🔹 Capital Adequacy Ratio (CAR) Calculation:
```
CAR = (Total Capital Fund / Total RWA) × 100
```
- Ensured CAR exceeded **NRB minimum requirements** (Tier 1 ≥ 7%, Total CAR ≥ 11% for development banks)

#### 🔹 Capital Buffer Monitoring:
- Capital Conservation Buffer (CCB)
- Countercyclical Buffer (where applicable)

- Prepared **stress testing scenarios** to assess capital adequacy under adverse conditions
- Monitored CAR on a **monthly basis** and reported to ALCO and Senior Management

---

### ✅ Step 4: Liquidity Reporting — CRR & SLR Compliance

- Prepared and submitted **daily and periodic liquidity reports** to NRB:

#### 🔹 Cash Reserve Ratio (CRR):
- Calculated CRR as percentage of total deposit liabilities maintained as balance with NRB
- Monitored **daily CRR position** to ensure compliance with NRB prescribed minimum ratio
- Investigated and reported any **CRR shortfalls** — calculating potential penalty implications

#### 🔹 Statutory Liquidity Ratio (SLR):
- Calculated SLR including cash, NRB balance, government securities, and other approved liquid assets as percentage of total deposit and borrowing liabilities
- Ensured SLR was maintained **above NRB prescribed minimum** at all times

#### 🔹 Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR):
- Computed High-Quality Liquid Assets (HQLA) — Level 1 and Level 2 assets
- Calculated net cash outflows under 30-day stress scenario
```
LCR = (HQLA / Net Cash Outflows) × 100
```
- Ensured LCR exceeded NRB minimum requirement

#### 🔹 Net Stable Funding Ratio (NSFR):
- Computed Available Stable Funding (ASF) and Required Stable Funding (RSF)
```
NSFR = (ASF / RSF) × 100
```
- Ensured NSFR met NRB threshold requirements

- Prepared **Liquidity Gap Analysis Reports** — maturity bucket-wise analysis of assets vs. liabilities to identify structural liquidity mismatches

---

### ✅ Step 5: Asset Quality Reporting — Loan Classification & Provisioning Returns

- Prepared and submitted **asset quality returns** to NRB covering:

#### 🔹 Loan Classification Report:
- Classified entire loan portfolio into NRB prescribed categories:
  - Pass / Performing
  - Watch List
  - Substandard
  - Doubtful
  - Loss / Bad
- Applied classification criteria based on **days past due (DPD)**, collateral adequacy, and qualitative factors

#### 🔹 Provisioning Report:
- Calculated **NRB regulatory provisions** based on classification categories:

| Classification | NRB Provision Rate |
|---------------|-------------------|
| Pass | 1% |
| Watch List | 5% |
| Substandard | 25% |
| Doubtful | 50% |
| Loss | 100% |

- Compared NRB regulatory provisions with **IFRS 9 ECL provisions** and applied the **higher of** treatment as directed by NRB

#### 🔹 Sector-wise Lending Report:
- Disaggregated loan portfolio by sector — agriculture, industry, trade, services, real estate, consumer, etc.
- Monitored **sector concentration limits** as prescribed by NRB

#### 🔹 Single Obligor Limit (SOL) Report:
- Monitored individual and group borrower exposures against NRB-prescribed single obligor limits
- Identified and reported any **limit breaches** or near-breach exposures

#### 🔹 Deprived Sector Lending Report:
- Tracked compliance with NRB **priority/deprived sector lending targets**
- Computed shortfall (if any) and reported penalty implications

---

### ✅ Step 6: Interest Rate Risk Reporting

- Prepared **Interest Rate Risk reports** for NRB submission:

#### 🔹 Repricing Gap Analysis:
- Classified all rate-sensitive assets (RSA) and rate-sensitive liabilities (RSL) by repricing maturity buckets
- Computed **gap (RSA – RSL)** for each maturity bucket
- Identified maturity buckets with **significant interest rate risk exposure**

#### 🔹 Net Interest Income (NII) Sensitivity Analysis:
- Computed impact on NII for parallel interest rate shifts of ±100bps, ±200bps
- Reported NII-at-risk under each scenario

#### 🔹 Duration Analysis:
- Computed modified duration of key asset and liability portfolios
- Assessed **Economic Value of Equity (EVE)** sensitivity to interest rate changes

- Submitted interest rate risk reports within prescribed NRB deadlines
- Presented interest rate risk analysis in **ALCO meetings** for decision-making on rate-setting and balance sheet positioning

---

### ✅ Step 7: Off-Balance Sheet Exposure Reporting

- Prepared and reported **off-balance sheet exposures** to NRB:
  - **Letters of Credit (LC)** — Import/Export LCs outstanding
  - **Bank Guarantees (BG)** — Bid bonds, performance guarantees, advance payment guarantees
  - **Loan Commitments** — Undisbursed sanctioned loan facilities
  - **Derivative Exposures** — Forward contracts, swaps (if applicable)
  - **Acceptances and Endorsements**
- Calculated **Credit Equivalent Amounts** using NRB-prescribed Credit Conversion Factors (CCF) for capital adequacy computation
- Monitored off-balance sheet exposure limits and reported to Senior Management

### ✅ Step 8: SEBON Quarterly & Annual Filings

- Prepared and submitted **quarterly and annual filings** to the Securities Board of Nepal (SEBON):
  - **Quarterly Financial Statements** — unaudited IFRS/NFRS financials within SEBON prescribed timelines
  - **Annual Audited Financial Statements** — filed within regulatory deadline post-AGM
  - **Shareholding Pattern Report** — distribution of shares by category (promoter, public, institutional)
  - **Material Event Disclosures** — reporting of significant events (Board decisions, dividend declarations, changes in key management, debenture issuance etc.)
  - **Insider Trading Compliance Reports** — disclosures related to insider shareholding changes
  - **Corporate Governance Compliance Report** — as per SEBON Corporate Governance Guidelines
- Ensured all filings were submitted within **SEBON prescribed deadlines** to avoid penalties
- Maintained **SEBON filing records** for audit trail and compliance evidence

### ✅ Step 10: Central Banking System Entries & Reconciliation

- Posted financial entries directly into the **Central Banking System** for NRB reporting purposes
- Activities included:
  - **NRB account reconciliation** — reconciling the bank's balance with NRB against NRB's records
  - **CRR balance posting and monitoring** — ensuring daily CRR balance was maintained at prescribed levels
  - **Government securities settlement entries** — T-Bill and Bond purchase/maturity settlements through NRB
  - **NRB standing facility entries** — SLF/SDF/OLF utilization and settlement entries
  - **Interbank settlement entries** — clearing and settlement through NRB's payment systems
- Performed **daily reconciliation** between:
  - Bank's CBS records ↔ NRB Central Banking System records
  - NRB account statement ↔ GL balance
- Investigated and resolved **reconciliation differences** promptly — ensuring regulatory account accuracy
