## 🎯 Project Objective

Nepal mandated a transition from Nepal Rastra Bank (NRB) / local GAAP-based financial reporting to International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS/NFRS). This was a historic, country-wide accounting reform across the financial sector. I was engaged across 20+ Banks and Financial Institutions (BFIs) to support this transition — analyzing accounting gaps, restating financials, building ECL models, and training finance teams.

> ⚠️ **Note:** Client names are referenced only where publicly disclosed. All proprietary working papers, internal data, and institution-specific methodologies remain confidential.

## 🛠️ Step-by-Step: What I Did

### ✅ Step 1: Diagnostic Review & GAAP-to-IFRS Gap Analysis
- Reviewed existing financial statements prepared under local GAAP / NRB Directives
- Identified accounting policy differences between local GAAP and IFRS across:
  - Financial instrument classification
  - Loan impairment methodology
  - Employee benefit obligations
  - Revenue and interest recognition
  - Deferred taxation treatment
- Prepared a structured Gap Analysis Report mapping each local GAAP policy to its IFRS equivalent
- Prioritized gaps by materiality and complexity to guide the transition roadmap

### ✅ Step 2: Chart of Accounts (CoA) Redesign & Financial Statement Restructuring
- Redesigned the Chart of Accounts to align with IFRS-compliant financial statement presentation requirements
- Restructured the Statement of Financial Position (Balance Sheet) and Statement of Profit or Loss and Other Comprehensive Income (OCI) to include IFRS-specific line items
- Introduced Other Comprehensive Income (OCI) section — a concept absent under local GAAP — to capture:
  - Actuarial gains/losses (IAS 19)
  - Fair value movements on investments (IFRS 9)
- Mapped legacy general ledger accounts to the new IFRS CoA structure

### ✅ Step 3: Financial Instrument Classification & Measurement (IFRS 9)
- Analyzed the institution's entire financial asset and liability portfolio
- Applied the **SPPI** (Solely Payments of Principal and Interest) test and **Business Model Assessment** to classify financial assets into:
  - Amortized Cost (AC)
  - Fair Value Through Other Comprehensive Income (FVOCI)
  - Fair Value Through Profit or Loss (FVTPL)
- Reclassified investments such as Government Securities (T-Bills, Bonds), equity investments, and debentures accordingly
- Recalculated carrying values of financial assets using the Effective Interest Rate (EIR) method for amortized cost instruments

### ✅ Step 4: IFRS 9 Expected Credit Loss (ECL) Model Implementation
> This was one of the most technically demanding parts of the engagement

- Replaced the incurred loss model (NRB Directive-based provisioning) with a forward-looking Expected Credit Loss (ECL) model
- Segmented loan portfolios by:
  - **Stage 1** — Performing (12-month ECL)
  - **Stage 2** — Underperforming / Significant credit risk increase (Lifetime ECL)
  - **Stage 3** — Credit impaired (Lifetime ECL)
- Incorporated Probability of Default (PD), Loss Given Default (LGD), and Exposure at Default (EAD) parameters
- Integrated macroeconomic forward-looking factors (GDP growth, inflation, sectoral risk indicators) into ECL estimates
- Compared ECL-based provisions against NRB regulatory provisions and documented the higher of treatment per regulatory guidance
- Prepared detailed ECL workings in Excel with scenario analysis (Base, Optimistic, Pessimistic)

### ✅ Step 5: Staff Loan & Employee Benefit Adjustments (IAS 19 & IFRS 9)

**Staff Loans at Below-Market Rates:**
- Identified concessional staff loans extended at below-market interest rates
- Calculated the Day 1 fair value loss (difference between nominal loan value and present value at market rate)
- Recognized the Day 1 difference as employee benefit expense and measured the loan at amortized cost using EIR going forward

**Defined Benefit Obligations (Gratuity & Retirement Benefits):**
- Worked with actuarial reports to recognize Defined Benefit Obligations (DBO) under IAS 19
- Recorded actuarial gains/losses directly in OCI (not P&L) as required under IAS 19 revised
- Ensured proper disclosure of plan assumptions (discount rate, salary escalation, attrition rate)

### ✅ Step 6: Deferred Tax Assessment & Computation (IAS 12)
- Identified all temporary differences arising from IFRS adjustments on:
  - ECL provisions (not yet tax-deductible)
  - Fair value movements on investments
  - EIR adjustments on loans and deposits
  - Staff loan Day 1 adjustments
  - Gratuity and leave encashment provisions
- Prepared Deferred Tax Asset (DTA) and Deferred Tax Liability (DTL) computations
- Applied the balance sheet liability method as required under IAS 12
- Ensured appropriate recognition criteria for DTA (probable future taxable profit availability)

### ✅ Step 7: Opening IFRS Balance Sheet & Transition Adjustments (IFRS 1)
- Prepared the Opening Statement of Financial Position as at the IFRS transition date
- Applied IFRS 1 mandatory exceptions and optional exemptions appropriately
- Passed all IFRS transition journal entries systematically with supporting workpapers
- Ensured that equity (retained earnings and OCI reserves) was correctly restated for all transition adjustments
- Documented reconciliation between local GAAP equity and IFRS equity as required by IFRS 1 disclosure requirements

### ✅ Step 8: Regulatory Reporting Alignment
- Coordinated with the Nepal Rastra Bank (NRB) reporting framework to ensure that:
  - IFRS financials were prepared alongside NRB regulatory returns
  - Capital adequacy ratios (CAR) were computed considering IFRS-based provisions vs. NRB regulatory provisions
  - Any variance between ECL provisions and NRB-mandated provisions was addressed per NRB guidance circulars
- Ensured proper disclosure notes in financial statements for regulatory compliance purposes
- Assisted in filing and reporting to NRB, SEBON, and NEPSE as applicable

### ✅ Step 9: IFRS Training & Capacity Building
- Designed and delivered IFRS awareness and implementation training sessions to finance and operations teams of client institutions
- Provided first-time IFRS implementation training to finance staff across 20+ BFIs — a milestone in Nepal's financial sector
- **Training topics included:**
  - IFRS 9 Financial Instruments (Classification, Measurement, ECL)
  - IAS 19 Employee Benefits
  - IAS 12 Income Taxes
  - IAS 32 & IFRS 7 Financial Instruments: Presentation & Disclosures
  - IFRS 1 First-Time Adoption
- Prepared training materials, worked examples, and case studies tailored for banking professionals

### ✅ Step 10: Final Financial Statements & Disclosure Notes Preparation
- Prepared complete IFRS-compliant financial statements including:
  - Statement of Financial Position
  - Statement of Profit or Loss and OCI
  - Statement of Changes in Equity
  - Statement of Cash Flows (Indirect Method)
  - Notes to Financial Statements (full IFRS disclosures)
- Reviewed and quality-checked financial statements for IFRS compliance, internal consistency, and cross-referencing accuracy
- Coordinated with external auditors for smooth audit process, providing all required schedules, workpapers, and clarifications
